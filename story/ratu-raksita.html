Neat And Warm

Ratu Raksita Dhira
Fantasy

In Leventopia, everyone is special. They have their special tools, special skills, or even special magic. But of course, great power comes with great responsibility. When they have a special power, they also have forbidden actions depending on their characteristics. If you violate your forbidden action, you'll go into the void.

This story is about a young lady named Celia B'flies. She was gifted with the power to communicate and control butterflies. Just like a butterfly, she was gorgeous. She moved to the townsite, so she needed this person to help her.

"Excuse me, do you know where van Fyr's house is?" Celia asked a florist in the town.

"Oh, it is over there, young lady. Just go straight until the end of the road. It's near the bridge on the right side," the florist pointed at a long road, making Celia sweat already.

"Alright, thank you very much, sir. By the way, your flowers are very beautiful. Have a nice day!" Celia said with her bright smile and started to walk again.

The florist was really happy to hear Celia’s compliment that it made every flower in his store bloom perfectly.

No one said it was a long road to get to van Fyr's house.

"Oh, come on, I'm in a hurry to complete my house. Where is it?" Celia was worried because the sun had leaned to the west already. "Oh, there's the bridge!" Celia ran as fast as she could to the only house near the bridge. She was very happy that finally, she found the house.

"Excuse me." Celia knocked on the door of a simple house that she believed belonged to van Fyr.

"Yes?" A sound came from the other side of the door. The door then opened, and a man came out with a casual look. He just woke up, that was what Celia thought.

"Hi! My name is Celia B'flie, and I'm looking for van Fyr's house. Is this the right address?" Celia asked with her smile. As usual, she hoped she didn't need to search over the town anymore.

"What do you want?'' His tone was quite unfriendly, it made Celia a bit uncomfortable, but she needed him.

"Well, I just moved to the townsite, and I need your help to light up my house." Celia tried to explain her intention with a worried face.

"I can't. Just ask Luciér or Mr. Lyn to light your house."

Celia couldn't believe it. After all her efforts to find van Fyr's house, all she got was rejection.

"Uhm, no, I can control butterflies just like Luciér controls her fireflies, and I know she doesn't like her babies trapped. And about Mr. Lyn, uhh... I can't stand Mrs. Torden's noise. So, all I have is you." Celia tried to convince that man to help her with all her reasoning.

The man then sighs, "you know the last time I tried to light someone's house, I almost burned Mr. Chopsticks' house."

Of course, this was surprising news for Celia. You could see doubt and fear in her face. But no, Celia couldn't ask someone else for this. "But I'm sure you can do it cleanly for now." Celia still tries to trust the man, "please, please, only you can help me for now." Her blinking eyes once again began begging to van Fyr.

With a heavy breath, the man then said, "okay, but don't blame me if a fire starts to burn your house."

He opened the door and let Celia go into his house. Of course, Celia was very happy right now.

"Thank you, Mr. van Fyr," Celia smiled brightly with a radiant face.

"Brian, just call me Brian," he said while packing his things.

Again, Celia could only smile and look at her surroundings. It was a neat minimalist house with white and orange tones dominating the room, making the room feel warm. Yet the room was quiet for a medium house. Celia believed Brian lived alone in this house. That made Celia a bit concerned.

"I'm done. Are you ready?" Brian asked her as he brought a backpack on his shoulder.

"Yes, let's go before it gets dark." Celia quickly got up, and their trip to Celia's house began.

Along the way, Celia took the lead and walked a bit fast while Brian followed right behind her. On their way through the town, Celia was greeted by several people she knew, and Brian remained silent. That made Celia curious why it seemed like Brian was not part of the town.

After getting to the quiet road, Celia starts to talk.

"Brian, can I ask you a question?" Celia looked at him and aligned their steps together.

Go ahead," but Brian looked not interested in their talk.

"Back in the town, why did no one greet you? Isn't your power pretty useful to help them?" Celia finally let out her curiosity.

"Well, they hate me. My power is indeed useful, but if only I do it right. No one will trust someone who makes a mistake." He says flatly without any expression.

"Is it also the reason why you live far away from the town?" Celia asked again. She felt bad for what happened to Brian.

"I remember you only wanted to ask a question." That answer is enough to make Celia upset.

"Oh, come on, I try to be friendly here," Celia pretended to sulk, making Brian smile slightly.

"Technically, yes, they are afraid I would burn their house, and I don't want to make them uncomfortable with me being around. So, I decided to live a bit far from town," Brian explained all Celia wanted to know.

"I'm very sorry to hear that. It must be hard for you."

You could see how concerned Celia was after she knew things about Brian's life.

"Not really. I'm getting used to it."

Okay, Celia ready to take her words back. This man, what can I say about him? Ignorant, yes, that might be the right word for him. An ignorant man that could make Celia pretty upset that time.

"Sometimes, I think I shouldn't have worried about someone ignorant," Celia sighed, that brought Brian's tiny laugh.

"Yeah, you shouldn't. Someone worries too much about little things. Others might think it's unnecessary."

Again, Celia doesn't understand how Brian's head works.

"Do you know that I talked about you?" asked Celia.

"Yes, and I talked about you."

Celia might have become mad, so she quickly walked away and left Brian with his smiles. He likes to tease her.

Their trip had come to an end. Celia and Brian were about to arrive at Celia's house after passing the pond. But it was already sunset, and what Celia had been worried about had come true.

"Hey there, sweetie, what do you have here?" A low voice comes from behind the trees.

"We have nothing, so please leave us alone," Celia answered with her voice full of fear. Brian saw their surroundings and looked for any possibilities an attack would come.

"Brian, we need to run," Celia whispers to Brian after a butterfly comes to her, "my kids said it's Grenouille, one of the thugs. I heard that he would take anything from anyone he saw," Celia explained with her trembling voice still at low volume. Brian was a bit worried to hear about that. He was worried that it would be dangerous for Celia.

"Alright, you go first, and I'll watch your back," even though he was ignorant, Brian was a gentleman who would protect women.

"Okay, on the count of three. One… two… thre-'' before Celia finished her count, a lasso rope wrapped around her body, and a man came out from the trees.

"Well, well, look what we got here. A fine young lady. Of course, you don't mind having fun with a cool guy like me, right?" Celia was right. It was Grenouille.

Grenouille always catches his prey with his special lasso rope, spiriat. It was a special tool he had which could grip anything because of its adhesive power.

"Brian, run!" Celia shouted in pain because Grenouille's spiriat started crushing her body.

"Oh, you have a guy over there," shouted Grenouille, "hey dude! You better listen to her words and run if you don't want to get hurt. So I can have a good time with this girl." That's disgusting for Celia, but she couldn't do anything.

"No, I won't," said Brian cleary.

He started to light up and burn everything in front of him. Everything, except Celia.

"IT'S HOT!" Grenouille screamed and released his spiriat from Celia, then ran away.

"You okay?" Brian ran to Celia with his worried face and helped her to stand.

"I'm totally fine. See, I told you that you have better control of your flame now," stated Celia while fixing her appearance.

"No, that's a coincidence," said Brian hesitantly.

You could see it because his head was down while talking to Celia.

"Coincidence? You saved my life. How was that supposed to be a coincidence? Come on, be more confident, Mr. van Fyr!" Celia patted Brian's shoulder and continued to walk. All Brian could do was smile, and as Celia walked in front of him, he said, "whatever you say, miss," and started to follow her.

Finally, they arrived at Celia's house, and Brian started to do his task. Now he did it clean and neat, just like Celia said. Celia's house became perfect.

"Oh my gosh, my house feels very comfy, as I imagine. It's all thanks to you, Brian."

Celia still went around her new house while Brian only sat on the couch in the living room. Yes, he had done a very good job this time.

"Oh, if you don't mind, could you stay for dinner. Just a little present for helping me," and Brian accepted Celia's offer. Of course, he was hungry after a long trip, completed his things, and saved Celia. He needed some food.

The dinner was delicious. Brian had never thought Celia would cook this well. And there Brian stood in front of the window, still having that thought about the food.

"So, how was the food? Was that good?" Celia came from the kitchen after cleaning some stuff.

"Not bad, at least it is edible," here we go again, Brian always teasing Celia.

"Say that again, and you will see my hand on your face."

Brian laughed with a big voice.

"Anyway, your butterfly here is beautiful."

Brian pointed at a butterfly glass decoration with colorful wings.

"Granny Glaze made it for me before I moved. Here, I'll give it to you as a reminiscence."

Celia took the butterfly and gave it to Brian. It was only palm-sized but a bit heavy because it was glass-made.

"You don't have to, but thank you."

Then it became silent for a moment. They were busy with their thoughts.

"Hey Brian," said Celia, suddenly with an unusual tone. It was more serious. "Do you know that butterflies don't live a long life?" a random question came from Celia. Brian didn't have any intention to answer. He only listened to her. "So I need you to do another favor for me," said Celia with no smile and cheerful tone. Only Celia with a gloomy face.

"You always ask more than you say, don't you?" Brian tried to light up the mood, but it didn't work.

Celia then faced Brian and stepped closer to him. It feels strange when something wraps Brian's body, but it also feels warm and mild.

Celia hugs Brian.

"I'm not supposed to do this, but it feels right," Celia tightened her hug.

"What do you mean?" Brian was confused. Everything was so sudden, and his little head was unable to process all of these.

"It is the end of me," after Celia said that, Brian was shocked. He knew exactly what she meant. It's Celia's forbidden action.

"But why?" was the only question in Brian's mind.

"Just like your power, you're such a warm person yet very lonely because no one would come close to fire. I know you deserve better than what you get now. So at least let me hug you because it can make everything better," explained Celia.

It is a big decision to sacrifice yourself to make someone feel better. Celia chose that path. Her soft heart couldn't leave Brian as he was. After meeting him, she knew Brian needed little attention and appreciation.

"Well, take care of my butterfly… and let's meet at another chance."

Celia released her hug and smiled as sweetly as she could. On the other side, Brian stared blankly at the space in front of him.

Brian looked at the glass butterfly Celia gave to him. Looking back at how Celia treated him differently from everyone made him overwhelmed with what just happened.

"I don't know if I should be sad or grateful," a tear starts flowing through Brian's cheek. "If you don't do this… it will be the end of me," says the fire who shouldn't fall for anyone.
