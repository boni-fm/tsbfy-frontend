Water in A Glass Mug

When I was younger, I always wondered why my mother always asked me to bring a glass of water in a glass mug for my father every time he got back after work.

She would say something like, “Lana, bring Dad a glass of water,” at first.

I froze for a moment and went to look at my father, who was leaning against our sofa. He was wiping his sweat with two sheets of tissue while smiling at me. So bright. But nothing that I felt except a strange feeling and a hesitation to smile back. Eventually, I grew a little stiff smile before I rushed to the kitchen to bring a glass of water for him, as my mother ordered.

I came back with a glass of water. It was so full that the water inside almost spilled—wonder why I did this. I walked carefully towards him. I paid full attention to the glass of water in my hands'. None other than because I was afraid Dad would scold me if I spilled it.

Even though he would never.

“Dad, water,” no words I could say except those two words. I put that plastic mug on the table in front of my dad.

Dad smiled and lifted up the mug. He sipped the water. “Thank you,” he said while smiling at me. I couldn’t help but smile back and retorted with a small yes.

“How’s your day?”

The first question my dad always asked me every time we met for the first time after months. Yeah, he chose the usual starting of father-daughter conversation. A daughter will probably be the happiest when her dad asks her that kind of question and begins to share every single thing they do in a day, but I didn’t find that daughter in me. I was speechless. I could’ve told him a story where boys in my class called me an ugly rhinoceros, where I got an A+ for an English test, how I got a good mark for the first time for Math test, or how I went to school alone by bus because Mom told me to.

“It’s good,”

But I couldn’t.

“Lana! Why did you use a plastic mug?”

I was startled when I heard Mom’s exclamation from behind my back. She brought a tiny plate with some pieces of cake that she made to celebrate Dad’s homecoming.

“Why?” I questioned innocently.

Mom took her seat first and put the plate on the table adjacent to the mug. She looked at me with both hands on her wrists. She didn’t say anything, just looked at me. I blinked twice, and almost laughed because Mom’s facial expression was so funny!

“Lana!” Mom exclaimed one more time. At that exact moment, I realized something. I should bring Dad a glass of water with his glass mug!

***

My father was a head chef on a fancy cruise ship. Mom said its name was In Deep Ship, but I didn’t want to know. He worked not for a day, not for a week, but months. He rarely went home. That was why my relationship with Dad almost as awkward as strangers, and why I always wondered why Mom always asked me to bring a glass of water for him in a glass mug that she claimed to be his favorite mug every time he got back from his work, which was just one month among the twelve months of a year.

Like, aren’t all mugs at home just as good? That was my thought before.

I quite didn’t like it when Dad was home. He was a strict person. He talked a lot. Once he talked, you could never throw even a single word to interrupt unless he let you. Even though he talked a lot, it doesn’t mean that he talked about unnecessary things. Well, although I used to think that he talked about such things. But, I realized now that Dad only talked about something important. He was always straight to the point. In conclusion, he talked about many important things.

“Lana,” that is my name. He called me.

I was startled that my TV remote was thrown out of my hand. At the same time, our eyes met. Dad was looking sharp, while mine looked at him in full shock.

“You haven’t slept yet?” he asked.

For a second, I went silent, and then I went back to reality. I could feel my heart beating so fast between shock and fear when I saw him folding his arms and leaning against the door of my bedroom.

“D-Dad …,” I mumbled with a stutter, “I … was about to turn the TV off when you came in.” I let out an awkward giggle after that.

Dad’s rule that I didn’t like the most was no TV or electronic activities above 11 PM. It was above 11 PM.

He let out a sigh, “Okay, turn off the TV now, Sweetie,” he said calmly.

I went silent again. Dad’s voice was low, and he never screamed. Unlike Mom. Mom always screamed whenever I got on her nerves— and it was understandable— but Dad was different. Never have in my fourteen years of life I found him screaming at my mom, let alone at me. His voice was so low, yet I knew that every time that voice was out, things could not be denied.

I nodded, turned the TV off and threw myself to the bed. Dad put a little smile on his lips while he was nodding. He then inched out of my bedroom. Before closing the room door, he mumbled, “good night, Lana.”

I just nodded and said the same thing back to him. Then, he slowly closed the door, as I fell asleep.

 I thought about nights like that. I was dreaming when there was a very gentle caress on my hair, not once, but twice or thrice, also a little kiss on the forehead.

The next day, I always found out that Dad was not here. Mom said that he must go back to work, and he did. I didn’t give a certain reaction, but deep inside, I felt disappointed. It was always like that. A question in my mind always lingered and left unanswered because I always missed the chance to ask him the question.

“Dad, about last night, did a gentle caress on my hair and a little kiss on my forehead come from you? Or was I dreaming?”

That question still lingers. It has been answered now, but with two different answers.

One day, at the age of fourteen, I came home from school. Just like the other kids at my age, I was happy simply because I finally got back home after spending seven hours at school. I did not lie when I said studying is tiring.

“Mom, I’m home!” I screamed excitedly. Just a few steps away until I reach my bed!

Unfortunately, my steps stopped right when I saw Mom’s up and down shoulders along with her rare sobs.

“Mom?”

 Excitement had gone away, and in a blink of eyes, it shifted to curiosity and perplexity. I stepped slowly towards her as she didn’t react a bit to my voice that called her. I put my hand on her shoulder. It was shaking badly.

I had never seen her crying this bad.

“What’s happening, Mom?” I decided to ask.

After minutes of refusing to look at my face, she lifted her face from her deep stoop. My heart ached when I saw Mom’s face wet. I could not tell the difference between her tears and her sweat. She looked very sad and exhausted at the same time.

“M-Mom, is everything alright?” I didn’t know why I started to get so scared when her eyes met mine.

She shook her head. I froze. Everything is not alright.

“Mom?”

“Dad…”

“D-Dad?” I repeated, “why? Does it have anything to do with him? Does he let you down, Mom?”

I swear if Dad will ever do that to Mom…
