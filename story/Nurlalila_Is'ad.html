Romance

Life in the city has always been such an exciting event for Louis. Growing up in the rural area of the country, there wasn't much the place could offer him. Everywhere he looked, he was met with the vast empty space of the hills. If he was lucky enough, he’d catch a glimpse of other’s cows and sheep munching on the grass, of little kids playing tag before disappearing from his sight.

Louis was six years old the first time he heard of life outside of his village. Mr. Collins, the owner of the biggest farm in the village, just came back from some business in the city. He had been gone for longer than three months—longer than anyone had ever been—and everyone was so excited to hear whatever stories he brought back from the city. It was one strange concept that little Louis couldn’t help but be weirded out by the thought of it. He couldn’t imagine not living in his amazing and exciting village. I get to feed the cows, wash them, milk them, and shear wools from the sheep. Why would I need to travel outside? he thought.

But as years went by and more and more people visited the city, his curiosity was piqued. One time, when he was no older than ten, his uncle offered to take him there. He was extremely reluctant; he didn’t want to leave his mother alone to tend the livestock. But his mother insisted that she would be fine with the help from the neighbors. So, with heavy-heart, he took off with his uncle. What Louis didn’t expect was how life-changing that one trip would be for him.

Tall houses surrounded him from every way. “How come the houses are taller than the trees? I can’t even stack my bricks to match the height of the trees in the backyard!” said Louis. “And why does every house shine so brightly?”

“Well, don’t that make this place so much more interesting?” Laughed his uncle. Louis really thought he was going to be blind that day.

Since then, he dreamed of packing and leaving in search of a more exhilarating life. After saving up for years from helping on his family farm, he finally felt ready to leave.

“Promise me you’ll visit your old mother here?” His mother joked to him.

“Mother, please. You’re the youngest mother with an adult kid here, you’re not old at all.” Louis reciprocated with a chuckle.

“I know, I know.” Jolene pulled him into her embrace, “Now go, Mr. Collins’ already outside. It’s not kind to let other people wait on us.”

“Yes, Mother,” Louis said while being ushered outside, “I’ll see you soon, yeah? Take care. Love you.”

“You too, hun.” Jolene kissed his forehead before letting go.

Louis hopped onto Mr. Collins’ ride. He could hear chatter from where his mother was standing, but Mr. Collins already took off.

Louis has been living in the city for more than half a decade now and he’s been back home for a total of zero time. It’s not like he doesn’t want to, but life in the city is hard. The first year he was here, he planned to visit his mother, but he was working at such a strict job. He couldn’t leave or risk messing up all the work he and his co-workers had done. The next few years, after he landed a new job, he couldn’t go back home because new and old clients kept on coming to see him.

To work using his brain turns out to be far more draining than any other physical work he has ever done in his entire life, and now that he thinks about it, maybe that first job was not strict at all and he just so happened to have an awful boss.

Louis was determined, though. He had decided that this year, no matter what, he had to be visiting his hometown and showering his mother with his love and embrace.

That’s how he finds himself driving his car down a similar road, one he’s taken numerous times in his youth. His car comes to a halt right before entering the village. He takes in the sight; the same place where memories of his childhood were made. Not much has changed since he left, he noticed; other than that, a new and shiny big building in the middle. Whether that’s a good thing or not he doesn’t know.

Louis was about to continue his drive when a thump coming from the direction of a tree a few feet from his car caught his attention. He watched a girl, not much younger than him, dusting off her pants in confusion. “Uh, where did you…” he trailed off.

“Oh, I was just up there on the tree.”

Louis can’t help but be surprised by her casualty in answering that. Living in the city for so long has made him forget how leisure life is here. “So, are you from here or are you just visiting?”

The girl in question looks at him in silence for a second, blinking before responding, “You don’t remember me?”

“Sorry, should I?” Louis grimaced.

“I’m Ellie, El for short.” She offers her hand for Louis to shake. “Does that ring a bell?”

Louis seems to be thinking about it, recalling every childhood friend of his and trying to match their faces with the pretty girl standing in front of him now.

“You hitched a ride with my father when you left for the city, Louis.”

Louis can feel his eyes widen on its own at that. “No way! You’re Mr. Collins’ youngest?!”

“Yup,” Ellie confirmed, popping the p at the end. “It’s the snot, isn’t it?”

“Yeah, ‘t was all over you then,” he chuckles, “But seriously, you’ve changed so much since the last time I saw you. I wouldn’t have recognized you otherwise.” Louis thinks he sees Ellie lowering her head at his response, but he isn’t one hundred percent sure of it.

“So, would you like to hitch a ride back home with me?”

“Oh my, Louis!” Jolene exclaimed at the entrance of their home, pulling Louis into her warm and tight embrace, “I’ve missed you so much! How have you been, love?”

“Good, been missing home so much, though, these days. I’m sorry I didn’t visit sooner.”

“It’s alright, love. I know you’ve got tons of things to do there.”

“Yeah, but that does not justify me not visiting you for five freaking years, Mother.”

Jolene pulled back, cupping his cheeks in her hands, “Hey, you’re here now. That’s what’s more important, alright? How long are you gonna stay?”

“Not for long, I’m ‘fraid. About two weeks at most.”

Jolene looks at his apologetic face before smiling, “That’s more than enough time for me to pamper you like old times.”

<<<>>>

Louis would be lying if he says he isn’t having the time of his life right now. He’s missed having someone who cares enough to clean after himself. He thinks maybe if he could, he would love to move back permanently and abandon his life and responsibility in the city. But life does not work that way, and Louis finds himself inside his car ready to head back to the city.

“Try to visit more often in the future, yeah?” his mother reminds him. “It does get lonely sometimes even with the mothers making themselves at home.”

Louis nods and leans out the car window to hug his mother once more before he drives away. He stops the car at the entrance of the village to take in the sight for one last time when a thud surprises him again. He sees Ellie kneeling on the ground, wincing her face as she tries to stand up.

“El, are you okay?” he rushes to her side, helping her to her feet. “You really need to stop scaring people like that.”

“Yeah, I’m fine. Thanks, Lou.” She smiles at him. “Are you leaving already?”

Louis nods, sighing at the reminder of his departure. “I preferred to stay longer if I could but alas.”

“Me too,” Ellie murmurs under her breath.

His head snaps so fast at that, “What?”

“Bye, Louis. I’ll see you when you get back next time.” With that, Ellie leaves him there, frozen where he stands staring at the back of her head. Perhaps he heard that wrong.

        Louis is getting better at visiting his hometown for the next couple of years. He never went more than six months without going at least once, and in every trip, Ellie never missed a chance to greet him at the same spot every time. They developed a rather unique friendship from the events. Or so Louis thinks.

Jolene didn’t think much about it at first, but with each time Louis came back with Ellie occupying his passenger seat, she grew more and more suspicious of him. She used to always give him side-glances and raised eyebrows. They are having dinner when she decides to confront him about it. "So, what's going on with you and Ellie? You've been coming back with her for how many times now?"

"Nothing's going on with El, Mother." Louis swallows the food in his mouth. "I just happen to meet her on the outskirts every time. Why? Is there anything wrong?"

Jolene blinks at him before her face contorts with disbelief. "You are way too oblivious for your own good." She shakes her head.

"She's waiting for you, Louis."

And so, when the time for Louis to head back to the city comes, he is determined to find out whether his mother is right about it. He leaves early in the morning in hope to reach his destination first. How right he was when he checks the tree and finds no feet dangling from above. He waits for a few hours before Ellie shows up at their rendezvous.

“I’m guessing Jolene slapped some sense into you,” she says as a matter of fact, holding back a smile.

Louis shakes his head yes and they both fall silent. “I—I have to get back now. Got an early appointment tomorrow. Have to prepare and everything,” Louis states, breaking the quiet atmosphere surrounding them.

“Oh. Yeah, of course,” Ellie says before adding, “Drive safely, Louis.”

Louis can’t help but notice the slight change in her tone. He decides there and then that he doesn’t like hearing it coming from her. “But I’ll see you when I come again, yeah? Here, on the outskirts of this village?”

A grin slowly appears on Ellie’s face, and yeah, Louis likes that much better.
