The Quintessential Art of Serenity

Rayhan Fauzan
Slice of Life

        The streets are jam-packed, along with the low lighting reflected in the bonnets, which encapsulate the transition of day to dusk, marked by the beautiful sunset. It is the usual mark of time where people return to their home or even just fancy themselves to pop up into the nearest restaurant for a dinner after a long splendorous day in their activities.

From here on, we accompany Raymond, a 32-year-old faithful husband, who is responsible as a President Director of an automotive company in Jakarta. As the traffic packed up nicely in Jakarta’s twilight street, Raymond sat comfortably with his low seating setting, awakened by the external noise, yet calm and composed with his peaceful mind as he turned on his favourite singer, and probably Raymond’s all-time inspiration, Taylor Swift.

        As the pack of traffic lights ahead switched itself from turning red, he took a slight chance to reminisce himself about how great life turned out to be. He used to think a lot about how things will pan out in the present, as people will often remind him not to rush into things, followed by their Cheshire cat smile. “Nothing lasts forever”, as he smirked on his short trip to the memory lane. In hindsight, people do come and go, and only a few of them will be committed to staying in our lane. He knew that he had to go back home, to find his own wonderland, his peaceful mind. As the green lights are on, Raymond snaps himself to focus on driving home, and returning to his lovely wife where he belongs, and his beautiful daughter.

“Darling, I’m home!” Raymond puts off the car key and his shoes.

“Daddy, come here!” Betty eagerly welcomed her dad’s presence and hugged him with comfort.

“Daddy, I met this new transferred friend in class, his name is James, he’s so friendly to me. I really wanted to be friends with him forever!” 

Raymond then listened to his young daughter’s friend’s discovery with an affectionate smile, as they sat together in their cozy family room. Betty and Raymond were soon accompanied by Dorothea, a hard worker, a beauty on its own, and a wonderful wife.

“Darling, have you eaten outside?” asks Dorothea with curiosity.

“No dear love, I have not”, Raymond responded with his cheek turning red every time he saw Dorothea as if they had just met for the first time.

“Tell me, how was your day, love?” asks Raymond affectionately to Dorothea.

“It went smooth as usual darling, I made up some really good progress in the office, and I managed to prepare dinner for you and Betty today!” tells Dorothea with her high spirits as the maid of the family took a day’s rest.

Raymond then responded with some good acknowledgments for Dorothea, “Marvellous, love! I’m so happy to hear everything’s alright and I’m so proud of you! Oh, how I love you dearly, Dorothea!” as she blushes away with Betty looking up to them.

        Raymond then leaves to take a bath and prepare dinner with the family. As a small, happy, intelligent family in the neighbourhood, a brief catch-up always left the mark of meaningful values on family members every time they step into the doorway are just as important to their livelihood, their connection between each other, and most importantly, their serenity. It is where each member of the family feels at ease and finds serenity whenever they are faced with great difficulties, as it fits to be called “home”.

Raymond soon is going to have dinner with Dorothea as Betty accompanies them to the dining room. Raymond and Dorothea’s matrimony and relationship were a bit touch and go in the first place. Just like the untied rope, a relationship is probably the very last thing they could ever think of. Raymond and Dorothea met in their college days, but there was a slight misunderstanding when Dorothea thought that Raymond still had feelings for Augustine, his ex-girlfriend. Thereafter the moment Raymond cleared things up with Dorothea, they made a memorable journey along the way, despite their love blooming in the very last semester of college. Sure, they had some differences and different principles, but as we often knew in tying the rope, it is the differences that made them significant and they patched up until their college days over as the work phase starts to arrive, up until the very moment where they decided to break the status quo and took a step forward in the holy matrimony and raises a lovely family together.

        As they finished dinner and dessert, Betty asked both her parents, with her eagerly asking the one-million-dollar question. “Dad, how was your first-time experience meeting with mummy?” Betty looks innocently.

Raymond then chuckles for a while and takes his time, thanking Dorothea for a lovely dinner and Dorothea smiles upon Betty’s innocent question as she is brought back to the memory lane.

“I think we have our unique quirks in the past. It was good, bad, joy, and tears, all mixed up nicely as we were getting along for quite some years from college and decided to be together in the end. It was nevertheless an enthralling journey, and it was worth the experience, Betty!” as Raymond recalled the all’s well that ends well journey with Dorothea. Betty then leaves the dining room with a small kiss from her parents, along with her little smile and acknowledgement of her parent’s trip down memory lane. “Good night mum, dad”.

        Months have gone by, and Betty is going to have her graduation ceremony. With all preparations going further, Betty asked James if he is going to the same senior high school as Betty intended to. But as Betty figured, James did not go to the same local school as Betty would have hoped so. Instead, he is seeking to study abroad and come along with his parent’s business in New Zealand. Betty was utterly devastated. She turns blue and the everlasting rain poured into Betty’s gold heart for months.

        She would never expect that the friend of her life is going somewhere without her. A crushed soul, filled with angst sorrow thoughts was soon accompanied Betty, wrapped up beneath her own mind. As a senior high schooler now, Betty took a lot of time within herself and decided not to take any interest in a relationship, even with a good-looking boy in her senior high school. Betty is somewhat looking grim and initially did not accept things the way it is. As she walks by the doorway, her dad is in the garden, tending the flowers with care and so-forth. Aware of her arrival from school, Raymond greeted Betty.

“Hello sweetie, how was your school today?” Raymond looked upon Betty’s frown.

“It was fine dad. Nothing really happened.” Betty sighed and hoped that she would get to her bedroom quickly.

“No sweetie, you don't seem to look fine at all. Come here, let me talk with you” Raymond then took a seat in the patio garden while Betty looked hesitant to join with her dad.

“What’s happening, sweetie? You know you can talk and trust dad, don’t you?” Raymond put down Betty’s bag with ease.

“Ever since James left, I really never got the closure for being close with my friend anymore, let alone a good friend in high school. He was such a good friend to me, he took interest in me, even invested himself in time that I will never trade for.

And now with his absence, there is nothing compared to him. I really wanted to go to New Zealand dad, I really do!” Betty is on the edge of sobbing, with Raymond slowly but steadily taking Betty’s arm to hold his lovely daughter.

        “There you go, my sweetie. You see sweetie, life is nothing short but filled with temporary people. The friends you have made along the way may never find their course on the same path as yours. Betty sweetie, I do believe that you can get your grip together, I believe it is okay for you now to experience your sorrow and all.

        Just don’t forget to rebound and find a good new friend in high school. Sure, they won’t have the same experience as you were having with James, but life is too short to live with angst and the pain of losing someone, isn’t it?” speaks Raymond as he is comforting Betty’s loss.

        “I think you’re right. Thank you, dad! It really means a lot to me. I particularly haven’t found myself within good rhythm with my high school friends, but I really wanted to ask you about something dad, how do you cope with your loss and grievances after all this time? Is there something I could learn from you or anything, dad?” Betty sighed with a slight relief at her dad’s comfort. Raymond then took a slight pause and thought of what he could give his insights to his young high schooler teenager Betty in her loss phase.

        “You see, it is hard to believe that grieving and the sense of building a detachment to someone or something, somehow find their way to run hand in hand. I know that we’re often seeing new people, but eventually, their novelty will soon turn into ashes and dust. Frankly, sweetie, I will never be sure how I’ve coped with loss and grievances all my life, but I have just got the inner belief and a sense of detachment to what was going on with my life at the time. As you may wake up in the middle of the night, you may feel the time is moving, but eventually it will be okay.

        Sure, it felt like the pain is evermore, but you have got to fight within yourself too. There is no better way to overcome your problems than believing in yourself to recover, even when you have reached a dire situation. When you experience hell within yourself, try to seek help within our family as well, I’m sure in moments like this, both me and mum might have help to listen to you, and give a bit of small advice if you need it.

        In short, you’ve got to keep yourself resilience for the battle that is worth fighting for, up until to the point where you have reached your serenity and says, “I’ve come a long way and I’m proud of myself!” says Raymond with high spirits and motivates Betty without undermining her conundrum.

“What is detachment itself, dad? Does it have any relation with our serenity?” asks Betty curiously.

“Detachment is where you practice a mindful set of inner beliefs without making excuses and mind our own business. When things are going well for us, we feel strong and positive, right? When we’re dealing with loss, grievances, and failure, being detached, and aiming for our serenity looks much more appealing than it ever before. It is a baseline of a lifeline, where we can move out of acute suffering into something close to our serenity.

        There are lots of ways we could figure out in detachment. Desire, fear, and hopelessness in Betty’s mind could soon liberate Betty into your own serenity, if you got the inner power you needed for and take your belief into the art of detachment as something positive to liberate yourself. So yes, detachment actually has a relation with our peace of mind. It’s just hard to do it at first, but once you get the grip of yourself, you can control your willpower and achieve your serenity at last!” as Raymond tapped her daughter’s head for comfort. 

        “Ah, I see. Maybe now I will learn how to keep myself clear of attachment and achieve my serenity. You know dad, I think life will always be like this in my future, isn’t it? There will be a lot of James out there when I think I could cling to him forever but eventually, forever is the sweetest con. I really wished that despite my vulnerability, I don’t really want someone to use my vulnerability as a way to befriend me for a while.

        I don’t want to waste my time evermore and think about something I cannot return, be it with the memories or the person I used to seek the most. I felt that what has happened between myself and James was beautiful and ethereal, we were almost like a sibling, a partner in crime, and maybe um… a couple.

        But I think now I’ve got your point dad. Life would be so boring and all without us not experiencing this kind of thing in my younger days and learning the trick of achieving our serenity, especially when it comes to relationships and our life overall. Thank you for listening and giving me insightful experiences to me dad, I love you!”.

        Betty soon leaves her father and kisses her dad’s cheek with a slight buoyant feeling on her face. It may seem like a normal open conversation between the dad and daughter relationship, but both Betty and Raymond are building a healthy and mutual understanding between the relationship, boundaries, and the sense of detachment which prioritizes serenity as a goal itself. For sure, when you are young, lots of people assume you know nothing.

        Betty learned this the hard way for her, especially given how young she is, but it is best to put into account that Betty is slowly learning the quintessential art of serenity, that she is accepting things just the way it is. She rarely complains about petty stuff, barely forces any relationship coming her way, what comes may on her friendship is the way it is, she gathered herself with confidence to meet and introduce herself to new friends, and she excels in her senior high school period.

        As Raymond soon collectively observed his young daughter’s positive transformation, filled with happiness and serenity, he asked Dorothea on a small occasion to celebrate Betty’s uplifted mood and her accomplishments.

“Hey Dorothea, don’t you think we should throw a small celebration for Betty? she accomplished so many things this year” Raymond whispered.

“You know what, that would be splendid! Let’s spend the night in a Signature Restaurant, I’ve heard that the place is buzzing with its buffet delicacy! Dorothea welcomed the idea extravagantly.

        As Raymond acknowledged Dorothea’s positive response, he then booked a reservation for the family in the Signature Restaurant, Kempinski Jakarta. As the sunset glazed the metropolitan area, Raymond’s family arrived at the restaurant.

“Isn’t it lovely, to spend some family time together with the three of us here” Raymond was jubilant with the idea and Betty’s upbeat transformation.

“Come to think of it, I think it is great for us to have this outing, a small celebration of what has been going on in our family, especially to Betty, my little daughter!” Dorothea continues as she passes the drink to Raymond and Betty.

        “Thanks, mum, dad. I really appreciate this dinner. I may have been a total wretch a couple of months before, but thanks to both of your efforts and care, I’ve managed to overcome the situation, and maybe, I’ve reached my serenity after all!”

“Well, now we’ve got our little superstar here, why don’t we raise a toast to a happier family in the future? Here’s to another peacefulness in our family!” Raymond, Dorothea, and Betty raised the glass jubilantly.

        Through the loss of someone so dearly within Betty’s arms, she managed to rebound and managed to find herself in a well-respectable college. She met with a couple of James’s personas in her past, but she has found her own serenity. As for Raymond and Dorothea, they managed to smooch their career ladder up as a respectable Director and Manager.
