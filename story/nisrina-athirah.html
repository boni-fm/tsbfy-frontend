Kara

Nisrina Athirah Yamin
Slice of Life

It had been a long time since I went outside on my own. Lately, my life had been exhausting since everything around me is mostly unpleasant, from the most trivial things to crucial matters that could be compared to obligations. I took a stroll without thinking of a particular destination, just walking whenever my mind wanted to go. One of the streets I passed was strange yet familiar at the same time. Probably the last time I saw this street was long ago when I could not exactly pinpoint the exact time. Buildings were still placed as they used to though some buildings changed their use. As I remembered, the yellow-painted small shop located in front of the park was someone’s house back then. The park still retains its original look. The seesaw looked strong like it’s newly bought. The swing appeared colorfully vibrant, and, last but not least, the slide showed no paint chips or rust found. I was wondering how the park could be extremely retained despite its age. That person who had been looking after the park for many years did an extremely amazing job.

After looking around the park, I almost mindlessly walked to another destination I did not think of. A cafe with a beige-painted front view with a letter-shaped light caught my eye because I felt thirsty after taking a long walk from the bus stop. Also, I needed to sit and view pleasant-looking interiors to enhance my restful stop. By glancing at the menu board near the entrance door, it seemed that this cafe serves tasty desserts and beverages. It was true because, as soon as I arrive, the seats are mostly occupied by people. Goodness, I finally found an unoccupied seat located near the window, which was my favorite spot if I went on my own because, whenever I went to a cafe, I could look outside whenever I got bored.

 “One piece of red velvet cake and one medium-sized bubble tea”

The person who served me looked familiar, and I thought she was one of my elementary school friends. Some of my elementary school friends had similar facial features back then. Two of them were identical twins, and I had trouble telling them apart except for their names, Bulan and Bintang. They were rather calm. The other pair of my friends who looked uncannily similar often act like Tom and Jerry as if they were actual siblings. Coincidentally, their first names were Jasmine. Everyone called both Jasmines by their middle name. I suddenly remembered that one boy who always pranked the whole class, Eugene, was dared to read a silly love poem in front of the class after he made one of my friends cry, which caused him. That girl became a so-called couple in the class too. Despite remembering what happened at that time, I could not exactly pinpoint the name of that girl until I saw her name tag, which says Kara. Ah, Kara was that girl who often cried in class, and she once got ‘rescued’ by a silly love poem. She got along with me very well, and she gave me a letter before the day she transferred to another school. The reason why she moved remains unknown until that day, she didn’t even give a clue in her handwritten letter. It had been known that the world was surely a strange place. People could encounter old friends or other people’s friends in unexpected places, just like my recent encounter with Kara.

I smiled at Kara after she finished serving me, and she smiled back. From her facial expressions, it looked like she recognized me as her childhood friend. However, she might smile as a part of her job to greet customers warmly. From what I knew, she had been a welcoming and friendly person since the day we became friends when we were in elementary school. She got along with all of my classmates. She once suggested the whole class play hide-and-seek together, and everyone agreed. When Benny, the seeker, counted up to fifty, Kara and I hid together inside the treehouse on the playground. It was not clever to hide there since the treehouse had an open window that we forgot to close. We enjoyed looking at the surroundings until we forgot about the hide-and-seek game we were playing. Luckily, we were not chosen as the seeker as someone else was caught earlier than us. As I stayed inside the other hiding spot, I met Kara and her friends, Dee and Via.

“Oh, it’s her again.”

Inside the hiding spot, we played lots of games, such as rock-scissors-paper, truth or dare, and more games that I could not remember because we changed the game a few minutes after we started apart from the two games I mentioned. Unlike the previous one, we forgot about the hide-and-seek game, but we did not get caught. We spent our time playing truth or dare for hours.

"Let's spin the bottle again. Who turns next?"

It was Kara's turn to did what their friends chose, truth or dare. Dee, Via, and I agreed collectively to assign Kara to tell any truth that she hasn't told everyone yet about herself.

"Come on, tell us a secret. We promise we won't spill it to everyone else. Every truth told in this game stays here."

I was trying to convince Kara to convey her thoughts. Every person who has known Kara said she is very amiable. She rarely told secrets, even to her closest friends and family. She played joyfully around us, as if we lived in a happy, safe place. On this occasion, Kara looked like she wanted to announce something important.

“Guys, perhaps this month is going to be my last month in this school. I am going to move to another school next month because of my dad’s job. Do you guys remember when I was that new kid at school in second grade? I was scared of not being able to make friends because that happened in my old school. I stayed for only a year and a half there. I hoped I could stay longer here, but it’s almost the same. Now, we’re at the beginning of fourth grade. It’s been a happy time spending with you guys for two years, especially around you three. I will announce it to the whole class next week, at the beginning of August. Also, I would like to give a farewell letter so we can stay in touch.”

We all put our attention to Kara’s message. Everyone is feeling sad since it was almost Kara’s farewell. An hour passed, and no one found them in their hiding place. Probably they forgot there were four players left still hiding somewhere.

Fast forward to the beginning of August, she announced her message before she moved to another school on the next day. I was curious as to what was the true reason behind her moving to another school beside her father’s job. My gut said there must be another reason why she moved out all of a sudden. Usually, people transfer schools in the second semester, at least in this school. There had been three, including Kara, who transferred into another school. The first two were the twins named Bulan and Bintang. They moved to Australia because their father worked for an embassy. We still contacted each other up to that day, and we even hung out once in a while. Currently they were already back in Indonesia. For Kara, no one knew her whereabouts for sure until I graduated elementary school.

The next day, I asked Lila, one of my friends, to accompany me to the cafe I visited the day before. We chit-chatted about things, from each other’s whereabouts, hottest gossip, and more miscellaneous things we cannot name them. Lila whispered at me,

“That Kara girl looks like my neighbor.”

After that I asked about the Kara she talked about. She said that her neighbor passed away a few days after their elementary school graduation. I was extremely shocked. None of my elementary school friends knew about this, even Dee and Via. They could not keep in touch with Kara despite their efforts, but she did not answer their calls, texts, and even emails.

“I was quite close to Kara until her very end. She was that new kid in fourth grade in my school. She told me she often transferred schools every two years because of her father’s job. She hopes to stay enrolled at the same school until graduation day. She did not want to move again because she had moved schools four times throughout her young age. Unfortunately, an accident took her life away several days after graduation”.

That Kara girl who worked in a cafe wasn’t my childhood friend, it was probably a lookalike who shared her name. I was sorry for what happened after elementary graduation day. Thank you for making my elementary school days happy, although we cannot meet again here. Sometimes, childhood friends made the best memories, and Kara was one of the best ones out of all the joyful moments I experienced. Remembering childhood friends had that certain feeling I couldn’t describe in words, but the only thing I could do was hope they were doing well if completely out of touch.
