The Quest

Nasyafka Dinanti
Fantasy

There is a sense of reckless bravery within every young boyhood’s dream, as well as the banal understanding of adventure engraved deep within the bones of children. Julian Smith, a young man from a small village, is not foreign to this concept. He thrives his whole life following this reckless pipe dream like a bird learning how to fly from its nest for the first time. He was wholehearted and ready to plummet down and immerse himself in the adventures provided in the lands unknown.

However, life is not that idealistic, nor fate was kind.

— q —

The world crumbled around him on a sunny afternoon, perfect for sightseeing compared to others. The irony was a cruel and hilarious thing that comes when you least expect it.

They were supposed to do a simple side quest, something to do along the way as they took the hard trek across the continent. The party consisted of 5 adventurers. One of them was a hotheaded aristocrat as the group leader. Ravus Mason gave the group a proper briefing of the plan as they stood under the southern part of the forest region within the quest area. The scout mission —because that was the only way to describe what they were doing— required them to enter a cavern that reported sightings of an unknown monster and confirm the threat without engaging against it. Of course, that was when the plane started to unravel.

Julian, the weakest who acted as the bard and the support healer, fancied himself to be a brave man. He planned to make the journey outside the comfort of his quaint and peaceful village along with his twin sister and the party’s second warlock, Josselyn. So he immediately and foolishly decided to cancel his plan as soon as they entered the mouth of the cave, which made the group annoyed.

“What are you doing?!” Ravus asked almost aggressively as soon as he realized the bard was moving deeper into the dark cave. Not like what they strategized, his left hand slightly hovering like he was ready to snatch the other man. “Are you an imbecile?!”

Both the young men barely got along well, under the mutual understanding of bare tolerance towards one another. They were complete opposites to each other. Julian came from a blacksmith family with humbling backgrounds. Ravus was the son of a duke and the sole heir of a sizable estate before his occupation as an adventurer. They were also opposite in views. Ravus was a realist bordering on skepticism, often giving sniping remarks and brutal criticism as his preferred form of communication. Julian was unrelentingly optimistic. He was so optimistic, that it was bordering on their idealism. Not only that, but the bard was also absent-minded, a wide-eyed country bumpkin with too much of an optimistic view but with very little luck.

Julian decided to ignore the last bit of the question thrown at him, “aren’t you, at least, curious about what the monster looks like?” he pushed forward with a torch in hand. The light from the fire casts undecipherable shadowed over the uneven walls of the cavern, and it gave an eerie feeling of an unwanted observer hiding in the darkness. From somewhere behind the rear of the party, there was Gemini Del Amer as the second-in-command as the party’s cleric and voice of reason. With an almost tired manner stated that they must not rush into danger carelessly, regardless of whether curiosity is involved or not.  

Different from the reckless Julian and aggressively sarcastic Ravus, the redheaded Triton —a humanoid piscean race— was aloof and cool-headed, coming from his background as a crown prince from a neighbouring country. He was never cold. He tried his best to heal his party members’ injuries, despite how many stupid stunts they pulled with only a sigh and their half-hearted comments. Opted to strategize the next plan or route the party would take instead of joining Ravus and Julian’s ridiculous competition to woo patrons in the taverns they visited. Of course he again tried to act as the voice of reason for the group.

“We’ll be fine as long as we stay together and be careful,” Julian reasoned with a grin, “besides, I’m getting better with my rapier.”

Ravus shot him a disbelieving look and smacked the back of the bard’s unprotected knees with the dull side of his sword to prove a point. Julian let out a surprised yelp.

“Was that necessary?” Josselyn scolded somewhere in the back, slightly perplexed by the treatment that her brother received. If Julian had bad luck, then his twin sister absorbed all the good ones. Josselyn was a fellow warlock and the only female at the party. She also had the ridiculous luck and power in her deceptively cheerful and often nervous personality, even surpassing Ravus, who had proper training. Josselyn tend to stick between her brother and Ophiucus, but lately asked Ravus to guide her on some of her spell works. However, she still prefered to stay beside her brother and the party’s rogue. Ophiucus was the final member of the party as the quietest one, an Aarakocra —a hybrid of human and avian— rogue that seems to be too soft and kind for his occupation. Currently, he looks like he would rather be anywhere else than get stuck in the crossfire between the other members.

As they began to bicker more heavily, a sudden rumble shook the entirety of the cave. The stalactites started to fall from the top of them, like an almost ridiculous rendition of the swords of Damocles. Julian gave another undignified yelp as Ravus yanked him from becoming a target of one of the sharp ends of nature’s deathtrap. Both men fell to the dirt with a loud ‘oomph!’. From behind, Ophiucus tried to tug Josselyn to the mouth of the cavern to witness it collapse under the weight of the heavy boulders that were acting as the roof.

“Run!” Gemini yelled as he dodged falling rocks, waving them to enter deeper into the cave to avoid more of the falling obstacles. His voice was barely audible over the continued rumble.

They ran for their lives as the roof started to cave in more, Gemini using his shield at the front while both Ravus and Josselyn tried their best to blast the stray rocks away from them. Julian was sinking. Felt the mortality forced him to run faster than he had ever done in his life.

Upfront tunnel began to widen, and the rumble lessened to a barely-there vibration. They stopped at a space inside a cave that looked similar to a circular room with two other paths branching. Before they could collapse to the ground, a loud growl was heard from above them. In an almost comedic manner, their terrified eyes shot up to wherever the source of the noise was.

Somewhere beside him, Julian can hear Ravus swearing loudly while Josselyn gave a frightened gasp. “Of all of the monsters hiding in this hole,” Gemini groaned tiredly from behind as they watched a grey orc observing them while hanging from the side of the cavern wall. Julian feels faint as he watches the orc give them a particularly terrifying grin with all of his teeth on display.

‘We’re going to die.’ he mourned silently.

Like a puppet cut from its strings, everyone sprung to combat as the 6 feet tall creature gave a roar and thumped its hands to the wall, causing stalactites and part of the roof to fall. Ravus rolled on his sides to avoid Ophiucus flying away.

“We need to even the ground!” the warlock yelled behind him, aligning herself enough to try to blast one of the orc’s hands holding the wall with her spell.

Josselyn gave a quick nod before running to the other side, “I call upon thee for power and blood, O’Being of Olde; Eldritch blast!” She chanted a spell that produced black flames on her hands which she threw in the general direction of the monster.

Gemini stabbed his greatsword to the ground along with his shield, chanting a prayer of protection to the surrounding radius. Right on time as the orc decided he had enough and threw a sizable boulder ripped straight from the walls at them. The rock created a deafening thud as it hitted the top part of the Triton’s yellowish shield spell, made the whole thing vibrate.

“How are we going to defeat that?!” Julian yelled almost hysterically.

“Grey orcs are not as strong as the normal ones, but they’re master tacticians,” Gemini greeted with his teeth, “the earthquake we felt must’ve been a trap to lure us in deeper.”

“Oh, bloody fun—”

“Watch out!” Josselyn yelled in despair as Ophiucus flew too close to the orc. They watched it helplessly as the monster batted him away like an incessant fly. Ravus swore again as he tried to blast the orc off. Only success could piss it off more.

“Josselyn, help me distract it!”

The female warlock started to cast a volley of black flames that she threw at the grey orc. The ground aggressively shook as it hitted the target. Julian tried his best to carry the injured hybrid avian within the shield. He checked for minor injuries, deducing that the rogue must have torn a muscle when he bounced off the uneven cavern walls.

"Please, don’t die, please! I will be more careful next time." Julian chanted in his mind as he tried his best to heal the pale-looking man. Julian was never actually trained as a proper healer. He only took the role due to his clumsy nature in their adventures as his reluctance to put more burden on Gemini, who was the actual healer. He was starting to doubt his ability before giving an exhale of relief as the wounds began to close.

Another loud boom began to echo within the cave as one of Josselyn’s spells managed the grey orc lose his grip from his perch. He landed heavily on the ground before gave an angry roar and banging his chest. Ravus gave a loud battle cry as he unsheathed his black sword and charged forward. The orc tried to smack him away, but he dodged skillfully, used his magic to boost himself airborne. Gemini tried to bind the monster to the grown with his own spell. Josselyn continued with her volley of spells.

Suddenly as fast as lighting, the orc swiftly took out a giant ax from its back and swung it to intercept with Ravus’s own sword. It took him out if not for his cushioning spells. Julian swore loudly and tried his best to run towards his sister, who seemed to be the orc’s next target as it swung a wide downward arch towards her.

For a moment, he saw her. Then she was gone under the dust and debris.

A horrified yell vibrated through the walls that Julian detachedly realized came from him. Seeing the shaken man and the fallen warlock,  Gemini stopped his protection spell by lifting his greatsword and shield to help them fight.

However, that was their first mistake.

The grey orc gave them a terrifying grin before suddenly throwing the ax at Ravus, who was still trying to attack the orc relentlessly. It forced the warlock to evade and gave space between them. The orc then took out a small device from his pouch. It looked at the remaining party members, mocking. It gave them an almost creepily human-like wave to taunt them before throwing the device to the ground and running off to the exit path on the left side.

Julian let out a terrified gasp as he recognized rumble from the start of their quest was once again present. Almost threw the injured Ophiucus, who was secretly trying to drag the passed-out Josselyn away off balance. The ground shook hard enough, and the floors began to crack. Ravus held an injured shoulder and a bloodied head. He threw a desperate glance towards the path where the orc left before yelling for them to run, “We’ll use the same exit!”

They started to run towards the exit as fast as their feet could carry them, dodging fallen rocks and cracking floors below them. Julian helped Ophiucus carry his unconscious sister while Gemini tried to shield them, and Ravus led the way. A sense of desperation and dread hanging over their head as they push harder. The floor in front of them gave way, and Ravus hastily jumped over, followed by the red-haired Triton and the avian hybrid.

Realized that his hands were full, Julian shot the party members with a terrified look. Gemini sensed his insecurity, and tried to calm him down.

“Just throw her here, and we’ll catch her, and then you will jump.”

“If you drop her, I will wreck you.” the bard said with a wave of protectiveness.

“Don’t worry, now do it!”

With a huff and a swing, he threw his sister’s unconscious body across the chasm to the waiting arms of Ravus and Ophiucus, gave a loud groan as the heavyweight forced them to strain their injured arms. However, she landed safely, and he gave a loud exhale of relief.

“Alright, my turn now.”

He called them from the other side. Their hands stretched to grab him. In case he didn't make the jump. More boulders and stalactites began to fall as the ground shook harder. Julian inhaled sharply to prepare himself before running to the edge and leaping with his hands outstretched. He jumped.

Tere lay their second mistake. They waited too long and were so distracted by Josselyn that they didn’t realize how the floor began to fall at an accelerating speed, widening the gap that separated Julian and the other party members even more.

His fingertips lightly grazed theirs before the world around him crumbled, and the ground beneath him disappeared.
