Man on the Mirror

Rizaldy Fikri Al Khairi
Horror

TOMORROW MY SISTER will visit me. She visits me once a week to check my health conditions. She is a fresh graduate from medical school out of town. Due to what she said, I have a weak heart and memory loss because of my childhood trauma. Apparently our mother's death was the reason for my trauma, according to my sister. I do not remember any of it. She also said that she did not know how our mother died. All she knew is that our mother was killed by someone.

        Since today is a special occasion for us, my sister is going to spend more time than she usually does. October 3rd was the day our mother left us behind with no guidance. Of course, I have no memory of it. My sister is coming late to the grave. She said she had to go to the supermarket first. I can see her across the street coming in my direction. I look at my watch, thirty minutes late. I am curious what made her late to visit our mother’s grave.

“Where have you been? The supermarket is only five minutes walk from our house.” I ask her with a high tone, almost angry and disappointed. “Sorry, I had to buy flowers for mom.” She gives me a reasonable answer.

We drive to our home together when she plays classical music on the road. She said it makes her calm. Suddenly I remember that we used to hear classical music when we were young. We are not saying even a word in the car. She focuses on the road and listening to the music, and I open up my phone and watch some stuff on Youtube. Suddenly she starts breaking the silence between us after the music stops.

“Do you still have the pills I prescribed you?” She asks me with a lower voice.

“Yeah, I think. Why?” I answer with hesitation, knowing that I don’t even know if there is any pill left.

“Call me if you need more, and do not let the bottle empty without telling me. Okay, Karl?"

“Okay. By the way, how long are you going to stay?”

“Uh, I don’t know. Maybe three days. I need to settle things here for mom.”

“What things?” I asked curiously.

“You know, it's adult matters. I’ll tell you once it's done. What do you want to eat for tomorrow?” She tried to change the topic of the conversation.

“I wanna eat chicken.”

“Alright, chicken it is.”

After such a long journey from my mother’s grave, I come back home with so little energy to take a bath and clean up ourselves. I don’t know about my sister, but once I am in my bed, my head feels dizzy. I hear the sound of a car machine outside my house. It must be my sister who is going outside. She must be buying the chicken that I want.

Three A.M in the morning, I wake up from my sleep. The dizziness in my head has gotten much worse. I try to find the pills that my sister gets me. I look into the cabinet where I stash the medication that I have. It seems that I cannot find it. I start to remember where the last time I put the bottle of those pills was. A glimpse came into my mind. Apparently the sink in the bathroom is the last place where I put the bottle. Luckily, I find the bottle, but as I see the bottle more carefully, I realize that the pills in the bottle are already empty. I feel dizzy again, and now it makes my head spin. I try to look at the mirror in front of me to get hold of myself.

Once I see my reflection in the mirror, the dizziness slowly leaves my body but the spinning still there. I talk to myself in the mirror and say that I will be alright. When I look into my eyes in the mirror, I notice that my reflection is smiling. I am pretty sure that I did not smile at the time. That phenomenon shocked me. I jump back, but when I blink, the reflection turns back to normal.

“What was that?” I ask myself about what just happened in front of my eyes. But I don’t want to make it into my overthinking. I assume maybe it's just a hallucination that my brain made for the effect of the headache. Noticing that the headache is gone, then I proceed to go back to sleep.

When I wake up, I go down the stairs and try to find food in the kitchen. But I found nothing. Then I remember that my sister left the house in the middle of the night. I wonder if she is home yet. I hear the sound of the stairs start to crack as I walk it over. Then I am already in front of her door. I start shouting her name, but there is no reply. I am about to open the door and hear the sound of a car matching coming from outside. I look down the window to check who is coming. It was my sister. I come down the stairs again.

“Where were you last night?” A question come from my mouth as soon as the door opened.

“Jeez, you startled me. My friend said she left her belongings in my apartment, so I need to get back there to open the door.” She answers me while having both hands ready to protect herself, just like the boxing defense position.

“Sorry if I startle you. I don’t mean it,” I replied while lowering my voice.

“Are you okay? Your face looks pale?”

I looked at her face. She looked worried about my condition.

“Yeah, I just couldn't sleep properly last night.”

“Rest properly, Karl. You know you have a weak heart.”

“I know, I know... Do you bring the chicken that we talked about yesterday?”

“Ah, I forgot about it. Why don’t you go to Mr. Gu’s store and buy chicken there?”

“Aaaaahhhh, alright, alright. I am going to go there. But let me borrow your car.”

“hmmm.. But only this time.”

“Alright.”

Then, I grab my jacket and the money for the chicken. While on the road I keep wondering, is there something off about her today. Then I remember, she never let me borrow her car before. Why today she let me borrow her car today? I am trying to be positive about it and just assume that she was feeling sorry that she did not bring the chicken.

        I arrive at Mr. Gu’s store and act fast to buy a chicken. I am about to leave when Mr. Gu stops and tells me,

“I saw your’ sister cryin’ on the park last night.” The information he gave me contradicts what my sister told me this morning.

“Is it Mr. Gu? Are you sure that you are not seeing another person?” I asked to confirm the information that Mr. Gu gave me.

“Yeah, I know it was dark at the time. But I know it is yer’ sister’s car that parked there.”

“Alright, thank you, Mr. Gu, for the information. I am going to leave now.” This makes my head hurt again. I don’t know what the truth is.

        The sky is getting dark, and the wind starts to blow. I get into the car and play music. The car radio in her car is broken, so it is just a music player. I look into a playlist in her car, and I realize that she only listens to classical music. It's just... I cannot enjoy it because I cannot sing along to the song. But there is no choice, so I play the only music in the car to accompany me on my way back home.

        When I get home, I deliver the chicken to my sister in the kitchen. She immediately starts to cut the chicken into some parts. I see her doing that, and I go to the bathroom in my room. My head still hurts because of the information that Mr. Gu gave me. I start to look for my medicine, but I remember it is empty. The headache is getting worse. I cannot scream my sister’s name to ask for help. But I still can whisper. Right now, I believe that this is just my hallucination because the man I saw in the mirror is not me anymore. His movement and his body language are very different from mine. His expression is also different from my expression right now.

“Who...are...you?” I barely can talk

“Me? You may not know me, but I know every little thing about you. I even know a memory that you don’t remember.” He talks very slowly, like he is playing with me.

“What? You... know... what... happened... on... the... day... my... mother... died?”

“Yes, do you know why? Because I was there when your mother died.” He laughs. His laughing is terrifying.

“What? You... are... me... right. So... it... means...”

“HA HA HA, you finally understand. Stop consuming the pills that your sister gave you, and your memory will slowly come back. The truth will be brought to the light.”

        Thirty minutes later, I wake up because I heard my sister’s voice. She asks me to go downstairs to help her with the meal. I look at my face in the mirror, and I do not see him at the moment. Rather, I see myself being pretty messed up because I was unconscious for thirty minutes. My eyes feel so heavy, my head is not hurt anymore, and my chest is getting warm as my heart is beating.

“Karl, come downstairs and help me serve the meal.” She screamed from downstairs, knowing that I did not answer her first call.

“Wait, I’ll be there soon,” I answer her call, trying not to make her upset.

        I washed my face and prepared myself to be as fresh as possible. But in the end, my eyes still feel heavy. As I walk down the stairs, the memory that I lost slowly begins to come back. One by one, the memory of the day of my mother’s death is coming into the light. The sound of the rain, along with the thunder, makes the mood in the house more gloomy. But it is lightened with the sound of classical music that my sister plays in the kitchen.

 “I am here. What do you need me for?”

“Help me prepare the plate and the drinks.”

I am not replying to her words like I used to and just do what she asks me to do.

“Can I ask you a question, Sis?”

“Yeah, what is it?”

“The pills that you gave me, what is it for?” I look into her eyes, and she looks worried.

“Why did you ask that question? Of course, it is for your heart issue.”

“That’s a lie, right? You know, when I did not take the pills, my heart was still fine. It's not like I have a weak heart. But rather, when  I did not take the pills, it only gave me a headache.” I talk with a little bit of anger because of a lie she said.

“Karl?! Are the pills empty? What did I say about telling me when it's about to get empty?” She sounds angry, and the look in her eyes, I don’t know what expression that is.

“I was about to tell you, but I forgot, and the man in the mirror told me not to.”

“Karl, my brother. It is not true. It's just your imagination. Don’t listen to that thing you see.” She tries to calm me down.

I do not reply to her words. My hands moved on their own to the direction where the knife was.

“Karl, please. Do not repeat what you did to your mother ten years ago. Please, Karl, I am begging you,” she asks me with tears along her face.

“So you know what I did to our mother? WHY DON’T YOU TELL ME THE TRUTH? I WAS LIVING MY WHOLE LIFE NOT KNOWING ANYTHING ABOUT IT UNTIL NOW!! WHEN I DID NOT TAKE YOUR PILLS, THE TRUTH IS NOW UNCOVERED.” I become furious about the fact that she hide the truth from me.

“I am sorry, Karl. Mother asked me not to tell you the truth before she died. I was there too when you killed her.” She is crying for her life.

        Once I hear that, I become more furious. I do the same thing that happened to my mother. The sound of thunderous rain combined with classical music make a perfect day for me to repeat what I did ten years ago. It is exactly the same as what happened ten years ago, a thunderous rain, my mother playing her favorite classical music. I believed that she lied to me at the time, that was why I killed her.

Now I understand why my sister acted like that to me after mother’s death. Never smile in front of me and the worried eyes. I know the reason. She is afraid of me. And her fear now became reality that put her down to sleep forever. I am starting to be afraid of myself. I left alone in this old house, leaving me no choice but to kill myself too, after calling the police.

Fin.
