Lonely Universe

Nadhira Aulia Azri
Slice of life

Hey, Eito, what do you think the universe is trying to imply when it decides that we shall meet underneath the starry sky?

I still remember the first time I met Eito, as it is one of the brightest memories I had in my mind. It is also a moment that I hold really dear in my heart, and I recall it with a fond smile more often than I would like to admit.

It was a beautiful night in April. The sky was clear without any smoke or fog apparent insight, twinkling stars decorating the horizon, drawing maps of constellations you can follow with your bare eyes, a rare occurrence in a center prefecture like Shinjuku. The faint scent of sakura fills the atmosphere with sweetness and warmth; accompanied by the gush of air that playfully blows the pink petals from the ground, chills lingering just enough. A perfect day to be spent outside.

I was sitting in a chair in a park, taking a breath to collect my mind after walking several blocks to buy new stationeries since I would have my first college opening ceremony the day after. I am going to be a freshman at a private university so I was very anxious because I don’t want to mess anything up.

Huffing, I gaze around the park, and that is when I took notice of four boys playing two-on-two volleyball on a nearby court. Interested, I put aside my belongings and watched them with a mid-interest. They were all so full of energy, screaming and laughing as if there was no tomorrow.

After some time, they stopped playing, dropping to the ground with heavy breaths. By the look of it, I’m pretty sure they will leave anytime soon. Understandable, it is getting pretty late. I too had calmed down so I grabbed my tote bag and walked, silently planning what I should eat for breakfast tomorrow so that I can still be on time. I think the usual rice and miso soup would be too much so maybe cereals will suffice?

“Excuse me,” a deep voice stopped my tracks from behind. I turned around and was faced with a strong pair of shoulders instead of a direct face. This person is tall TALL, I think his height is almost two meters. His height just single-handedly called my non-growing body a midget, sigh. Why is he so tall?!

I blinked then tilted my head upwards. Oh, he is one of the boys from before, looking downwards with a friendly gaze. He is handsome and his body proportion is nice to look at, my brain supplied unhelpfully. Not gonna lie that spiky but seemingly soft hair is very adorable, also. Like a hedgehog.

“Hedgehog?” Crap, did I say that out loud?

“Well, I do have pet hedgehogs at home so being called one is a huge compliment.” He laughed, then grabbed something from his pocket. “I just want to return this, anyway.” He handed me something – oh my god how can I be so clumsy that I almost lost my student id even before I was an official student – “Or well I think this is yours.”

“Ah, yes! Thank you very much!” I took it gratefully, putting it in the deepest pit of my wallet and then shrugging it completely in my bag.

“I also go to that college so we may cross paths many times after this.” A bright smile appears, showing sparkling bunny teeth. Crinkled eyes shining with mirth and friendly joy that could put the stars to shame. “My name is Eito, nice to meet you.”

Something in my universe shifts.

Just like meteors dancing in space, our fates interlace; a realization I like to embrace.

Trying to be more active outside my academic life, I joined the journalism club, specifically online article making. The senior that welcomed me and immediately offered to help me in the club is a warm and smiley person who told me to call him Shunsuke.

Today, Shunsuke asked me to join him on a duty to give me a quick overview of how the club activity is. He told me that he is in charge of the monthly article for the men's volleyball team, which is apparently very strong and famous in the college championships.

"I talked with the manager and she said this interesting person is applying, the one we are going to interview today,” Shunsuke explained, opening the door of the gymnasium and heading straight to a secluded room in the corner, smiling and bowing his head when we passed through some of the players sitting on the floor, who greets back with lazy nod and wave. “He is the key player of his high school's victory in the Spring Tournament this year, even winning the Most Valuable Player award."

"He got many free passes from well-known colleges with reputable volleyball teams yet he decided to enroll here because he insists on focusing not only on the sport but his academics as well." He adds, opening his laptop and ushering me to sit beside him.

I nod, interested. He seems like a genuine and hardworking person who already has his life together. I am not at that level yet so I might be able to ask for some tips!

"Apparently even the strongest professional team in our league right now offers him a spot." He gestured to the document, asking me to see. "But since the recent requirement does not allow an active student, he refused, saying he wants to pursue his dream as a teacher as well."

I looked at the profile displayed on the screen, skimming through the information when the door slid open, making us immediately stand to greet the person arriving. Two people, a girl that Shunsuke said is the team manager, and— Eito?

 

When I’m fluttering in my small and quiet milky way, you always come to save the day.

Several months have passed. I grew closer with Eito each day since I was practically the co-writer for the volleyball team’s monthly article with Shunsuke at that point. Shunsuke laughed at that, saying that I should be honored to be put in his care. I laughed along, feigning to be touched with his kindness.

It was a sunny Friday evening, the team is going to have their regular practice so I came along to take some pictures for documentary purposes. The gymnasium was loud with the boys’ screaming, accompanied by the heavy sound of balls being thrown and shoes scratching the floor. The usual lively and bright atmosphere always puts me at ease, a pleasant feeling I’ve grown accustomed to.

I made a beeline to the spot the freshmen were practicing and immediately found Eito sitting on the floor with his back facing me, a bubbly laugh and several high-pitched laughs can be heard quite clearly. The others are spreading around, doing their usual drills I have seen countless times.

“Eito? You’re not practicing?” I greeted.

“Hi! No, not yet.” He faced me, greeting back with quirked lips. “I was assigned to take care of the coach’s kids at the moment.”

That’s when I noticed two little girls sticking around him, one circling her arms around his neck and one gripping his left arm. Wow, Eito is good with kids as well, huh? Folding my legs to sit near the group, all of them then looked up at me, wearing the same sunshine smile on their faces. Eito pats their arms, “Say hello, girls.”

“Good afternoon!” The two kids shouted joyfully. Eito nods in satisfaction, proudly ruffling their hair with the softest expression I’ve ever seen, and I–

I feel like floating inside the fluffy clouds.

My constellation has long forgotten how to shine, but Eito, your presence kindles it divine.

It was exactly one year and one month later. We were in our second year. I was still sticking around with Shunsuke, working together with him to keep the volleyball website alive and updated. Talking about the volleyball team, they were currently busy preparing and practicing for the upcoming Spring League. Eito being Eito has glued himself to the practice court more often than not.

Eito was very excited today, he was practically buzzing and bouncing in his seat. A reputable alumnus is going to come to visit to give them some motivation and all that jazz. Eito told me that he really respected him, saying that he is his main role model and it was a big honor to be able to inherit his jersey number, that he wants to be his successor. Not only that, he also declared with fire in his eyes that he would surpass him someday.

We are standing near the door of the hall, Eito is leaning on one wall with his usual megawatt smile. I’m in front of him, helping Shunsuke set up the camera and microphone. Since the senior is a very famous player from the national team, he was asked to document it not only in the article but as a video, too.

Soon, the aforementioned senior arrived. He is a tall and broad man who eludes this serious but humble and approachable aura around him. I took a peek at Eito and that boy was practically beaming. I could imagine a tail wagging behind him, a huge grin appeared on his face. I can’t help but mirror his expression. Adorable.

After some time, two freshmen inched close to the door, looking at each other in hesitation. I realized that the senior and Eito are blocking the handle and they certainly don’t want to be disrespectful to just pass through in front. Honestly, I wouldn’t know what to do either.

Eito, bless him, noticed as well. He casts a quick glance, slowly stepping away from the door; opening it just enough without breaking the conversation with the senior at all. He gestured to them to enter, continuing the discussion in front of the camera easily. Very attentive.

Ah, Eito, how can I not adore you if you are like this?

 

Why say I'm lonely like it’s a curse when I have Eito as my universe?

Eito and his extremely lovely behaviors do not stop there. Throughout the three years we’ve been friends there would always be something that makes him my inspiration even more. One thing is his soft spot for animals. Eito has pet hedgehogs for real, just like what he mentioned the first time we met. Not one or two, but a whole six member crew, all named after him with just several changes in the last syllable, like Eiro, Eina, and Eimu. He always talks about them in the fondest expression you could imagine, calling them his babies he loves the most. However, since he was currently living in the sports dormitory, he had not been able to see them often, complaining about how much he had missed them.

This also goes without saying but Eito is the most hardworking person I know. The way he could balance his academic life, his passion for volleyball, and his social life at once is beyond my imagination. I mean, competing in the national level competitions as a starting player while keeping an outstanding GPA and catching up with his relatives? Amazing, really. I could never. Just look at me struggling to do my homeworks even though the only thing I do is laying down at my bed. Not something to brag about, certainly. Beside that, Eito had even pointed to talk as a key speaker in a freshmen welcoming ceremony, proudly sharing his journey; illuminating the world with his light. Chuckling, it has just occurred to me that I had actually become Eito’s number one supporter. His biggest fan, if you will (I can hear Shunsuke snickering and calling me a whipped coward but no, Shunsuke, you don’t get it!). What I like the most about him is the fact that all his fascinating qualities do not make me feel inferior, but rather, inspired;

Eito is the brightest star that does not burn me, but one that gives me hope and aspiration to shine on my own as well.

 

Thank you, my Ursa Major, for helping me find the right path.

We’re now in our last year at university. Eito is still as busy and thriving as ever. I had long accepted how amazing Eito is that I thought I wouldn’t be unfazed anymore. Yet, seeing him hustling and writing his graduate thesis all the while being the vice-captain of the volleyball team and our province’s representative still makes me sigh in awe, because really, how? Thankfully, though, the journalism club does not give the main positions’ duties and responsibilities for the fourth year students because frankly, I did not trust myself enough to be able to manage my time perfectly. Shunsuke had also graduated this year, so I do not really hang around in the club as often anymore.

Eito is a stable part of my life I don’t want to separate with. He had taught me many things, he was also the one teaching me to believe in myself. Everything from finding myself, seizing the present, to even planning my future; every single thing I do, I remember how he is always there with me, encouraging with his comforting presence.

We are currently sitting in our usual spot in the library, looking through the open window that faces the garden filled with falling sakura petals. Eito has his left palm supporting his head, expression soft and relaxed in serenity as he gazes ahead at the pink carpets decorating the whole landscape. I look at him for a while more before focusing back on the scenery, smiling.

Our graduation ceremony is coming next week, and I will enjoy these precious moments with Eito to the fullest.

 

The future lies in undefined gravity, but we’ll be fine; our glow will always align.

The ceremony had ended many hours ago. After saying the (honestly very dramatic) farewells to my friends both in the class and in the journalism club, I stride to the gate, heavily preparing myself for what to come after.

Eito is leaning on a wall nearby, perking up when he spots me. Without saying anything, he grinned and joined me, walking side by side to the familiar park near the campus that held many memories for us. I halted in my step, staring up at the vast and clear sky decorated with twinkling stars, beautiful with all the sentiments it carried.

“Stars,” Eito muttered, blinking slowly. He chuckled, pointing at one group of stars forming a constellation that looks abstract but aesthetically pleasing nonetheless. “Look at that, the two stars nestling together!” He grinned. “They were just like us, right?”

I breathed, smiling. His joy is always contagious to me. “Yeah, they really do.”

“But you know,” Eito whispered in a serious tone. “Even after tens and hundreds of years, I really hope we did not forget about our light and connection just like those stars do.”

The chilling breeze blew, bringing along the faint sweet scent of sakura. Everything is the exact same as our first meeting. Memories flow through my mind, along with the tears that started to block my vision. All the cry and laughter we had shared that I cherished. If we were really to vanish into the thin air like bubbles, I would be so sad that I could not stop crying.

“Hey, don’t cry,” Eito mutters, laughing softly. He turns to face me, the faint glow of the moon lit him from behind, hugging his figure just right. “I’m sure we will meet again.”

I don’t respond, still trying to regain my breath; fighting the blurred light and lines that are still consistently hanging in my sight.

“Hey, we promised, didn’t we?” Eito smiles, patting my head, a gesture he often does when he senses someone is anxious and in need of some reassurance. “We’re going to shine together in the future.”

Our eyes met.

“Until then,”

 

“Let’s keep our stars alight.”
