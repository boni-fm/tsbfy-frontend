Hasta La Vista

Rosdiana Sahara
Romance

SOME people were not familiar with who they met, talked, and what they did every minute. Let’s say it was something that was out of control, or they didn’t care about anything around them. That’s possible. Jihan wasa girl that always thought that she was in a wonderland because she had never once felt or experienced what she was experiencing right now. She felt happy, so happy. It was quite strange that people around her sometimes didn’t see that.

        Jihan smiled right away when she saw her childhood-neighbor best friend stepping into the bus that she was in. Why was she smiling? It was easy. She had been having a big crush on him forever, and people knew about that because she kind of showed it in public.

        “Hello, joyfriend.”

        Yes, they called each other joyfriend.

        “My joyfriend Janu!” Jihan greeted him. She looked happy as usual. “Oh. Woah! Have you got a new phone? That’s crazy.”

        Janu nodded and smiled without saying anything. Janu was a shy, introverted, and calm person, so nothing new if he acted that way. Everybody knew it.

        The passengers in the bus with them started looking at Jihan for some reason, and Jihan realized that they all were looking at her with their weird glaze. Is my voice too loud? She asks herself, but she forgot about it and started not giving a deal with it.

        “Is everybody here looking at me, or I’m tripping?”

        Janu frowned. “Really? A star syndrome in a bus? You’re kidding, Jihan.”

        “Oh, c’mon! Do I have something on my face?”

        “Nope, you look okay.”

        “Oh my god! Why are people so intimidating?” Jihan sighed because she thought that she had a problem with herself. She couldn’t do literally anything freely in public. At the end of the day, she made every little thing a big deal for her.

        “Sorry for talking too much. I’m afraid people will judge me. Oh, they definitely are!” She added.

        “Just don’t mind them.” Janu gave her a pat on her shoulder.

        “Well, I will.” 

THE day continues. These best friends would sit in front of Jihan’s house to talk about everything for hours. Just like that time. They were sitting side by side and facing their neighbors’ houses. Jihan was always grateful if they both had time together, even though they always do. It’s Jihan’s happiness to be with Janu in her free time. For some reason, she felt at home if she was with Janu and didn’t feel the same way when she’s with her family. Her family, especially her dad, hated her for so long, and Jihan didn’t know why until now. People around here would say that she was a crazy girl because she always spent time alone. At that moment, Jihan would find herself saying, alone? Because she thought she was not. Of course, she had Janu.

        “Do you think I can move out of this house, Janu?”

        Jihan hitted Janu’s hand and then laughed. The last time she felt happy was about six years ago when she and Janu went on a vacation together with their family. She didn’t understand why they never hung out anymore like that time. Besides the family hatred towards her, they could’ve just hung out together, both of them. Janu had never said anything to Jihan when she talked about how she had been wanting to go on a vacation together.

        “Jihan!” A neighbor of theirs that was the same age walked down the street with a bag on her shoulder. It seemed like she just got home from somewhere.

        “Oh, hi, Mel!”

        “What are you doing, sitting there? It’s almost nine p.m.”

        “Talking?” Jihan answered and got confused.

        “Like… on the phone?” Mel asked again.

        “No, with Ja-” before Jihan answered, Mel cutted off the conversation by saying that she had to go because her mom needed her. Both Jihan said bye to Mel. Jihan smiled a second later after seeing Janu said bye to Mel as well, because she knew everybody knew that Janu wasn’t that easy-going. He did say bye even though it’s only a bye. Why was he being weird?

        It’s Jihan’s second time catched people looking at her while talking.  She had no idea what’s going on, but she thought people were weird these days.

THREE days later, again, Jihan spent time with Janu. Both of them would never get tired of spending time together because they were just comfortable with each other. Strange things kept coming. Jihan notices that people always looked at her when she was out of the house minding her own business. Meanwhile, her parents always yelled and said that she was a crazy girl that lived in their house. She didn't understand. Why do people look at me that way? Why do my parents say I’m crazy?

        “I’m not being self-centered, but do you know why people are staring at me, and my parents say that I’m a crazy girl, Janu?”

        “You will keep talking about that?” Janu looked at Jihan.

        “Yeah because it just keeps getting weird, you know?” Jihan sighed. It’s been hard for Jihan. People weirdly looked at her, got scolded by her parents every day, and got called crazy by them. She was just pissed off, but she couldn’t do anything about that. “If you want me not to think about it, well, I do want to, but I feel sad.” She added.

        “Maybe you’re just pretty.”

        “Oh, c’mon! People would not look at me with their weird-scary gaze if I’m pretty. And my parents saying I’m crazy? That’s not possible.”

        Janu giggled. Seeing Janu’s smile always made Jihan happy. Let’s say that was the only thing that kept Jihan happy and alive.

“But, you are, though.”

        “Oh. Woah, thanks, joyfriend.” Jihan smiled.

“But, isn't it weird that they only look at me. I repeat. Only me, not you.”

        Janu shrugged. Jihan continued eating her hotdog, tried to forget it. They sat in front of Jihan’s house again, ate and talked. Not long after that, Jihan’s father’s car came into the yard. Her father came home from somewhere. Jihan had no idea what her father did outside because she never talked to her father. After all, he hated her, and so did Jihan.

        Jihan tried not to make eye contact with her father when he got out of the car with his bag on his shoulder. He looks scary.

        “We’re not wrong to say you’re crazy.” That was Jihan’s father.

        Jihan frowned. “What do you mean?”

        Her father looked back when he was about to open the door.

“You’re crazy. You have a problem with your mind or whatever. Don’t you know that? You know what? I don’t want to have a crazy daughter like you, if you want to know. You’re crazy, and so sick of you.”

        “Excuse me?”

        “Of course, you would answer with ‘what do you mean? ‘Excuse me?’. You’re a crazy girl. I hope you know that.” Jihan’s father slammed the door.

        Oh wow. Jihan’s father never talked that much to Jihan. Once he did, he broke Jihan’s heart into pieces. At this point, she didn’t even have the energy to cry. Instead, she questioned a lot in her mind. She didn't get how her father kept saying she’s crazy. She had no clue at all.

        Janu looked at her.

“Are you okay?”

        “I am. Don’t worry. I don’t understand.”

        “He’s so-”

        “I know right?” Jihan continued. She knows what Janu was talking about.

        Again, Jihan didn’t understand why she kept getting this so much. She had never done anything bad, and she had good grades. At least that was what parents wanted for their children. Right?

        Minutes later, the door opened. Her father was there again with the gaze that Jihan understood. Oh, he’s about to yell again. She mentally prepared. She knew she would get called crazy or stuff again.

        “Are you seriously crazy?!” Jihan’s father asked. His voice rised.

        “Dad, listen. I’ve never fought you or whatsoever all this time. When you called me crazy, I accepted that. So now, I don’t understand what you’re talking about. Why do you keep calling me crazy?! Oh my god.”

        Jihan’s father looked at Jihan with disgust. “Oh, you’re screaming at me? Are you dumb?”

        “Why are you…-No why do you always call me crazy? Why do you hate me so much?”

        “BECAUSE YOU ARE CRAZY!” Jihan’s father yelled. “Do you know that you always talk to someone that we can’t see? You’re crazy!”

        Jihan frowned. She clearly didn’t understand what her father was talking about. Talking to someone you can’t see?

﻿“What do ymean?”

        “Yeah, I hate you so much for always being a crazy daughter, talking to a ghost or whatever, calling Janu’s name like he’s still here. Are you crazy? He died three years ago, and you’re telling me you talk to him all this time?!”

        Jihan sat limply on the floor right away. She couldn’t believe what she just heard. She was so sure that Janu had been there with her. She was still sure. But then again, that time, she realized that she hallucinates. Janu has not been here since three years ago.
