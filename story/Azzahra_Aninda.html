The Undisclosed Truth

Azzahra Moulia Andinda
Horror

My name is Audrey. I was 12 years old when my family and I were involved in a terrible conflagration. I couldn't remember anything except for the screaming sounds and the piercing pain of my burning skin.  When I woke up, I had no memory of ever being a child. My parents, along with my sister, died before they could be identified. I've spent these past few months searching through our memories to see if there was something that could shed light on what happened in the fire. There's nothing. It seems like the only way to find any answers would be to dig into our own minds. So, I have. But I keep coming back to the same questions. Why did I end up here? How was I able to survive the fire? I wish there was someone that could tell me the answers. Someone who knew what happened and why I found myself this way.

I sit alone in a somehow abandoned hospital room, staring at the walls as I wait for the nurse to come back with the information I'm looking for. All around me are people sleeping peacefully, their heads resting against the soft pillows they're sitting on. The silence is deafening. Every so often I hear footsteps walking down the hall, and when I do look up, I can see nurses checking everyone's vitals or changing someone's sheets. As far as hospitals go, it's not too bad. There are a lot of flowers decorating the halls, making sure patients know they are loved and cared for. The smell of fresh flowers fills the air, but it isn't enough to calm my nerves.

Finally, after what feels like hours but is probably only minutes later, the door to the room opens, and a familiar woman enters. It's nurse Edna. She walks towards me and sets a stack of files down on the bed next to where I am sitting. Her blonde hair reaches her waist, and she smiles at me. "So, how you feeling?" she asks.

"Fine, Edna." I respond flatly.

She raises an eyebrow. "That doesn't really sound like a fine answer. Are you sure you don't want to talk about it?"

I sigh and shake my head. "It's just been bothering me for weeks now."

Her eyebrows raise even higher than before. "Audrey, what exactly has been bothering you?"

"I...don't know how to explain it," I finally say. "But every time I try to think about it, I can't make sense of everything. Maybe you should start by talking about the fire..."

After a moment, she nods. "Alright. You may have heard about it already." I nod in response. The news is playing on repeat in the background right now. "Your family had a quite large library, right? Tons of books and papers in that place.  They kept quite a collection." I hum, trying to picture the house. "Well, papers aren't that hard to be burned. That's why the police concluded that the library was where the fire originated from." I reply, "So, there must be someone who is responsible for all that accident. There is no way the fire came out of nowhere." "The lamp" I slightly raised my eyebrows, "What?" She explains "The candle lamp, dear. Somebody forgot to put out the light.

That's why the flames started. And then the heat from them spread throughout the house until the fire got too strong for the oxygen tanks to handle. Then, boom! Everything went up in flames."

I frown, trying to piece together how all of this could have possibly gone wrong. "That does seem a little out of character." "Yes, well, there are some theories floating around, but none of them are very probable, at least none I've heard of."

Suddenly, my eyes widen. "There was something else!" I quickly grab the folder on top of my stack. "I think I read somewhere that one person ran away from the house! That person escaped through the window. We never saw him, he was just gone before we could figure out what was going on." I pause, "Did anyone follow him?"

The nurse shakes her head. "Not a soul. It wasn't reported that anyone ran, but I imagine there will be many more reports to come about his disappearance. He left behind a note though. I believe the reason he ran away was because he didn't want to get involved."

"Why wouldn't he want to get involved?"

"Maybe he felt guilty. For leaving your family behind, I suppose."

"You mean he may be responsible for all that happened ?"

"Yeah.. it could be." She answered unsurely.

I nod as I look over all of the pages that are written neatly in black pen. Suddenly, an image comes to mind, of the man in the photo. He looks vaguely familiar.

"Excuse me? Do you recognize the man in the picture?" I ask, pointing at one of the pictures that is on file. I feel my cheeks turn pink when I realize I said it out loud.

The nurse looks at where I'm pointing and frowns. "I'm afraid I don't. I'm sorry." I look back down at the page I had previously marked as important and begin reading again. This time the man in the picture gets my attention, despite what the nurse thought.

After finishing the book, I close it with a heavy heart. It's been two days since I last slept. At first I tried to stay awake all night while I researched everything I could find about the mysterious arsonist, but soon I began to run out of energy. With a groan, I set the file aside. It's a bit disappointing to find out that there's nothing new in regards to the case, but at least it' s better than nothing.

Before I fall asleep, I glance one more time at the page, hoping that one part will trigger something in me, but nothing happens. Instead, I decide to get a glass of water, and walk slowly across the dark room to the bathroom. Once there, I open the cabinet and pull out a bottle of pills. After swallowing the pill dry, I lean against the sink and stare vacantly at the mirror as I begin taking deep breaths. Soon enough, I fall asleep, still wearing my clothes from the day before, my face buried in the cool surface of the sink.

When I wake up, I immediately become aware of two things. One, I'm warm. Secondly, my throat is parched. I try to swallow, but my mouth refuses to cooperate, causing me to cough. I try to speak, but once again nothing comes out. I feel panic begin to rise in me. I can' t breathe. I try to push myself off the ground, but that only makes it worse. Panic starts bubbling in my chest as I begin hyperventilating. I manage to get up a bit, leaning heavily on the counter as I struggle to remain upright. I breathe deeply.

Calm down. Breathe. In....and out.

I continue breathing in and out. Slowly my body begins to relax a bit, although I'm still shaking uncontrollably. The burning images of the past still lingers in my mind. Mom, Dad, Lily, I wish I could save you all of you back then.

Slowly, I reach for the small sink and turn on the faucet, letting the cold water wash the remnants of the pill off of my neck. Once I finish washing my hands, I turn around and walk out of the bathroom. Then, I went out of the apartment door. In the corridor, a small voice calls out for me to stop, but I ignore it. I'm aware that I'm still not sane enough for it. The words 'hospital' ring in my ears. I keep walking.

The voices call out to me again, telling me to turn around and return inside. I turn back and saw a silhouette of a man. My heart starts to pound fast. I quickly turn around and try to run away from it but the sound of footsteps is getting louder. He chased after me, closer, closer. My breath quickens, becoming shorter.   A hand wraps around my wrist, stopping me from escaping. The world becomes blurry around the edges as tears roll down my face. The voices get closer. So close.  Too close.

Something flashes in front of my eyes. Something red. Red blood.  Blood is dripping from something. Someone? Someone's nose?  I don't care. Someone needs help, someone is dying. Who?

My vision goes white and black, and suddenly the pain disappears. Everything goes silent, and then fades into darkness.

As I open my eyes I see Dr. Jones staring at me intently.

"You okay?"

I blink slowly, attempting to focus on the doctor in front of me, but it's difficult, the memories I just remembered flooding back. "Yeah, I'm fine." I mutter under my breath, but I know he caught it.

I'm sure he did anyway.

"Do you remember anything?" he asks. I frown as I try to recall if there was something specific I noticed, but nothing comes to mind. Finally, I shrug my shoulders. "No, I guess not. All I remember was...that red color. Blood." The doctor frowns. "Are you sure that's all that you were seeing?" I give him a confused look.  "I...guess so...Why?"

He doesn't respond to that at first, apparently thinking.

"Have you seen any red stains on any of your clothing recently?" he says eventually.

I shake my head. "No."

"Are you sure?" he presses, raising an eyebrow.

This time, it' s my turn to frown. "Of course I'm sure." he stares at me for a couple more seconds, making me feel uneasy, and I'm not even sure if he's looking at me directly or not. "Then what were you feeling?" I think for a few moments, trying to remember, but I don' t know. The feeling is so vague now. I try my best to think, but my thoughts end up coming up empty.

"Nothing...like..." My mind is blank. I don't even know how I should describe the weirdness that was filling my head when I woke up. I don't even recall if anything else happened.

"Is this related to your memory problems, or did something happen while you were unconscious?" he asks, "It seems like you were really confused and scared, so maybe that's what caused it." he continues. I nod, "Maybe. But I also don't know."

He sighs, "I'll leave you alone to rest, then. Try and take care of yourself, alright?" I smile weakly and nod again. He leaves the room, closing the door softly behind him.

I lie there staring at the ceiling, wondering what I'm supposed to do next. Should I go back to sleep? No...I need to stay awake. I have no idea what will happen if I fall back asleep. I try to clear my mind, but all I can think about is the red stain I saw. What was that all about? Why was it here? What's so special about it? And the man in the corridor.. I wonder who that is..? I'm guessing he's the same man in the that picture that nurse Edna showed me.

How long has it been since I've actually seen him? I can't seem to remember.  Is this some kind of dream? Am I going insane?

I let out a shaky sigh. I don' t like having unanswered questions. They never make sense, and they're always followed by feelings of dread. When it feels that way, I usually end up spending most of the day crying. If I don't cry, I don't talk. If I don't talk, I don't sleep. If I don't sleep, I' m tired.

And today I ended up being tired for reasons unknown.

I rub my forehead gently with my fingers as I attempt to distract my anxious thoughts from the fact that I'm alone in an unfamiliar apartment with no answers.

All of sudden, I hear soft knocks coming from the main door of the apartment. I'm startling  at the noise, sitting straight up on the bed, nearly falling off as I try to calm myself down. Who would be knocking at this hour? Then all of a sudden an ear-piercing scream  echoes throughout the building, and my heart stops beating entirely.

What was that? I jump off of the bed and rush towards the door. I pull open the door and my eyes widen as I look outside. Standing beside the entrance, looking right at me, is the man I saw earlier standing in the hallway, blood splatters all over his body. His eyes are glowing bright red, and for several seconds I stand frozen with fear.

Finally, his gaze shifts toward the floor, and he growls angrily. Before I can react, however, he grabs hold of me with both arms, dragging me backward through the door. I yelp out loudly as my back hits the wood, my whole body jerking forward from the force of his grip on me. As he pulls me along, I notice a huge gaping wound in his stomach, oozing blood everywhere. I try to fight him off, but I'm too weak to do anything.

"Help...,"

"Your father.. It was all his fault!"

What does he mean? My father already long gone. He died from the fire.  I don't understand. How did this man know about my father anyway?

Just before I can figure out why exactly I am seeing things, the man shoves me backwards and I land hard on the floor, gasping for air as the wind is knocked out of my lungs. The man stands above me, glaring furiously, his eyes glistening with bloodlust. Before I can even say anything, he pounces forward and pins me beneath his weight, pinning my arms above my head with one hand. I squirm underneath him, unable to break free. Then, suddenly, I am engulfed in flames. Tears begin to fall as I recall my family burning alive in front of me. I let out another painful shriek as pain fills every inch of my body.
