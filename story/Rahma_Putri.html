21

Rahma Nabila Putri
Romance

A hectic day in a busy city, with a sea of people doing their business. Everything around me seems always cool tone color. The greyish sky was decorated with pale blue clouds pouring water anytime they want. People were busy walking with their black umbrellas, their heads low to the gray ground, the view of the pale blue skyscrapers that surrounded this little café where I worked as a part-time job between my classes at the college that I excitedly apply at that time. The white and greyish shelf is displaying the homemade bread and cake, then muted brown as the wallpaper combined with the wooden floor. One of the tables was placed near the big window, from where people could see the view of the city and the people walking by, and that’s my favorite spot to enjoy the warm coffee as I waited for my shift, mostly spending my time thereby trying to finish some work, or sometimes just stared at the sky and thinking about which cheap ramen noodles to eat today as my dinner, my attempt to save some money. It was very different from the time when I was 15. It was sunshine and rainbows. I didn’t have to be worried about food, clothes, and life. Until I realized that the reality that I have to face is not as exciting and free as what it is pictured by those actors who’d wearing fancy and flashy outfits- which is something I couldn’t afford at all now, in those movies on the TV screen. I was too optimistic, which ended up hurting my expectation. And no one tells me the truth, not even my mom.

“Mom, why do I have to wear this boring and ugly uniform all the time?! I want to wear something pretty and colorful too.”

There was a moment of silence before my mom laugh softly at my question.

“Oh dear, of course, you can wear pretty clothes! That’s why you have to study harder to be able to study at a good college.” I look at her with eyes full of hope and nod my head. “I promise, I will!” I said without any doubt.

Naïve. It’s not just like what I imagined from the whole 3 years in high school. The thoughts of having the freedom of choosing what dress or skirt or anything I wanted to wear to the class- not the boring and ugly uniform. Having new friends with the new people who are excited to know about the story of my life, and eating lunch together with them in the cafeteria. The thoughts of having to visit the cool places, and museums just to get inspiration for the given project, or even having to search books for a specific class in the national library, taking some good pictures to post them on my social media accounts to remember those memories in a special place with people who I will call them as ‘friends’. I kept playing those fake but-at least makes me happy scenarios in my head over and over, like a broken DVD player. At that time, those were the only escape from the hellish reality in high school that I could do in my silly 17 years old, the freedom of college life was the only thing that kept me half alive. Meanwhile, half of me was already numb, just like on that day when I had my nose bleeding.

“Oh god, Nadine! Your nose is bleeding again! We should go to the school’s clinic this time!”  She worriedly grabbed my right hand, strong enough to make my body stand up from my seat. But I yanked her hand away and stared angrily at her.

“NO! I CAN’T LEAVE, NOT UNTIL I FINISH READING THIS BOOK, THANIA! JUST PLEASE LEAVE ME ALONE!”

After saying that, my body automatically sat down, glued to the old chair. I swapped away the blood with my sleeves until I think the blood stops coming out.

“You! What a crazy witch. Fine, I’ll go as you ask. But remember, you’re gonna ruin your own life and go mental in the future.” She said firmly before tidying up her things and moving to another seat in the back row, far from me. I could feel her cold stare stabbing my back. I lost the only person I have at that time.

Thania was right, she always and has been right. I’m the one who stacked my life with a bunch of false hopes that lied to me. I have to accept a lie after living for 19 years of life in this ruthless world. I realized that I hurt myself even more because of my expectations. Even though, not all of them were lies. Where I did use the freedom to dress up, I met a lot of new people with diverse backgrounds and personalities and went to the museums. But I couldn’t feel the happiness that I have been dreamt of. All of those slowly turned into something even more burdensome, for me. The fear of people judging me is haunted my mind. I even talked to. Meanwhile, the rest of them were most likely only talking when they have something really necessary to talk about. I had no one to talk to about life. But that’s not their fault, it’s me. “I know. I’m the problem.” it was my last word before falling further into the cold and dark pit, alone. I came to the point when living was too tiring, but dying was too scary.

When most college students searched for a part-time job, I’m the opposite. All I want to do was to focus on my study and be able to finish college and find a job with a great salary. I know, it sounds monotone. I just wanna have a normal life, just like what my mom said. But once again, it seems like God has another plan. In just a blink of my eyes, the hospital and white room with the smell of medicine, and the sound of the medical devices that support the patient life, have become a very familiar view, aside from my bedroom and the classroom. The patient in that room is my father. The situation become even more serious when the nurse announced that my father was suffering from a rare condition, he need numerous bags of blood to save his life. That’s it.

“Nadine, I have to tell you something…I know you must be still in shock, aren’t you?”

Her voice sounds painful and hoarse, like someone that has been crying for hours but still trying to comfort me when she isn’t in any better condition.

“Your father… needs bags of blood and further examination about his condition. All of them cost us more than our life budget, sweetheart…” That’s all I heard, before the rest of her words just turn into a sound of static.

Lonely. Tired. Painful. There wasn’t any option left, except to be a part-timer in this little café- another safe place beside my bed, in the middle of the city. In this place, no one talks to each other. People are busy with their businesses. The conversation with everyone that I ever had never lasted long, usually only a simple good afternoon or how’s your day, or a casual question about the order, and that’s all. Not until I met him, a very tall guy, with tan skin which made him look even more stand out in the sea of people. His dark-brown hair looks extremely soft as the wind blows them away. When he smiles, you can see a pair of a tooth that looks like a fang, making him look unreal. He looks like a dream. I didn’t aware of his presence until the day he talk to me during my shift. The day when everything is changed.

“Hello, how was your day, sir? And what do you want to order?” I said while slowly raising my head, ready to greet the person in front of me. And that’s when my eyes met his eyes. The eyes are full of warmth and hope.

We just stand there, feeling like we’d been cast by medusa and turned into statues, we stared at each other. “Uhm…excuse me, sir?” trying to break the awkward silence.

“Ah- I’m sorry, I want a cup of black coffee and one slice of cheesecake, please.” He shyly scratched the nape of his neck.

“It’s okay, sir.” I couldn’t help to smile as well just by looking at him. “Do you want to eat here or make it as a take-out?”

He paused for a while, before replying. “I think I’ll start enjoying them in here from today.” He said as he put one hand on the counter table and smiled gently at me.

I learned his name from that day, Matthew. It was the longest conversation I ever had; when we talked it feels like the world stop moving. It sounds cliché, but it’s the truth! I can’t help to steal some glances at him from the back of the counter as I’m serving other people. I was mesmerized, but never had any thought that our interaction from that day could turn into something more than a customer and waitress. And he keeps his promise, the promise to always eat or simply drink his black coffee in here, only at my shift. I don’t know why, but it feels like the burdens on my shoulders are slightly gone as we constantly met. I think it’s not the Medusa who cast the spell on me, but Matthew.

I learned a lot about him as we talk in the middle of his order. He’s 3 years older than me. Matthew isn’t not originally from here, he had to move here because his work required him to do it, and there might be a chance that he’ll get transferred to another city- which I hope he didn’t have to. Until one day he didn’t come. I thought maybe he was busy that day, so he couldn’t come or he already found another café with better a menu. But I’ve always waited for him, hoping for him to come, even only for a second. Then, it’s been 5 days since the last day he come. I tried to brush away my hopes, didn’t want to get another false hope. But this time it seems God feels pity for me. He answers my prayer because Matthew is here! He brings a bucket of pink roses. This makes me feel kinda envious of whoever will get those roses. But what can I do? I’m just somebody for him, right? So, I calmed myself, tried to act normal, and greet him.

“Hi! It’s been a long time since I see you come here. Is everything alright?” My mouth just slips away, which makes me sound like I’m here only to see him.

He looked shocked but happy as he replied to me with a smile.

“Hello! I was busy these days, I’m sorry I couldn’t visit…I miss you though” His eyes shoot open after realizing what he just said, but quickly added, “I mean I miss your coffee!”I was shocked, then I can feel butterflies start flying around in my belly.

“Uh…It’s okay, you don’t have to apologize to me, I-I’m just glad that you’re okay.” as my mouth struggle to make a clear sentence. “So, are you gonna order the usual?” I tried to hide my nerve by pretending to use the tablet menu.

“Yes, and can I talk to you after your shift, Nadine?” He replied immediately.  

“Alright, a cup of black coffee and a slice of cheesecake will come to your table, sir,” I replied. “Oh, sure! But my shift end pretty late, is that okay?”

“It’s okay. I can wait anytime for you.” then he walked away.

        I flipped the open sign into the close. We talked, and he asked me. No, he’s not asking me to date him, he asks me if I already had my dinner or not. If not, he wished to have me come over to his place and have dinner with him as he offers the bucket of pink roses to me. Matthew said that this is the only way to say thank you to me, simply because I made his days better just by seeing my smile while serving other people. “You look like an angel when you smile at people- someone you didn’t know. The smile that could save someone ‘s life without you realizing.” I didn’t believe him at first, but when I see his red face and the seriousness in his eyes, I can’t refuse. I wasn’t regretting my decision. His place is very neat, simple, and warm.

From that day Matthew and I started hanging out after my shift ends, he’ll come over and wait patiently. He’s always there, beside me. Even when one day I got a call from my mom telling me that my father’s condition became worsened, he was there. Give me a ride to the hospital and still tried to comfort me while driving his car. He’s always been a gentleman. Watching all of the things that happened to me. Unconsciously, Matthew has become a part of my life. The most exciting part of my life is spending my time with him, feels like part of my worries are gone. We go everywhere we can in his car from the park, river, beach or any restaurant we see to buy some food before going back to his place and eating them together. Giving the food reviews and stars, like what the judges do in those cooking competitions. Childish, but I love it. I love him. He often has to teach me how to play a game on his console, only for me to beg defeated by him in a second. We always love to have small chats before sleep about anything. One time, we exchange stories about our life. Matthew’s life is just perfect, born into a pretty wealthy family. Nothing really worried him. When it was my turn to tell my stories, he couldn’t say anything but just hug me tightly in the end, as he said, “How painful must it have been…I’m sorry, Nadine…I’m sorry.” he said as if it were his fault.

As his place has become my second home, our relationship didn’t have specific c status. Sometimes we are a lover, friends, or enemies, we can be anything we need. Talking about bedtime, Matthew gives his bed to me and he would likely sleep on the sofa in the living room. “I just didn’t like sleeping with the pillow, they hurt my neck!” his excuse every time I ask him to switch places with me. Not until one night, he’s his acting was different than the usual. I could see his sad face between his smile. I kept asking him, wanting to know about his day. But he just replied with a question and promise. “Can I sleep in bed with you only for tonight, Nadine? I promise I’m not gonna do anything weird to you.” He replied with a smile. I know that he was faking his smile.

        I know he has been hiding something from me. But I didn’t have any courage to ask him, didn’t want to know the bad news. I didn’t want to lose him. That night, was the first time we slept together in his bed. The way Matthew looked that night, his eyes closed heavy, hair soft after I had to blow-dry it for him. In his sleep, he was constantly trying to tuck his warm arms between my smaller body, bring me closer to him, which always ran hot, his body always radiating a comfortable warmth. He tried to get away with it by giving one of his gentle smiles, which always makes my heart clench with an onslaught of overwhelming fondness and emotions. That was the last moment of him before he was completely gone from my sight. Leaving me with an empty and cold bed, and a note, saying how happy and grateful he was for having a chance to spend his precious time with me. How I inspired him to be strong, hoping he can stay forever beside me, seeing my face in the morning beside him.

Nadine. I’m sorry for leaving you so suddenly. I was trying to tell you about my I’m not ready to say goodbye. I wish I’m brave enough just like you, angel. You deserve someone who will do everything to keep you happy, and I wish I can be that someone. And if you see this note, I hope you know that I’ll miss you, I will. Let’s meet in another series of twenties, okay? See you.

                                                                                                                 Matthew.

It was the rainy season and I was in my 21 when I met him for the first time and summer comes in when left. He, a stranger who entirely changed how I live my life, has turned into someone I love that changed the way I see the world. Now, I can face the world sincerely. As someone who never expected anything special would happen in my life, his presence is truly a gift. My life has been filled with a sequence of unlucky things, which makes me didn’t realize that there were a lot of little things that could make me happy. Not until I met him in the lowest state of my life when the whole world seemed so dark. So, Matthew Koch, thank you so much for finding me. You’ll be always in my remembrance and I hope there’s a little part of your memories about me too. Let’s meet at 25? 26? I’ll wait.
