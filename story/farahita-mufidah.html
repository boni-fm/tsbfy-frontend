Acceptance

Farahita Mufidah Artanti
Drama

“Ugh! this water is awful,” Jelly complained.

“Don’t worry, Fara’s dad will change the water any second now,” Bitti consoled her.

“Ours fortunately not that bad,'' said Boba, half teasing after coming down to the first porch where their water was.

“Let me try!” Said Shin while coming down the first porch. However, Shin, who was coming down from the third porch purposely, bumped Joy’s body on the way.

“Watch your step. Big guy!” Said Shin. Boba and Nugget were just laughing at what Shin had just done to Joy. Joy, on the other hand, couldn't do anything because he was outnumbered and just accepted it calmly.

“When is he gonna show up? It’s almost 5.30,” said Poko as if she could tell the time.

Right after Poko nagged, the balcony door—which separated Fara and her family’s house and their place—was opened by Fara’s dad. Her dad is willingly in charge of preparing their breakfast. He also water the plant in the morning, except when he had to go to work earlier, he would tell Fara to take over those things. It was not that Fara was lazy. She was just really difficult to wake.

“I bet he’s going to get the fancy pink bowl again,” snapped Boba to himself loudly. Everyone knows he was referring to Joy, who always gave the single-pink bowl.

“Oh my God, cut it off, Boba! Everyone knows you guys don’t want to eat with him. That is why Fara and his dad always gave Joy the pink bowl. It is the only single bowl in the house,” yelled Pudding to everyone who heard her.

She might be small compared to everyone and a little bit lumpy because of her childhood injury. In personality, she was furious but kind. While they were debating, dad prepared their food, put it into their bowls.

“Here you go, guys!'' The bowls were handed out. Poko and Jelly ate together in a green double bowl. Bitti ate with Pudding in the same kind of bowl as the one Poko and Jelly used. On the other side of the square, Shin and Boba also got matched in the green double bowl. Nugget got the blue double bowl all for himself. Unfortunately he also didn’t want to eat with Joy. And lastly, Joy got his usual single-pink bowl. They eat voraciously, even though they just had their dinner at 11 p.m. yesterday.

 

The sun was at its highest place, and everyone was getting dizzy because of the heat. Poko started to run around in front of the door while waiting for Fara to open it up.

“I bet that lazy ass human overslept again and forgot about us!” She complained.

“It’s not that horrible here, you know? We still have our water and toilet. We’ll survive staying here for today,” said Nugget, who happened to be a potato couch among all of them. He had a cute mushy tummy and sleepy face.

“Nu-uh, I need to get my freedom again!” Poko replied. As soon as she countered Nugget’s opinion, the balcony’s door that separated their place and Fara’s house opened, Fara came out.

“Hi, my babies!” Fara greeted all of them. Everyone got excited and jumped around to see her, including Nugget, who had just said he likes being inside.

“Fara! Do you see my shining body? I just bathed myself a while ago. Tell me I am pretty!” Jelly screamed, flashing her body to Fara in front of the door.

“Hi, Fara!” Joy excited and replied with growls from Nugget, Shin, and Boba, even though Boba was one inch close to slapping Joy for being too close to him.

“I’m sorry, I was finishing my assignment earlier.” Fara apologized while opening the door of their see-through place. Everybody gushed out of their place to the balcony and entered Fara’s house. But Joy was left behind. Fara saw Joy walking slowly. She caressed his head and scratched his chin.

“Joy, how are you today?” said Fara. Joy then stopped walking for his scratches, and Fara picked him up off the ground and cuddled him.

“My baby…” mumbled Fara while carrying Joy in her embrace into the house. 

Fara walked into the house and used the stairs to get down to the first floor. Everyone was already running around everywhere. Pudding and Nugget were drinking from the pond. Poko was waiting in front of the refrigerator with a little hope that she could get fried fish which Fara’s mom usually prepared for everyone. Jelly was chasing Bitti in the foyer while Boba and Shin bathed themselves on the living room sofa. Fara then reached the front of the house intending to open the door so everybody could play outside and make some friends with others. Fara put Joy down from her embrace and let him play outside, followed by Poko, Jelly, Shin, and Boba. Nugget, Pudding, and Bitti were more comfortable playing inside the house.

The ones who went outside usually would play at the nearest alley from the residence. Joy would visit his friend, named Oreo. Oreo was an oldy who had lived in this housing complex for over five years. If you may ask why he wanted to be friends with Joy, the answer is so simple: he didn't care about everything; he just lived for eating and sleeping. It was explained by his large body. He used to have a home close to the front gate, but up until now, he lived anywhere he wanted as long as that place had a roof to protect him whenever it rained.

“My man, Joy!” Oreo greeted Joy

“Hey Oreo, how’s everything around here?”

“Well, same old, how’s everything at your place?”

“Not much. Everybody still hates me even after eight months living together. It’s just that. I don’t even do anything wrong. I eat and sleep like everybody else. I am not trying to invade their territory and fight with them. I just want a home where I can be protected from hunger and evil humans.”

“I got you, man. Not everybody could accept a change in their life. You’re so similar to their late brother, Bubble. Maybe they thought you were trying to replace him. Let me give you some advice, seeing how things are going in your home. I think you should leave the house for a couple days, you know? Just to clear your mind of the bullying they have done. To be honest, your house isn’t even that different from the outside world, both stress you out. You still can find a roof to sleep at night, and for the food, you can go to a diner or something. They sometimes have leftover fish and chicken. I know you probably worry about this idea but look at me. I am fine with being feral. I am well fed and always have a place to sleep.”

“Tch…you don’t say, you live here! This housing complex is very friendly for our kind but not the outside world, your example is not entirely valid.”

“Well, my man, I am just giving you advice, you could take it or leave it. Besides, it is not that you’re gone forever, think of it as a vacation to be far away from your stepbrothers”.

Joy got in silence for a while after his chat with Oreo. He thought about Oreo’s advice deeply and measured the pros and cons of the idea. He did know that the outside world was not as good as Oreo said, because he had been there. Starved on the side of the street and sleeping with an empty stomach. Until he met Fara’s family, he was also lucky enough to have the same feature as Bubble, who passed away last year due to urinary infections. He then decided to leave the thought for a while, and enjoy the rest of his playtime hanging out with Oreo.

It was almost twilight, that meant their play-time was over. Fara collected Pudding, Bitti, and Nugget were playing inside the house to be put in their place again for dinner and nap time. All of them are not very pleased with this idea. They like their freedom outside. However, the house needs to be clean, and they only make it dirty if they couldn't gather in their place. After putting Pudding, Bitti, and Nugget, Fara continued her search for the others outside the house. Boba and Shin are already waiting on the other side of the door for Fara to open it. After the front door opened, they ran inside the house and went to the balcony on the second floor. The remains, Poko, Jelly, and Joy were still playing outside, so Fara needed to search for them individually. Fara collected Poko and Jelly after seeing them laid in her aunt’s front yard, which happened to be Fara’s front neighbor.

On the other hand, Joy was still thinking about Oreo’s advice even though the playtime was over. He saw Fara walking out of her home to collect Poko and Jelly, and he got indecisive. He wanted to go to Fara so she could collect him at their place, but he also got afraid as he would be in the same place as his bully. He then made up his mind. “It’ll be okay, it’s just going to be a couple of days, and I will be at the house again soon. Like Oreo said, think of it as a vacation from the bullies,” he said, deciding. He hides at the nearest house so that Fara wouldn’t find him. When Fara was collecting Poko and Jelly, he scrammed hard to go outside the housing complex so that Fara couldn’t find him and collected him.

And Joy was right. After collecting Poko and Jelly to their place, Fara came back outside her house to find Joy, but he was nowhere to be found. She checked the place where Oreo used to hang out since Joy oftenly there, but there was no sign of Joy. Fara then comes home and cleans the house before her parents from work. Fara thought Joy wanted extra play time and went outside of the housing complex. However, until the night came, Joy still had not come home yet. Fara then told her parents that Joy was still outside and asked her dad to look for him around the Mosque, inside the housing complex, when he went there to pray.

Being outside again after eight months of living in Fara’s house-made Joy thrilled. He had this strange excitement of seeing the outside world again but also felt a bit scared of how this turned out. He went to the alley he used to live in back then before meeting Fara and found the nearest diner for his dinner. Some were already closed because it was night time. He decided to find another source of food, which was the street vendor that usually opens at night and sells fish or chicken. The street was pretty packed today, people went out using their vehicles, and Joy felt chickened out again. He was afraid of being hit by the vehicles. He convinced himself again, “You got this, Joy, you’ve been in the street for so long, a couple of days on the outside world won’t hurt you,” but he was partially wrong. He even couldn’t get himself any food. And since he wears the red choker Fara gave him, people became a little bit skeptical to give him food considering it was owned by someone else. Therefore, they didn’t have the right to give him some food. He was starving and a little bit tired from the long walk from the housing complex to the place where the street vendors are. Then he suddenly remembered the person who usually gave him food when he was still feral. He then walked again to the first alley and searched for the person. He went to his house and called the person a little bit hard so that he could hear him considering his front door was closed. One call, second call, third call... no sign of the door opening, and Joy began to lose hope. He then waited on the person’s house porch because he couldn’t think anywhere safer than here.

Joy was half asleep when he heard someone approaching the porch. He opened his eyes widely to see clearly who this person was. It was him, the person who often fed Joy when he was still feral. The person was shocked to see Joy in front of his house again, let alone with the fact that Joy was wearing a red choker.

“Hi! Oh my god, it has been so long since you were here!” He greeted Joy. Joy then replied, “yes! I am hungry, so hungry! Do you have any leftovers or some pre-made food for me?” asked Joy, screaming.

“Wow, you got a necklace right there, are you lost?” said the person while stroking Joy’s head. Joy continued screaming with the same words he said before, and the person finally realized that Joy was hungry. He then came to his house and brought out some pre-made food in the bowl and some water for Joy to drink.

“Your owner must be very sad knowing you were here,” said the person while watching Joy eating his pre-made food.

“Why did you come back here? You know I couldn’t keep you. The landlord hates you and your kind. I also couldn’t feed you well because business is not working out lately,” he said. “You must go back to your owner. I bet they love you very much. They even bother to get you this pretty necklace”. Joy was eating in silence because he was so tired, starving, and thirsty.

Joy spent his vacation day around the person’s house to make sure he was well-fed and had a place to sleep. Even though the person always asks Joy to go back to his owner, Joy didn't listen. He would go back to Fara’s house, but his vacation wasn't over yet. He then filled his vacation days by acquainting with the other residents there, and he also met some girls. There are so many new friends that Joy met, but little did he know one of them brought some fungi that later infected Joy.

By the time Joy came back to Fara’s house, his body was so dirty, and his ears were crinkly because of the fungal infection he got on his vacation. Fara was very sad and immediately bathed him. “How could you have ended up like this? Where were you for the past three days?” said Fara sadly while bathing him. “Oh, don’t you worry, My Lady, I had the best vacation. I met new friends and met some girls,” replied Joy. Fara continued to bath him and realized this fungal infection couldn’t be healed by just bathing him. He needed to see the doctor. So, Fara brought Joy to the doctor, and it turned out that crinkly ears were scabies. He then got scabies shot and advised to be separated from the others until he healed completely.

Everybody was so jealous of him, especially his bullies. They thought Fara treated him as a special kid. Oh boy, if they knew the truth about why Joy was outside their regular place, they would thank Fara. Joy was glad to live inside Fara’s house until he recovered. He could spend time with Fara and get treats sometimes. He realized that even though others couldn’t accept his presence in this house, he still will be loved by Fara’s family no matter what. He didn’t need the acceptance and recognition from the others. Only Fara’s family and she are more than enough.

So, when Joy was moved again to their regular place, he wasn’t going to be stressed all over again. He just tried to embrace their spite and consider it something to let go. If they tried to hit him, he just had to fight back and stand strong. Joy belonged here, he was chosen, and he chose to be here whatever the circumstances. He knew they would never change their attitudes towards him, and he was fine with that as long as Fara’s family and her still loved him and were with him.
