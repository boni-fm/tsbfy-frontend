Crown of The Sea

Arvin Novyan Benardi
Fantasy

Gemini is the only heir of the Underwater Kingdom of the South. His father said he would one day become the King, succeeding his father, King Namor. As the only heir, he knows the succession where he rose to become King is still years away. He pleads to his father, where he goes on a journey to prove himself to be worthy.

His father assures him there is no need for him to embark from the Kingdom after he steps down. He will still take place in the Kingdom, where he still oversees the Kingdom until Gemini is ready to make his own decision. Like much of his stubbornness, Gemini says he will take on the journey for at least three years to prove himself worthy of holding the title King.

—

“...and that’s pretty much it,” says Gemini. The night is pretty calm for the party as they recuperate after the battle in the cave. They barely came out alive. Ravus, the party leader, is honing his bloody sword. Josselyn, as a Warlock, is resting herself after lending too much power on behalf of her fire spirit. Julian is listening to the story of Gemini. Ophiucus, as a bird humanoid, tends to his broken wings.

“So, in summary, you’re a prince in one of the Sea Kingdom, and you decided to go on a journey just to make sure you are worthy?” says Julian.

“To be honest, I was stubborn about it. My father said, I can learn as I go, but I am not ready. It was a long way, to be sure, but this could be my last year on the journey,” says Gemini. “One thing that happened in this journey was this healing power I have. I must thank Apollo.”

The forest’s sign of life is only the bonfire the party created. From the distance of the moon above their head signified it just barely past midnight. The situation of the party is not great. All of them are wounded to some degree, from broken wings to bleeding. Gemini is helping as much as he can with his power to prevent further injuries. They need to come to the nearest church or temple for proper treatment.

“Now, where is that guy? He told us to wait here,” says Gemini. “Scouting the area shouldn’t be this long with many people joining him.”

Gemini looks at the party once again and sees they are now in a better state. “If he is still taking a long time, I will be down by the river. Tell me if we are ready to move.”

The river is not too far from where they camp. Gemini arrives after a short walk to the small river flow. As Gemini looks down, moonlight shines on the river revealing his own reflection. His red hair is messy, and his face is covered with blue bruises darker than his bluish skin tones. The one that is fine from the battle is his ear fins, a feature of Tritons, people of the Sea.

Gemini sits and reaches out his hand into the flowing water. The power he always held, and he is certain was given by the waves, is to communicate with his people through the flowing water. As long as the water flows to the sea, he can speak through it or sees his own memory.

Gemini’s eyes are hollow, knowing the effort to communicate through the waves to his home is futile. It’s been almost half a year since the communication was not going through. The only thing he is able to see is his own memory. The memories he can see are from the core memory he always holds dearly to him.

—

Gemini looks at the kingdom he lived in for one last time. It was his idea to embark on a journey, and he is now ready to leave. He already accepts not being able to see his parents or even home for how long? He doesn’t know neither.

Before him, he sees his mother standing at the front gate. It is unusual for his mother, as a Queen, to steps outside the kingdom. Gemini steps forward to approach her. He can see a necklace with a half-crowned pendant in his mother's hand.

“Gemini,” says his mother. “I know this is only temporary. May this farewell not be the last time we talked.” Then she hands the necklace to Gemini.

“The pendant is a symbol of your identity. Royal Blood, but not yet ready to fulfil the destiny as one. Protect it with your life. Once you come back, I will hand the other half of the pendant.”

—

That’s the only thing he remembers from that day and can be shown through the waves. Nothing new. No matter how hard he tries to communicate, it won’t come through to his family far away.

As Gemini is ready to leave the water, the atmosphere suddenly changes. The atmosphere of an empty forest suddenly changed to a cold one. It feels like under the sea. Gemini took a deep breath and closed his eyes. There is no way he moved into the sea by dipping his hand into the river. He opens his eyes. The vision of a forest is completely gone, replaced by the vision of rubbles and corals, the underwater.

Gemini draws his mace from his hip and readies his shield. From the point he is standing right now, he can see many people, probably minions, and someone summoned at the end of his view.

‘Can’t be caught, too many of them to fight one by one. I’m not near the forest. Someone must have pulled me here. That means I’m alone here.”

Gemini moves from one rubble to another and arrives at the front gate. He knows it so well, his kingdom. Once stand in glory, now what he sees before he is the remains of its glory. The front gate suffers minor damages, few holes and structures are broken. But beyond that, it’s almost beyond recognition.

The buildings are now one with the dust. Houses, taverns, and markets are all gone. Gemini can’t believe his eyes. It’s not a pleasant view to see. It might be real or not since someone pulled him here.

The place is already overrun by minions. They look like half-human and half-fish. Whatever they are guarding must be important. Could someone that is commanding the destruction of this place? In his life, Gemini doesn’t know if his father has many enemies, nor does he know his allies. His father never let Gemini accompany him in political meetings with people throughout the sea.

Avoiding remnants of buildings is hard. Make a wrong step, and it will alert at least anyone nearby. It’s not helping Gemini a favour. He’s also wearing metal armors. He takes his steps through the path where it was emptier, through the back of the market east, where it has a shortest way to the castle.

Gemini’s is at a loss with the situation of the market now. Nothing is left there. Only remains of once trade activity in the kingdom. There’s nothing much he can do while affected by Waves magic. What he can do now is only to find the source of the person who pulled him there.

—

After walked through the path, Gemini is now in front of the castle gate. No minions nearby guarding the gate. With no hesitation, he enters the gate. Gemini is now in the field leading to the castle. No signs of life anywhere still, but the damage of the castle is severe.

Gemini slowly walked the path. Rubbles of broken statues are all over the path, making it look like a ruin. Place where Gemini once lived and grew up is now miserable. Once stood strong it now only rubbles, a shadow of its former glory. Gemini stops for a moment to look at the castle. It is sad to look at it.

"I wonder if I didn’t leave, will it still be here? Is it my fault not protecting this place?" He has gone for so long and not knowing dangers as he is too far from home.

While looking at the castle, Gemini heard a movement in the rubbles. He looks around, and no one is there. He then moves his gaze back on the castle and notices a figure standing in front of it now. The figure has a sword sheathed in his hip and shield strapped in the back. Gemini readies his shield, bracing for an attack. The figure stood there, not moving an inch. It is directly looking at Gemini.

“Who are you?!” Gemini shouts, but he gets no answer. “If you are the one who dragged me here, identify yourself!”

In the speed of a blink, the figure leaps forward and charges at Gemini with his sword. The slash of the sword is slower than their speed. It is dealing a blow to Gemini’s shield, forcing him aback.

“Heh, surprised you can block my attack,” says the figure.

Gemini pants, the strength of the swing definitely caught him off-guard. If he did not ready to block back, he would have been injured.

“Our fight is not now. I merely want to talk.”

“I don’t buy it! Identify yourself!” Gemini shouted.

“Got the spirit of your father, huh. You are truly the Crown Prince of this kingdom.”

Gemini readies his shield again in case of the following attack. The figure then walks to Gemini and lowers his shield. Assuring there is no more attack.

“I’ve been waiting for you to use the Waves.”

“What do you want from me?” Gemini asked.

“To know you, the last Triton of this Kingdom, you may not know me, but our paths will cross again, Gemini.”

“How do you know my name?”

“I know your family, your father, to be specific. I am… acquaintance at the very least.”

“Once again, I ask, what do you want?”

“Straight to the point, huh? You have something that I need.” The figure then points the sword to Gemini’s pendant. In reflex, Gemini stands back and clutches the pendant of the necklace.

“You only seem to have half of it. I will be waiting for it to return fully here.” The figure sheathed his sword back. “We will meet again, Gemini. Our destiny was to fight here to the death.”

“Oh, and one last thing, all of these are real. I can’t wait for you to see it in person.” The figure said. He raises his hand, and there are waves around his hand. He unleashes it, and Gemini’s vision turns to black.

—

Gemini seems to be back in the forest. The figure he met there said that the view was real. The kingdom is destroyed, and it seems no one is left there. Whether it’s true or not, he needs to see it.

‘Maybe not now, after we are all back in a better state. We must go there. It is my destiny, he said.’ Gemini thought.

Gemini comes back to the party, and the person they waited for, named Cecillion, comes back. There is no sign of battle in his armor. His helmet is still intact.

“No signs of enemies. We can start moving to the nearby temple by sea. Approximately two to three hours from here,” says Cecillion.

“That’s good, then.” Ravus says to him. He turned around and saw Gemini already back. “There you are! You have been gone for a while by now.”

“You looked pale. Are you alright?” Cecillion asks him behind his helmet. “You can join the second cart with the others who are sick.”

“I’m alright. Just tired,” says Gemini. “Just encountered something. I will tell you all later. Nothing dangerous… yet.”

That maybe will confuse them, but they only need to rest for now. The problem Gemini and the party will encounter comes later. Whatever that figure wants with Gemini seems important and could put all of them in danger. It involves the pendant to return, but it’s only half of it. Gemini needs the other half before anyone finds it. If it means to fight to his death, then so be it.
