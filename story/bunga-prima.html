Of Love And Hope

Bunga Prima Niarti
Romance

        It is the nineteenth century in the countryside of England, where a young beautiful girl of twenty two named Helena Collins lives with her family. She is the first daughter of Mr. Henry Collins and Mrs. Susan Collins, and the twelve-year-old Olivia Collins is the only one sister of her. On this bright summer morning, Helena and Olivia look very jolly while they are playing with their lovely dogs. A couple of golden retrievers seem very enthusiastic to catch the balls that Olivia throws in the yard. They will pursue, catch the ball by their mouth, and give it back to her. While Olivia and two golden retrievers happily run around, Helena just sits on the white swing and watches them. She has a pale complexion with long golden brown hair. She lets her long hair hang loose with some of the left and right side tied in the back. Light gray is her eyes color. It is round and filled with long and curling eyelashes. Smiles and laughs come now and then in her lips when she looks into her sister or to the two little puppies that sit on her lap. They are very adorable and this cuteness can’t make her stop caressing them. Every once in a while she will kiss them. It pleases her heart even more.

This makes Helena think that the house where she was born and lives in has provided much warmth and happiness for her. But in fact, there are some reasons that make her want to flee away from here. Sometimes this place is really suffocating her until she thinks that it is better for her to not live in this house. Helena’s father, Mr. Henry Collins is someone who is prone to outbursts of rage when his mood is not really good. He is a short-tempered and authoritative man. When something doesn't suit him, he will be so upset. On the contrary, in this house, no one dares to criticize him or they will get scolded back. Helena truly hates this kind of behavior of her father. She has been through something like this all her life. To be sure, her mother has experienced this much longer than her.

Helena realizes that her mother, Mrs. Susan Collins, is the most patient and indulgent person in the world. She is able to endure to always stay by her husband's side. Mrs. Collins always excuses Mr. Collin’s behavior, and she will say to her daughters ‘that is how your Papa is, my dearest’. Helena believes that she doesn’t completely take after her mother. Of course her father’s blood runs through her body as well. That is why she is not a truly patient and tender girl like Mrs. Collins. She can not blame anyone for this reason. After all, Mr. Collins still has much affection for them. He is a responsible man as well. Helena loves her father very much since he is actually a kind and loving person. But when he turns to show his temper, it is the worst for her and she doesn't like this at all. It makes her suffocate and she is really sick of this kind of situation. She is afraid that this hate feeling will always come for such a situation. He is still her father and she won’t hate him at all. She wishes that this bad behavior of her father will disappear, but perhaps it is impossible. By all the moments Helena experienced until now, it makes her realize that someday she only wants to meet a man who is very kind, gentle, and affectionate, without a temper like her father.  

When Helena is still in her own thoughts while caressing the two puppies, she catches sight of her mother and Aunt Martha, the housekeeper, who come closer to her.

“Helena, Mrs. Tinsley said that Mr. Davies is here. He has just arrived at his house.” Aunt Martha informs her enthusiastically. Knowing this, Helena’s eyes start to gleam.

“Don’t you want to see him, Helen?” Her mother asks her while taking one of the puppies from Helena’s lap. She hugs and kisses it.

“Really? It’s been a while.” Helena responses. She is standing and carrying the puppy by her arms.

William Davies is an old friend of Helena. His father is Scottish but his mother is English. When he was seventeen, he and his family moved to England. At that time, Helena was only twelve. William Davies has a younger brother named Bryan Davies. He is two years younger than William. They three then became close friends since they lived nearby. However, Helena was closer to William as slowly she began to love him. William is a handsome man who is very loving and gentle. Helena really loves how this man treats her. She sees William as the man she has been looking for since he is not a man with a short-temper like her father. He is very kind, calm, and affectionate. And Bryan is actually the same.

Not only Helena, William actually also has the same feeling for her. However, for some reason, their relationship was only a couple of friends who are caring and loving each other. Helena is a girl so she thinks that it is not appropriate to express her own feelings to the man she loves directly. And for William, he never says that he loves Helena. Somehow they keep their feelings very well. Unfortunately, while their relationship grows closer, when William was twenty two, William and all his family had to move back to Scotland because his grandfather died. The house here is still inhabited by the maid, Mrs. Tinsley. During four years after their move, it is only William who comes to England and visits Helena for twice or more a year. He also often sends her a letter. However, for more than one year, William never comes. So when Helena knows that he comes to visit, she feels very happy. Then Helena, accompanied by Olivia, comes to his old house.

They walk down the street until they arrive at a large house. This house is dominated by brick red with many large white windows. It is surrounded by a low iron fence. In the entrance, there is a path straight to the house in which on the left and right side is planted with many beautiful roses. Helena believes that Mrs. Tinsley takes good care of this house very much. The garden looks very fancy since it is decorated by many beautiful flowers. She always reminisces about her old memories with William and Bryan when she comes to this house. When they enter the backyard, Helena and Olivia see a woman and a man who are standing back to them. Helena guesses that they are Mrs. Tinsley and William. Then they approach them.

“Mrs. Tinsley… William…” Helena greets them. Then they turn around.

“Helena…” The man in front of her is smiling. But the smile on Helena’s lips slowly faded. It is not William.

“U-uh… S-sorry. You… you…” She stammers since it is a bit awkward. Helena tilts her head. She feels familiar with this face.

“Bryan/Bryan” Helena and Bryan exclaim together. Then they hug each other.

“It’s been a long time, Helen.” Bryan mumbles behind her shoulder. Then they break the embrace.

“Bryan, you change a lot.” Helena stares at his face.

“So do you, Helena. You have grown up and look very lovely.” Helena just chuckles lightly hearing him.

“Sorry, Bryan. I thought it was William, but it is actually you, Bryan Davies. I don’t expect it at all.” Bryan just smiles.

“Oh Mrs. Tinsley, Aunt Martha didn’t tell me that it was Bryan who came to visit. She only said that Mr. Davies had arrived. So I guessed that it was William since he is the one who often comes to this house.” Helena explains eagerly while holding Mrs. Tinsley’s hands.

“My dear. You really miss him, don’t you?” Mrs. Tinsley stares and smiles at her tenderly.

“Of course. It’s been a while since he came here.” Helena answers her.

“Who is this little girl?” Bryan shifts his attention to Olivia who had only been listening to the conversation of people in front of her. He bends over and caresses her head.

“It is Olivia, my little sister.” Helena answers his question. Hearing this, Bryan lifts up his head and stares at Helena’s face. He is frowning, but then he stares back at Olivia.

“Hello, Mr. Bryan. I’m Olivia. You’re as handsome as Mr. William.” Olivia says it brightly.

“Really? You are good at praising.” He comments with a giggle and pulls himself up.

“Yeah. I have met him several times.”

“So this is our first meeting, right?” He asks again while folding his arms across his chest.

“Yes. I’m very happy to see you, Mr. Bryan. And I think Helena too, isn't it?” Olivia raises her head and stares at Helena.

“Yeah, certainly.” Helena answers her.

“Is that right? But I’m not really sure since she came here to meet William, and not me.” Bryan says. It makes all of them laugh.

“Olivia, I want to make some pies. Would you like to help me?” Mrs. Tinsley asks Olivia.

“Sure, Mrs. Tinsley. I really love pie.” Olivia answers her eagerly.

“Alright. Let’s come with me and leave these two people to have a talk.” Mrs. Tinsley says while chuckling and taking Olivia’s hand. They leave Helena and Bryan in the backyard.

“How are you doing, Helena?” After a few moments of silence, Bryan asks her.

“I’m good. I’m really good. You? How about you?” Helena feels rather clumsy.

“Not bad.” Bryan answers her briefly. Then he guides her to take a seat on a bench.

“Olivia…” Bryan says hesitantly.

“She is my stepsister.” Helena explains as if she knows what is on his head. Bryan is a bit stunned.

“A year after you left this house, my father took Olivia home. She was so little and adorable. He said that she is his own other daughter and would be my sister too. I believe that it was really hurtful for my mother knowing this truth. She said that Olivia’s mother has died so she would be her mother too. At first, I couldn't accept her presence. But seeing my mother’s love and sincerity, I realize that there is no use for hating someone. My mother gives her much love. I just want to do the same thing too.” She pauses for a moment.

“I don’t know what is on my father’s head. He always asks me to love my stepsister, but himself even has a bad relationship with his own sister, Aunt Vivian.” Helena adds with a sigh.

“I know exactly how you feel.” Bryan stares at Helena with a smile and clasps her hand.

“She is a good girl. And you are a kind sister for her. I believe that your kindness is as much as your mother’s.” Bryan states.

“I’m not that good. I believe that I can’t put up with such a condition of married life.”

“No. You will not. You will never experience that. I believe that you will have a happier one.” He says earnestly to convince her. They look at each other and smile.

“I hope so, Bryan. So, why did you just come after five years? It was always William who came to visit.” Helena starts to change the topic.

“William came here only to see you, Helen. And I think my reason to be here is the same.” Helena frowns when she hears him.

“Helena, do you still love William?” She is a bit surprised with the question.

“W-why did you ask that?” Helena gives him a question back. She feels ashamed.

“Helena, I know that you love him, so did William. He said to me that he loves you very much and he wants to marry you. He wants you to always be happy. Please, don’t fill your mind with grief. You can also share everything with me to ease your mind.” Bryan pauses and stares at Helena who only hangs her head in silence.

“Helen, look at me,” Bryan holds Helena’s hands tightly. She then lifts her head and looks at Bryan’s eyes. “Please forgive William because he never confesses to you. But, you have to know that he does really love you. He always tries to be your best company. He will not let you be alone and feel sad. A-and, and so do I. I do have the same feeling too.” He explains slowly.

“Y-you, you what?” Hearing Bryan’s words, her eyes start to be filled with tears.

“I… I love you, Helena. I’m sorry, I have never experienced such a feeling before. When I knew the fact that you love him more, I just tried to get over this feeling. But I think I still keep it in my heart.”

“Bryan…” Helena tries to interrupt him.

“No, Helen. Just listen to me first. I will explain to you.” He continues. “William was sick.” He pauses. His eyes look very sad and his lips trembling. Helena seems fidgety.

“He has had a serious heart condition since he was a child. When we moved here and we met, he told me that he wanted to keep his disease a secret. He didn’t want to tell you and make you sad. So he always tried to be alright in front of you. He realized that he loved you, but he didn’t want to confess to you since he was afraid that he couldn’t always be with you and would just leave you alone in this world. He wanted to marry you but he couldn’t.” He pauses again.

“He was very strong. He had struggled with the illness for a very long time. But sadly, one year ago, he gave in to his life. He passed away.” Bryan tells the truth. His heavy tears streamed down. So does Helena.

“N-no… d-don’t fool around with me, Bryan. You're lying. Even the last time we met he said that he wanted to take me to Scotland. To his home.” Helena sobs and becomes increasingly restless. She tries to deny Bryan’s explanation.

“No, I’m not lying. I will take you to Scotland to see the truth.” Bryan pulls her into his arms. He hugs her tightly between her sobs. Helena’s sob sounds very hurtful for Bryan. All this time, Helena just knows that there is no something wrong with William. She never knows that he has suffered a lot. He always soothed and consoled her when she was distressed. Helena even once imagined living happily with William, but now it is only a dream and an impossible thing.

The next day, Bryan and Helena off to Scotland. There, Bryan brings her to a cemetery, to meet William. It truly suffocates her knowing the truth that William has passed away. She can not see him anymore. Only a tombstone which becomes evidence that William has been resting peacefully. If Helena could repeat the time, she would say to William that she loves him very much. Now, Helena is just trying to accept the reality that William is not her fate. Helena can not hold back her tears. Bryan just stands next to her silently, then he gives her a letter. He says that it was a letter from William for her.

Through this letter, William expresses his feelings. If he can, he will stay by her side for a long time, marry her, and give her nothing but happiness. However, he thinks that he is not capable of doing that. William is really grateful and glad to meet a girl like her. Helena becomes a reason for him to survive. When he is not here anymore, he will not leave her alone. William wants Bryan to marry her. He believes that Bryan will give her much love. Bryan will protect and cherish her. William will be glad if his wish comes true. Hope Helena will have a happier life after this. She is overcome with emotion and confusion. Helena folds the letter and raises her head. She stares at Bryan’s eyes. Helena believes that Bryan has known about this. He gives a faint smile and they hug each other. Helena can not make the decision for now.

Two weeks after their return from Scotland, Bryan proposes to her. Helena has carefully thought about it. She believes that Bryan can give her much happiness. She can see that this man truly loves her. Then Helena decides to accept his proposal. Bryan looks very happy. Helena realizes that it is useless to lament her past. She wants her future to be happier and she believes that Bryan is the one she needs. She will fly away to pursue her happiness with someone she loves. Helena trusts him and she will love him as much as he loves her because for now she only hopes that the future life of her and this man is having a very happy married life.
