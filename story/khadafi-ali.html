The Pure Girl and The Sinner Boy

Khadafi Ali Mactasya
Fantasy

I flipped over the page from the book I was reading. That time, my parents were holding a party for the nobles in our kingdom like usual. I was sitting there in the library to escape having to greet their acquaintances. I hate attending parties since I have to force a smile and endure the boredom of waiting for my parents to end their conversation.

Five minutes went by when suddenly the door to my library burst wide open, revealing a little girl around my age who's most likely the culprit. She had a very bright smile and beautiful blue eyes. An unforeseen lovestruck feeling overwhelmed my heart.

I averted my eyes back to the book I was reading. From my peripheral vision, I could see the girl was closing in on me. My hands were trembling a bit. I never interact with kids my age since my parents arrange so many lessons for me in my house, let alone a noble's daughter. She jumped in and sat on the arm of the sofa where I was sitting.

"Hey, what are you reading?" Her magnificent voice entered my ear. I swiveled my neck a bit and found her face just a few inches away from my face. Her gorgeous blue eyes fixated on the page I was reading. I could smell the scent of her soap wafting towards my nose.

"It's a tome of elemental magic." I tried to hide my embarrassment by focusing on the book. Heard the answer to her question, she threw another question.

"What are you wearing ?" She asked after she jumped down and stood in front of me, pointed at the thing in my chest.

"Oh, this? It is a protection amulet. It emits a barrier when you're in danger." I answered while holding the necklace I was wearing.

She couldn't hold her amazement, making an o-shape with her mouth. "THAT'S SO COOL!!" She said with a loud voice, half yelling, I might add.

I tried to continue my reading session, but I couldn't focus on the book that the girl was standing in front of me, staring at me. Then she wandered around my family's library, searching for an object of interest to spend her attention.

Not five minutes passed when suddenly she grabbed my hand and dragged me out of the library to the garden. I even nearly damaged the ancient tome that has been passed down on my family for hundreds of years.

When we arrived at the garden, she swiftly sat down on one of the park benches in my garden. She then patted the space beside her, gesturing for me to sit down. I didn't have any other option, nor did I dislike the idea in the first place, so I complied.

"You know, my parents forbid me to read or study about magic," she said. "They always forced me to take dancing lessons and etiquette classes. It is as though I have no other future than being a pawn for my parents to use, marrying me off to some older nobles that they think would profit them. They already married off two of my elder sisters, and I'm the next in line. They brought me to this party to put my dancing and etiquette classes to the test. But, as you see, I'm running away from them now."

Her story had quite an impact on my feelings. I could sympathize with her. I was not close with my parents, and I have always been with the maids as far as I remember. And even after that, they put me through many classes to make me a proper noble and fit to rule the land, as they say. But I was not interested in that. I've always been amazed by the adventurers who came and went by our territory, taking monster subjugation quests now and then. I wanted to be an adventurer. I didn't want to be a stuck-up noble that continuously schemes and sucks up others for their benefit.

Getting no reaction whatsoever, the girl wiped the tears that streamed down from her eyes. It must be hard to share a story like that with a stranger like me. "Ah, sorry for bringing your mood down too. I-"

"No, not at all. I could relate to your story as well. My parents aren't forbidding me from studying magic or swordplay. They always force me to be the perfect noble that could rule the land as they see fit." I cut in before she could continue what she wanted to say, hoping it could brighten her mood.

She smiles. "Then we are two peas in a pod!" She remarks with her bright and cheery voice like before.

"Yeah, I guess we are two peas in a pod."

We continued to share stories. She talked about her favorite toys, her favorite snacks, the books she had read before, and many more things. We stayed in the garden until the sun in the sky swapped its place with the moon.

Then her parents came to the garden to take her home. Seeing as she held hands with her parents and thought that I might never get a chance to see her again, I mustered up as much courage as I could to ask about her name.

Hearing my question, she turned around and answered, "Lunafreya," with her usual bright smile.

After that incident, both our parents grew closer, and so did us. We spent time together whenever her parents went to my house to discuss their business. We did many things together. Most of the time, it was her dragging me around. But I did not dislike what she did. We read books together, pranked the maids together, went to town together, or only sat together in the garden silently watching the sun set and the moon rise.

Then, on that one occasion, we had a big fight. On the previous visits I showed the swordplay I've learnt so far. After seeing my swordplay, she pleaded to me to teach the swordplay. But I disagreed. She pleaded once more, but I didn't change my mind. Her mood instantly dropped. And for several subsequent visits, she is visibly angry with me. She, who always has such a bright smile, pouts and sulks when I greet her. Her eyes, that always looked so gorgeous, scowled at me whenever our eyes met. It wasn't for long that our parents noticed our fight and started questioning us.

"It's his fault for being meanie," Luna answered, pointing at me.

Seeing her accusing me like that, I flipped out.

"It's not fair, Luna! You are always so talented at everything you do! I can't play piano while you play like a famous minstrel! You always have a good mannerism while I'm being called a barbarian! You could draw beautiful people with complex scenery, and I can't even draw a face properly without an oversized eye or an asymmetrical lips! It's not fair that you could always do what you wanted and be good at it. I also want something to show off to you, something that I'm good at, and you could admire me for it" I vomited all my pent-up feelings to the floor. Both my parents and Luna's parents were visibly shocked by that. Luna seemed shocked too. I could feel warm liquid flowing down from my eyes. I didn't know when I started crying. I wiped the tears out and ran out to my bedroom.

I didn't know how many times had passed since, but I knew it was already dark outside when Luna showed up by my bedroom's door. She was noticeably hesitant to step into my bedroom, but she swallowed her hesitation and entered the room. I was still pretending like I hadn't noticed her presence. "I-I'm sorry, Ravus. I never knew you felt like that while playing with me. I-If you are annoyed by me playing with you, I'll stop coming here." I could hear the shaking in her voice. When she turned around to head for the exit, I hastily grabbed her hand.

"Wait," I exclaimed.

"It's not like I hate you or despise your existence. It's just… very hard for me to accept that you're very talented, and I envy you for that. I'm sorry too for flipping out like earlier."

Hearing my words, she smiled brightly as usual.

"Then we could play together again?"

"I guess we could." I awkwardly scratched my cheek.

"Thank you, Ravus. I don't know what I would do if you hate me". She seemed very happy to hear that, so much so that she pulled me into a hug.

We then walked side by side to the room where our parents were. While Luna was away, they agreed to have Luna join my swordplay class and make me her senior in the class. Well, I did not make a fuss over that again, so it was a win-win situation, I guess.  

After that, we grew even closer than before. We were almost inseparable. Someone could ask a maid about one of us, and what they found would always be the two of us. Since we were already in our teenage years, we started to go to parties thrown by the nobles. Sometimes still with our parents, other times with just the two of us. We always danced together at those parties, and we never got tired of it. Until one day, my mom jokingly said that would make a cute couple together.

Hearing my mom's words, I felt my face warm and averted my eyes.

“Mom!" I nearly yelled out of embarrassment.

Luna reflectively did the same, averting her eyes while making a light complaint to my mom. Seeing both our reactions were all our parents needed to know that we both had feelings for each other. And for the rest of that night, they weren't bringing up the topic. Later I knew they started to lay the groundwork for our engagement.

And when finally the talk of our engagement came around, both of us sat down together. They asked our opinion about it. I couldn’t say a word to answer their question. But Luna, while having the reddest face I had ever seen, gave her consent with a nod of agreement. And at that moment, I felt like I was the happiest man alive.

What followed was quite hazy. Both our parents were very busy handling all sorts of preparation for our engagement party, while me and Luna were still hanging around like usual. The difference was that we didn't hesitate to hold each other's hand or even give a hug. Her soft hands and the warm feeling that I got every time I hugged her would never get replaced by anything in this world.

But then, that incident happened. I messed it up. Just because of a cursed ancient book talking about a magical pact with a demon. When I told Luna that I wanted to try to make a pact with a demon, she cheerily nodded in agreement.

I screwed up. I had never thought that she would be the cost of the pact. It was all my fault. If only I read that damn sentence, if only I paid a little more attention, I would still have her by my side. She is worth more than a pact with a demon. She was worth more than even my life and soul.

I flinch when I feel a cold hand on my shoulder. I swivel my head around and find the healer of the party. Gemini is standing behind me. "Get some sleep, Ravus. It's my turn to guard the tent now."

I think for a second and then shake my head a bit. "Nah, I still can guard for a few more hours. Get some more sleep, Man. You must be tired after that last battle this morning."

"You're the boss, Ravus. But don't expect us not to kick you up if you feel drowsy during tomorrow's battle" Gemini stares at me for a moment and then walks back to his tent.

I laughed a bit, responding to his remarks. As I sit here, guarding the bonfire before battling the force that wants to destroy the world tomorrow, I swear to you, Luna. I swear that I will use all the power I have to stop those monsters and die in the process. I will be with you once again, and I hope you don't hate me for what I have done.
