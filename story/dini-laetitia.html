Egg Tart Memories

Dini Laetitia
Romance

"Morning". He smiled. She chuckled a bit. 

"You look lovely today." His smile gets wider as he speaks. Of course, he is smiling, who wouldn't be happy if that one girl you have liked since tenth grade in high school actually had the same feeling towards you. It feels like a dream, she’s like Madonna in the middle of dobby. Tall, beautiful hair, slender body, has long and curly eyelashes, not to mention first in class, absolute nerd, good at sport, and has the heart of a saint, and has the most beautiful smile in the world. Somehow, he's mid in class, surprisingly also a huge nerd, not really handsome nor has he had many achievements, at least he's confident with his looks thanks to grandma who always reminds him of how handsome he is.

"I just like you," she said. Wow, he really doesn’t understand the logic of attractive people. The scene can be seen pretty much in every romance movie, predictable. The prince charming fell in love with the servant’s kindness, the lovely princess can’t resist the butler’s charisma, not to mention the boss and secretary cliché. He had always loved that kind of story but never imagined that it would actually happen in his life.

You can say that he is very much insecure. When they are together on today’s Sunday date, all the people in town at least take a glance at her, appreciating her beauty while it lasts before they split away and never bump into her ever again. She just wears casual tees and jeans. 

"I don’t really fashionable," she murmurs cutely. Of course, she is, with that face and body proportion she even makes her school uniform a runaway fit. He is glad, so that he at least can match with her.

The first time they talked, in a civic education class. Boring, no one likes civic education class. Sitting in class studying the constitution, even her beautiful eyes want to hide. But that old hag in front of the whiteboard somehow had an idea to ruin her sweet dreams, 

"Can you recite Constitution no.12 about Pancasila as a source of law in Indonesia?". Half sleeping, of course she wasn’t listening. He, being dramatic, wanted to be her mighty hero by raising his hand after she faded out for almost 30 seconds. After his dramatic presentation, he looked at her. She let out a crooked smile, half asleep. He swore that was the most beautiful smile he had ever seen in his life, after his grandmother, of course. After that, they somehow ended up in pairs for next week's presentation. That was the first time they ever talked in their two years of high school life. Up till now, his feelings towards her haven't changed a bit.

They’re heading to the nearest mall, as expected double nerd, no one wants to watch a biography movie on a date, except for them. The movie was partially boring, but he never once let down his smile, with her hand intertwined around his, it was instantly the best movie he had ever watched. He glances at her every five minutes, examining her facial expression, mostly flat, but that was the prettiest she's ever been, thanks to the dimmed movie theater lightning.

After what feels like eternity, the movie ends. It's 8 PM, still they have plenty of time as tomorrow is the weekend. They decided to go to the boy’s house. It was their first anniversary. Before going home, they decided to buy half a dozen mini egg tarts to celebrate their mini occasion. Walking together in silence, but they knew even without seeing each other's faces, those smiles never faded.

As he is in charge of being the dramatic boyfriend, but his brain, filled with butterflies, forgets to buy a tiny candle for their mini celebration. She laughs,

 "Let me get it in alfa near transJakarta stop," she suggests. The boy almost lets out a voice until she interrupts,

"I want to buy some ‘bread’ too." Aah, he gasps and turns an understanding gesture.

"don’t be long," he said while preparing some tote bags for her.

While waiting for her, he slipped some orange juice from the refrigerator, her favorite, poured some into his fanciest mug, and arranged the egg tarts on the table prettily. While waiting, he fiddles with his laptop, finding a good playlist for their tiny occasion. Fifteen minutes, there is no sign of footsteps at the front door. He checked his phone, no sign of a blinking light notification. Ah, he remembers there is a construction of a bridge near that store; she perhaps took the longer route to avoid the construction area. He patiently waits while poking the cream from the egg tarts, sweet and creamy.

Thirty minutes had passed. She should have come ten minutes ago, reached out his phone, typing in WhatsApp 

‘Where are you? You still remember the way back home, right?’. One checklist, not delivered, she is offline. He texts her again through messaging, but still no answer. Call her phone, out of service. 

‘Did the battery die?’. He rushed to the front door, didn't bother to wear a jacket, and with just a slipper he ran. Ruthlessly, he pushes the alfa door. The cashier gave him a scowl look while saying 

"Hello, welcome to Alfa, happy shopping" don’t bother to reply, he asked the cashier girl,

"Was there a girl buying a candle and a pad here about fifteen minutes ago?" the cashier rolled her eyes tiredly. It seems like she’s having a bad day too. 

"I apologize, according to our policy, we can’t give you information about our customers" she didn’t bother to hide her annoyance anymore. 

"I’m her boyfriend, she should have arrived at my house but I can’t find her". If so, they should have bumped into each other on his way there, but there is no sign of her. 

"Have you tried to call her phone?" he sighed loudly as a reply,

"Of course, I have, if she answered I wouldn’t be here going after her" the cashier perhaps doesn’t want to extend the problem as her shift just started three hours ago and she is really tired at the moment. 

"She buys maxi pads, orange juice, and a few mini candles and leaves about 20 minutes ago" 

 "Oh, my dear God, thank you". He rushed out and ran, still trying to call her phone, still answered by a robot customer service lady. He prayed all along the way, his face whitened as all the blood flowing right to his heart pumping it back and forth hoping that the body wouldn't faint in the middle of the road. He then takes turns going to the construction area. Still no sign of her presence. He then turns another way, on a small road, wondering if she got lost between the alley. Desperate, he returns home hoping that she is already at the table while arranging the mini candles in her favorite egg tarts. He holds his breath as he turns the door handle.

"Are you here?". The only thing that answers him is Tori Kelly from his laptop playlist singing Paper Hearts, her favorite song. 

"My god where are you?!". He dashes back into the road, still trying to reach her phone, investigating all the possibilities of her being lost or simply going somewhere else to buy a surprise gift for their celebration. Who knows?

11PM, almost three hours and he was still running through the block. His shirt is wet from sweat, his face is redder than a boiled crab, his smartphone overheats, and his love is nowhere to be found. He even went to her boarding house, but she was not there. There is no way she went back to her parents’ house because they live across the island. He contacts everyone who possibly knows her locality, from her best friend to her tutor students, and all the answers are no.

Desperate, he went to the police station, wanting to make a report of a missing person. Unfortunately, the police can’t process his report because she hasn't been missing for approximately 24 hours. He swears at the officers but he can’t do anything. So, he went back home and waited, hoping that she was just trying to prank him even though it’s not really funny and he almost had a heart attack because of her. Silently, he didn’t bother to put the egg tarts on the refrigerator and decided to sit in the hallway so that he could hear if someone was coming.

3AM, his eyes wide open, almost crying, his fingers restlessly playing with the hem of his shirt, hoping it will calm him down. Eyes and ears steady pay attention into any blink of his phone hoping the notification with a red heart shows up. 

10AM, he startled, since when had he fallen asleep in the hallway? He immediately checks his phone, no notification. He rubbed his face roughly, frustrated, when did this go wrong? does it when he did not insist to company her to the market, or when he suggests to buy that stupid candle, or when they planning their date from weeks ago for their anniversary. Anger filled his body, he then decided to wash his face in the bathroom, change his dirty clothes, and decide to go out and continue his search. 

While walking, he did not stop just once, trying to contact anyone who possibly knows her location, waiting for good news. When he passed by the river bank, he noticed that somehow the place was more crowded than usual. There's a police officer and some rescue teams preparing a speedboat. His heart pounding loudly. It couldn’t be true. Can not beat his curiosity, he asked the nearest person there

"Did something happen?"

"They found a body near the bank," the person answered while looking at the river. His heart is going crazy. He prayed in his heart that what he was thinking was not what actually happened. When suddenly, a man on the speedboat yells out that they already secured the body of the poor victim. He glanced at the body bag. It seemed like the person rather had a slender body and a little strand of long black hair sticking out. It is a woman. He fell to his knees, covering his mouth with his trembling hands, kneeling in disbelief. Thirteen minutes later, the police identify that the body is a teenage girl. There is a wound in the back of her head because of a blunt object collision. The police are still investigating the case, whether it is a murder or suicide case. Then the body was taken to the hospital for further identification.

He is still sitting in his place, not moving even an inch. When suddenly, at 2 PM, his phone rang. Unknown number.

"Hello," a man with a very low voice speaks through the speaker. He stated that he is a police officer in charge of the boy's report from last night. He said that he had good news, that his team had already found her. If he hadn't said the next sentence, the boy's heart could have stopped immediately. The drowning victim who he just saw earlier is her, his girl. The officer then asked him to go to the police station to be interrogated. With a straight voice the boy answered. All of his emotions flew away from moments ago. The color of his world went gray.

A few years later, he sits on the river bank near the bridge. He could recall every memory, every dialogue, every scene that happened that day. Scrolling through his phone, he messages the very same number every day. That damned day, when he arrived at the police station, there were a few police officers and about 4-5 construction workers waiting while being handcuffed. The police explained that his girl was harrassed and murdered by those workers. It happened so fast, when the girl was walking home from the store, she passed the construction site of the bridge. The typical workers teased the poor girl, from the frontal and inappropriate remarks, until one of the two workers tried to follow her and even touched her shoulder, sexually harassing her. The girl, of course, gave a reaction of rejection, but the workers persistently continued to tease and harass her. Because of defense reflex, the girl slapped one of the workers. Shock, the angry worker pushed the girl backward too hard, the girl naturally lost her balance and hit her head on construction concrete. She was unconscious right then and there. Panicked, the workers tried to save the girl who was bleeding profusely on the back of her head. When the rest of the co-workers gathered to check on her, the girl was no longer breathing. Not wanting to get in trouble for killing someone, they agreed to drown the poor girl's body in the river.

His eyes have lost their spark as he types word by word on his phone, texting her number as if she could read or reply to those chats. Everyday, he plays Paper Hearts by Tori Kelly to sleep, buys some egg tarts on the weekends, and never runs out of orange juice in his refrigerator. He'll do anything to keep memories with her. He even watches the biography movie that they watch on their anniversary every once in a while. He wants to remember every detail that happens with her. 

Many years later, at 74 years old, he still has the same exact smartphone. Even though his friends and grandkids say that he is very outdated, he doesn't care. Because that is the only thing that keeps his memory with her. Although he has a new love, a new family, and a new life, he still waits for her every second. He tells them about his day, his complaints, their birthdays, anniversaries, his experiences, adventures, as if the old cellphone was a diary, and even though he routinely turned on the reminder to always send a message at least once a month, because apart from his new life, this is what makes him the happiest.

Today is their umpteenth anniversary. He had half a dozen egg tarts and two glasses of orange juice set on the table. His shriveled fingers, because of aging, were a little too overwhelmed to light a match for a few small candles in his egg tarts. His dim eyes reached for the glasses beside the drawer. When his vision was clear enough, he reached into the old smartphone in his trouser pocket, typing some sweet words to the girl to celebrate their special day. As usual, he pressed the send button. But for some reason, something was different. The status, which usually only ticked one unsent sign, suddenly became two blue ticks. Not believing his eyesight because he was getting nearsighted, he put the cellphone back on the table. Then he heard a subtle ding. She replied, he isn't hallucinated as he never chugged a glass of alcohol today. 

‘I’m sorryyy I took too long, I'll be there soon, don't eat the egg tart without me! ’ He read those words carefully, then let out a small smile, before typing a few words to reply.

‘What took you so long? I'm tired of waiting for you. If you don't come in five minutes, I'll start eating them one by one until you run through that door! ’ Right after he sent the message, he heard the doorbell ring. He woke up immediately from his chair and walked briskly toward the front door. With half-quivering hands, he turned the door handle. As the door opens, he can see that exact smile, that long beautiful hair, and that face, standing in front of him while waving a tote bag filled with candles, orange juice, and maxi pads, giggling. His smile widens as his eyes water, his heart filled with joy, this isn't a dream, she is standing right here, with the same outfit as the last time they met. The man was stunned to cry, and the girl said in a voice that he missed the most. 

"Sorry you waited so long. Eey handsome, what's with that face?  did you cry?", he chuckled, and held her hand with his crinkled hands, led the girl to the dining room, and asked the girl to sit down. He even joked that he had eaten half of the tarts. They kept smiling and laughing while the girl put the candles she had brought and replaced the melted one that he lighted earlier on the egg tart. They held hands while looking at each other face to face after everything was set up and pretty, and the girl uttered the most beautiful smile.

"Thank you for waiting for me, I know it's really late but thank you too for filling my days happily I always read your messages, I'm really happy that you don't forget me, happy anniversary, I love you" the boys’ eyes finally have his color, his eyes filled with happiness tears as he replies with a trembling voice

"Happy anniversary!" they blow candles together with a happy smile. Then the guy naturally closed his eyes to make a wish, and like magic, when he opened his eyes, he was young again. He was himself on the first anniversary. Like when they went on that date with his ugly nerd T-shirt. Then, they ate the cake together with a smile.
