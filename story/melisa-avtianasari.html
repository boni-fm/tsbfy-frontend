Eternald

Melisa Avtianasari
Slice of Life

Of the many coincidences that exist, meeting him was the one thing I would never regret. The warmest man. Human who was too genius to compare with me, the human who was too rigid–as opposed to the chatty me.

        On a sunny afternoon, the cacophony of car horns echoed each other. Tired? Surely. They wanted to go home as soon as possible and laid down after a long day of activity. One of them was me, Claufiana Aleera, a freshman who finished the first day of orientation. It was 2050, and the world was getting more sophisticated, but the orientation still existed as a tradition. At that time, I was busy cursing how tiring the day was while waiting for Transjakarta.

“Huft, what kind of orientation is required to depart at 3 a.m and finish at 5 p.m? Does the committee not have a brain? They thought my home was near the campus, huh? Oh, hey, are you a freshman as well? What time do you leave today?”

I didn’t know what went through my mind at that time. All of a sudden I asked a boy beside me, who, of course, I also didn’t know who he was, but from his appearance, who looked calm and not strange, I was quite sure he was a freshman just like me.

“1 a.m,” he looked curious at first, but since I stared at him, he answered immediately. Of course, I was dumbfounded for seconds. My eyes bulged. I thought I was the only one who leaved too early, but then I found someone who leaved earlier than me,

“seriously? Why so early?”

Let's say I was too brave to talk with a stranger, but yeah, this is my weird habit. Then he nodded. I didn't know the correlation between nodding and my questions about the reasons. But that’s fine. At least I didn’t feel sleepy while waiting for the arrival of Transjakarta, which unfortunately took a long time to come. Seeing him, who I didn’t know his name, responded to my questions twice, so I continued the cursing agenda to him even though he only responded with “oh ya? Then? Seriously?” or even just a shake or nod. Hadeeehh, what a stiff boy.  

Finally, Transjakarta had arrived. Woah, it looked so crowded. So I hurried up to get a little space for a stand–since I knew all the seats were full. To keep my courtesy to him, who I didn’t know his name, I decided to say goodbye,

“Thank you for listening to my cursing, hehe. By the way, I’m Claufiana Aleera. You can call me whatever you want. See you tomorrow!”

After that, I ran while waving my hand to him. Now I realize how weird I was. I hoped I could meet him again since I hadn’t asked for his name and his major.

On the second day of the orientation period, I met him again. There was something odd because I saw him stand beside one of the orientation committees. It was even weirder when I realized that he used a committee name tag. Ho-how could? How’s my fate? What would happen to me later? What if he suddenly told the committee that I was cursing at them yesterday? Ahhh, Cla... you should watch your mouth.

During the orientation period, the thing I feared didn’t happen. Whether he had been told to that senior or not, I had to meet him this afternoon. Good fortune, he was 200 meters in front of me. He was looking down. I didn’t know what he was looking for. I followed him and looked down as well, trying to help him search for something I didn’t know. Later he realized my existence, a bit surprised, of course. Then tried to neutralize his expression.

“Hai, what are you looking for?” He said confusedly.

Surely, I was more confused than him since I intended to help him.

“So, what are you looking for, too?” I was asked the same question because the one who should have asked like that was me, not him.

“I’m not looking for anything,” he said briefly, of course with a flat expression that I have seen since yesterday that hasn’t changed apart from a surprised expression. So monotonous.

“Hah? So why did you look down? Are you not afraid of getting hit by something?” That time I was really confused.

He noticed I looked confused. He smiled faintly.

“This is the way I’m walking,” he answered. I was completely dumbfounded for a moment. What kind of people walk with their heads down? Ah, by the way... that was his first smile at me after meeting me twice, as thin as HVS paper, but that’s not a problem. It means he was not a robot.

“Uhm, I’m very sorry for what happened yesterday. I thought you were a freshman just like me since you look so calm. But please… don’t tell Reno I was cursing at him yesterday and said he was as grumpy as Squidward.” My two hands were holding each other as if begging him, plus a look of sadness, so he would pity me.

Without replied to my words, he instead stretched out his hand and said, “I’m Aksarabinaya. You can call me Abi.” My eyes just blinked. I responded with a mouth wide open in surprise. I felt weird and confused as well. As far as I remember, I didn’t ask his name.

“For God’s sake, I didn’t even ask your name, bro,” I grumbled.

“Let’s go home. It will be crowded later.”

Once again, he answered out of context, then walked away, leaving me, who was still gaping at how weird he was.

I followed him and tried to align his steps.

“But please don’t tell him anything, can you? I’m begging you.”

“Yes,” he said shortly. I smiled, relieved to hear that. It meant everything to me. My secret was safe with him. And again, I was left behind. His footsteps were so long. I followed and aligned his steps again and said, “Thank you, Aksa. Tomorrow I’ll buy you a click. I promise hehe.”

“Abi, Cla…Call me Abi, not Aksa,” He protested, but I ignored him. I didn’t care. Aksa or Abi, were still the same person.

Yupp, in short, that was our weird meeting. Not as dramatic as many teenagers in general who immediately get to know each other. I instead nagged at him at the first meeting. I still remember clearly how confused he was and his flat expression. That day I also knew the first fact that I was the first person who called him Aksa, not Abi.

Day by day, Aksa and I became closer. Don’t ask what kind of relationship we had since I didn’t know, and I would pretend not to hear that question. We just let it flow. As long as we felt comfortable. Aksa always accompanied me to go home until the last Transjakarta stop, and I enjoyed those moments. I was too busy chattering as much as I wanted to. I even forgot to ask where his house was. At that time, I thought our home was in the same direction since he always left the same stop as me. But then I found out from one of my friends, Kaia Laluna, that he always goes back to the same Transjakarta and turns around. His home and mine were in different locations.

I asked him,

“Why didn't you tell me if our Transjakarta route was different? You have to go back and forth because of it,” I peeved. I made him go through the way back home twice as far.

“It is indeed my wish to accompany you back home while making sure you arrive safely,” He said it firmly, as if he didn’t want to be denied. However, I felt touched by his actions. Aksa was always kind, always paid attention to me, and always knew the smallest detail in my life, which I often forget. I always forgot to appreciate myself, and then there was a person without whom I expected would give a reward to me. No words and no promises.

        One day, a package came to my home. I didn’t order any online packages, and I was a bit confused. The urge to open the package was being covered by fear. I was afraid that anyone out there intended to take revenge because of my jokes. Since I was too curious about what was inside the package, I finally opened it. There was a handwritten letter from Aksa. He wrote many things. He said this package was his form of reward and appreciation to me, who went through difficult times– the final test. I was speechless. I had never even thought to please myself since I never knew what I wanted. In fact, he understands me better. He understands all my struggles better than I do.

        He was too kind as a human. Until I wondered to myself, did God not make a mistake in creating him in the form of a human? Everything about him always amazed me. I no longer understood. There was always a side of him that made me admire him more and more every day. About how he was always wise in looking at and solving every problem and how to see the pain from different perspectives. He was also a genius, so I was not surprised anymore when he told me that he was offered a scholarship to continue his postgraduate studies in Sydney. He deserved all the kindness in this world. He asked for my consent at that time, and I 1000% supported him since it was a good opportunity for his education and career in the future. Then he accepted that scholarship, which means he must go overseas next month.

At this time, it was not the Transjakarta bus stop but the airport. If the Transjakarta bus stop was our first meeting place, the airport would be our farewell place.

“Please, don’t be a man who is more impressive than this, can you? Later I will find it more difficult to improve myself,” I said with a joking tone. He chuckled, and I was sure it was a laugh I would miss. It was true. I couldn’t deny it anymore.

“I’m doing this to deserve to be with you, so I no longer think that I don’t deserve you,” he answered with unchanging gazes. Still as warm as 20 months ago, the first time I met him. The world likes to joke with all its unintentional, and this one is my favourite. God's accident brought my messy self to meet with his warm figure.

I smiled and said this was a calm smile. Do you know how it feels to try to keep calm with all bubbling emotions?

“Take care of yourself. The world isn’t doing well. You can come home anytime. You know. I’ll still be here.”

It had been one year since he left. We were still keeping in touch via Gmail, even with fairly sparse intensity. Aksa didn’t have any social media accounts that could be stalked whenever I miss him. He once said that the answer of longing wasn’t just looking at photos but with meetings. I was pouted. There was Whatsapp, which made communication easier to any extent, but he chose Gmail. Resentful. Gmail was often stacked with Gmail Classrooms, you know!

Day after day, the longing continued to pile up without ever being met. The thought of giving up always came to me at a crucial hour–in the middle of the night. It was true, it had been two months since I didn’t answer his messages. I let him focus on his studies and work—Aksa was also doing an internship there without burdened by this feeling of longing. The night was getting late, the clock was always ticking, without ever stopping. Even though I was begging God repeatedly to stop the time for a while. The time when he was beside me, and I didn't have to worry about his leaving. My feet wobbled, didn’t ask me where all my energy had gone.

“Let me take you home,” he said, as if breaking the silence. I didn’t know what kind of hallucinations had entered my sense of hearing. Out of nowhere, I heard his voice. If that’s Aksa, I swore I would refuse it and run as fast as I could, and immediately got into a blanket. I wanted to cry.

Yeah, that’s Aksa.

“I can do it by myself, Sa.”

He laughed as he rubbed my head. I didn’t know what kind of funny it was, but I couldn’t find it out. Maybe, he saw my surprised expression with teary eyes.

“You know, I never refuse. Whatever it is.”

He said firmly. I accepted his offer in the end. He ignited the car engine. This time without the driver who usually accompanied him. It felt like a long time since the last time of our night drive. I looked around, nothing had changed. Even the silhouettes of our photo are still neatly displayed there. Yea, the silhouettes only. Aksa didn’t like his handsome face immortalised in the photo.

There was no conversation. Only silence surrounded us. Ah, if I could turn on the phone, I would play the sound of crickets, at least to break this silence. Twenty five minutes passed, and we arrived in front of my home.

“Thanks, Sa.” I smiled painfully.

For God’s sake, why did it feel awkward?

“Wait a minute,” he interrupted my steps. I turned to look at him. He was just silent, staring into my eyes. His gaze started to glaze over. He slowly walked towards me.

“Cla…,” he whispered hoarsely, his gaze implying longing. I knew that look. He hugged me tightly. I heard him cry between our hugs. Amid his sobs.

“My life is getting worse without you. I can’t focus on anything. I made many mistakes during work. I can't hold on to it anymore. Come back to me and don't ignore me, please? Don't act like we're strangers and nothing will happen between us.”

“Sa, I can’t…”

I stroked his back, hoping to relieve all the tightness he’d been holding in for so long. Then, he loosened the distance between us. His surprised expression couldn’t be hidden, one of many expressions I missed.

“Aksa, I have told you. You’re the sun, and I’m the moon. You can emit your light without the moon, even have that light. You shine brighter without me. Long before we met, your light was already bright, even too bright for me, who had been stuck in the dark for so long…”

I took his hand and rubbed it, unable to hold back the tears that had been breaking down my defense, the tears that were never absent when I missed him.

“Aksa, you can get whatever you want, and you got it, even without me. You’re doing well so far. Career and education, everything is in your hands. I won’t be able to burden you with the longing that always forces you to meet. Sa, it's your dream, and you can't give up on this. Remember that. My presence only adds to your burden and messes everything up.” Still sobbing, I was too embarrassed to look into those eyes I missed.

“Cla, you know exactly that you are never a burden for me, even for once. You're on my priority list. I’ve never felt burdened with distance and longing, Cla. I'm fine with that as long as we keep in touch and you're not ignoring me. Please remember all the things we have been through, we have been doing fine for the past year.”

Aksa kept convincing me that everything would work out just fine. I didn’t hear a firm voice there, only a helpless voice. I thought Aksa forgot that dream and love could not go hand in hand. One of them must be sacrificed.

        What is over is never really finished.

“I think we should break up even though we never started any relationship. We have to end this before we can't stop. Let’s end this happily. You know, sunset. Right? It left in all its magnificence. I want our memories to be filled with only beautiful things, including this farewell.”

I insisted on forcing him. The one thing I believed at that time was that I would go first to leave him. I hugged him for the last time, and I was quite sure that it would be the last hug from the warmest person I have ever met, though I denied that there would be another time.

        He was flying even higher. So high til' I couldn’t reach him. I couldn’t be a selfish person who kept him staying beside me in any circumstances. He was too perfect for me, who liked to complain about anything. In fact, without realising it, I had been restraining him indirectly. I made him hesitate to fly higher. I was highly sorry for that, Sa. Now, you could go wherever you want and reach whatever your dream. I wouldn’t force you to stay here. I realised you were too good to be true.

        The room was dominated by white, and sounded boisterous with all the emotional turmoil. Cla has finished her story from the beginning until the farewell with Aksa. Cla was still sobbing. It was not easy to open up the wound that she has bandaged all this time. A month before her meeting with Aksa, she was officially diagnosed with stage 4 brain cancer. The doctor said it was possible that a part of Cla’s brain would be damaged, and it will undermine her memory slowly, though not completely. Cla could’ve lost some of her memories of Aksa, and she didn't want that to happen. To anticipate, she decided to go to Dr Raditha Viyanka, the leading neurologist at that time, to perpetuate the memory of her brain.

        In 2054, the technology was very sophisticated. People could erase their memories or even immortalise them in the form of a roll of film known as the ‘eternal moment’. Cla chose the second option. Both options were quite difficult, because when the patient had finished the story, the recorded memory had been transferred to the film roll, then the patient would automatically not remember anything. The memories that had been transferred would be completely on the film roll, not in the hippocampus anymore.

        The process required Cla to sit in a chair and relax, telling the story she wanted to eternalise from beginning to the end, complete with detailed explanations of the facial expressions of the characters in the story and the location of the story. Emotions played an important role here because they could determine the type of memory. The electrodes attached to the left and right of the hippocampus would automatically detect a series of stories and recorded memory activities in the brain. The results would appear on the Eternal Monitor and can be processed into a film roll.

Cla managed to do it until the end. She had captured all the happy and painful moments she had with Aksa. Her film roll of the eternal moment was in her hands. She believed that despite how painful it was, Aksa had been the reason she could laugh all of a sudden when she was sleepy with his weird behaviour. She wanted to remember every detail of her life with Aksa. She didn't want her memories to be partially lost, so Cla proves that her memories were stronger than that damn cancer. At least, she could live in the memories she created herself, and those memories were on a film roll, far beyond her brain cancer. Those memories would last forever even if they were not in her memory anymore.

–Fin–
