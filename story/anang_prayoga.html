The Fantasy Of Schoolgirl

Anang Prayoga
Tragedy

It was cloudy that day. Strangely, you know this. I rarely saw a clear sky. Of course, the definition of bright here was a blue sky, with clumps of clouds that sometimes impudently passed in front of the sun. But I didn't care, because I had to go to school early in the morning. It's not that my school home was far away, but that I had to complete the task that the teacher gave me last week. The task must be completed. Otherwise, there would be someone injured in the class.

After I took a shower, I wore a school uniform, a white shirt, and a skirt in the same color as the shirt. This skirt was very comfortable for me, even though it was a bit narrow and above the knee length. Then I went to the dining room and saw that the food was already on the table. Mom slept, and I didn't want to wake her up. I didn't want you to wake up. I didn't know what would happen if that happened. After breakfast, I checked the bag again, then I went.

As I said before, the distance between school and home was close enough that I only needed to walk to school. My school was an elementary school with a large field. Consisting of 3 floors, you could see a beautiful mountain background with rice fields and forest that were slowly being replaced by building structures for residence, they said. And that's why I never liked the government.

I entered the class, which was on the 3rd floor. I sat in the middle of the bench. I didn't want to get the teachers' attention or be considered a naughty schoolgirl for being in the back position. I also didn't like sitting in front of the class. After sat down, I opened the book and began to complete the rest of the tasks that were actually almost finished. After the task was completed, I fell asleep.

"Hi." Someone calls me.

I woke up in a strange room. This room was quite narrow. It looked like a living room, but without windows and doors. There was also no ventilation, but strangely this room did not feel stuffy. There I found someone who greeted me.

"Who are you?" I asked.

The man replied, "you don't need to know. But, don't worry. I'll accompany you here.”

The guy was wearing a black hoodie.

I asked again, "since when are you stuck in this room?”

He said, "I don't know. But it seems to have been quite a while. I don't want to think about it.”

I started to panic, "then how to go back to school? I don't want to miss class.” I started crying, imagining myself as someone who would be stuck here forever.

The man approached me and said, “if that's what you want, you just have to close your eyes, and everything will return to normal.”

I closed my eyes, and suddenly I woke up in the classroom. The bell rang, and I went out into the field to hold a morning parade before class began. After singing the song Indonesia Raya, the principal explained the importance of a strong mind and a spirit of kinship between fellow students and teachers. After the morning parade, I went back to class. However, while trying to climb the stairs, I felt someone fingering my skirt. I tried to see, but I couldn't find the culprit because there were a lot of students cramming up the stairs to their respective classes. I didn't want to worry because it's not the first time I've experienced something like this.

In class, I sit in my seat. Not long after, Mrs. Harni, the mathematics teacher, enters the classroom. From his physical appearance and the way he dresses, he looks old. The rumor says that she is soon retiring, so this is likely her last year of teaching. She is famous for being very fierce. She will not hesitate to hit her naughty students or have bad grades.

"Marina, come here!" Mrs.Harni calls me.

"Yes, ma'am.” I go to the front of the class, showing the assignment I just completed this morning.

“You remember what I taught you in class last week?" Mrs.Harni asks me.

"Yes, ma'am," I answer.

“Why don't you do this task according to the formula that I taught you?" This time she asks me in a high tone.

"Because I think this is the fastest and easiest way, ma’am.” I answer in a fairly low tone. I'm starting to feel a bad feeling.

"Get out! What do you think you taught me that formula for?" Mrs. Harni seems very emotional.

I walk out of class. The other students in the class look at me with sharp eyes. Some look at me like they are watching a comedy as I go to the library. I pick up a book and approach a large glass in the corner of the library. There I glisten, noticing the scars on my face, arms, and legs caused by the blows of a broom from my mother. Then I fix the clothes and the short skirt I am wearing. After that, I sit leaning against the wall next to the big glass, and suddenly I fall asleep.

"We meet again," says the mysterious person

"Why Can I be in this place again?" I ask in confusion.

“Every time you fall asleep, you will return to this place. So every time you sleep, you'll find me here.”

I closed my eyes, but nothing happened, "why don't I go back to the real world?”

The mysterious person replied, "You are asleep,” showing the projection of my body in the real world.

“So you have to stay here for a while.”

I also sit on the couch with a feeling of resentment. In order not to get bored, I ask him. "I asked you, once again, who are you?”

He said, "I am the resident of this place. And this place is actually your subconscious.”

I was a little surprised, then asked again, "then why can you be an inhabitant of my subconscious?”

The person replied, “I don't know either. All I remember is that I was suddenly in this room.”

Suddenly I hear the bell ringing. A sign that the school has entered recess. I freak out about having to go back to the real world.

"Don't panic, now close your eyes.” The man covers my eyes with his hand. I wake up. I put the book I didn't read to where it should be. I go out of the library to the cafeteria. Damn, I meet Hendo. He's been bullying me a lot.

"Hello, Marina.” He greets me with a terrible smile. He is close to my family because he was the one who helped my mother sell, especially since my father died recently. Since my father's death, my mother has been more aggressive and has often beat me if I do something she doesn't like, such as getting a bad grade or just saying no.

"Come with me!" Hendo pulls my hand, forcing me to follow him to a quiet corner of the school. Hendo forces me to walk beside him by occasionally holding the skirt I am wearing. He's done this to me a few times. Sometimes he also commits other acts of abuse. I have reported it to the teacher a lot, but they always ignored it, so I can't do anything about it. I can understand his behavior caused by his association with delinquent children under the overpass.

"I need money," says Hendo firmly.

I asked, "for what? After all, you will be given money from helping my mother as well.”

Hendo lowers his tone, "I want to buy an answer key. Besides, your mom from 2 weeks ago hasn't paid me any money yet!”

I say, "Why don't you just ask my mom? What if I don't want to give you money? What will happen?”

Hendo gets angry as he says, "I'll tell your mother that your grades yesterday did not reach the passing grade!”

Hendo just left. I go to the cafeteria before the bell rings. The school ends, and I go home, enter the room, and change clothes without taking off the skirt. To me, wearing this school skirt wasn't just a comfort, but I feel that it showed me that I am a girl who doesn't need to act like a boy to be strong.

At night, there are no stars in the sky. It may be covered in clouds or vehicle smoke. Suddenly I hear a voice calling me.

"Marina, get off!" My mom exclaimed.

I came down to Mom. Her face looks angry.

"Marina, bring your duty book! Is it true that Hendo said that your grades have been below the passing grade lately?”

I can't say anything nor do anything other than going up to the room and hand over the assignment book to Mom. After seeing my work, mom goes to the kitchen to get a broom.

"You idiot! Your brain doesn't work! Useless child! Sorry, mom gave birth to you! You should have died from the beginning!" Mom says it while hitting me with a broom. As I cry while enduring pain, I suddenly get into an empty room, along with the mysterious person.

"Why am I here?"I asked.

“I don't know." The mysterious man replied. The room suddenly shook.

"We have to get out of this room!" I exclaimed. "How do we get out!?”

The mysterious person suddenly had an idea, " this wall is quite thin, so we have to break it!”

"Can you?”

“We have to try, before we get really stuck here.”

I also try to break the wall with him. It works. There is an iron door that looks very thick.

"I found the key!" The mysterious man exclaims. He tries to open the door, but the Key doesn't work. In front of the door is the inscription ‘access not recognized’.

He says to me, "maybe you shall open it."

He gives me the key. I took the key and used it. The door recognizes the access seen in the writing, but the key is very difficult to enter through the hole. I have to pull it out, then put the key in and push it even deeper so that the door finally opens and I come out. When I look back, that mysterious person has disappeared somewhere.

When I wake up, I see sauce spills falling off the table. Mom looks unconscious with her whole body covered in sauce. I notice the blue T-shirt and the white short school skirt that is still attached and also covered in sauce. I try to wake her up, but she is still asleep. Because I am too sleepy, I decide to go to my room to sleep, and for the first time, I can sleep well.
