Rahma Nabila Putri
Romance

I still remember it clearly. It was at the end of September in 1603. I can still smell the scent of the grass where we both lay that night. I can still see the shadow of the trees that hid us from those outsider eyes. I can still feel the breeze running over my nightgown and brushing over my hair. I can still hear your small chuckles to every joke I made regarding the suggestion for the name of every star that we found that night. It was a very long time ago, when I still believed that one star could not decide what would happen to us. It was when I still believed that those moments that we spent together would last forever. It was my first life.

Our bond started since we both used to sneak out of the house after midnight just to see the stars when they’re at their brightest state. It was also the only time when one could easily discover a new star that people haven’t seen before. We met each other at the wasteland a few meters from our backyards. It was him who gave an idea that we should do stargazing every Friday in that place which later became our secret spot. The spot was basically just a turf that looks safer to lay on than the others. It was surrounded by 20 meters pine trees whose shadows were wide enough to hide us from anyone, particularly our parents just in case they realized that we weren’t home and went to look for us here.

It was Friday, and we spent that night laying on top of the turf in our secret spot just like usual. I wasn’t feeling good but still decided to come as I didn’t want to let him win over me for discovering more stars. The ground felt so cold or maybe it was my body’s temperature that was too hot. We both silently lay there occupied with the sound of our breath. I stared blankly at the black canvas that didn't look as amazing as last week until the red dot suddenly came out and it surprised me as we haven’t seen that star since last year.

“Look! It’s your star!” I said with a loud excitement while pointing my finger to the red dot that was twinkling like it’s flexing its glow at us.

“My star?” he asked with a confused look.

“Yes, the red one,” I said still with a loud voice. “It’s the unique one, remember?” I finally sat down to make sure he remembers that star.

“Shhh…Don’t shout!” he said while chuckling. “You’re not in a good condition and you might lose your voice if you keep talking in that tone,” he said softly. I almost forgot that I was about to faint if it wasn’t for the red star that suddenly appeared.

“Ahhh…sorry! Oh right, you promised to give it a name if you happen to see it again. Now that you’ve seen it again, you should start to think of names for this red guy!” I said. “Think carefully or I will tease you for it for…forever!” I teased him while lightly tapping on his chest. He just smiled and then looked up at the red dot that was still twinkling its light to us.

“Betelgeuse,” He suddenly said.

“Huh-?”

“Let’s call it Betelgeuse,” he said once more, looking at me still with that smile. The name somehow sounds right.

***

I’ve lived many lives and this is the sixth time that I woke up in a new body and a new era, but still with the same memories. People call it reincarnation while I think this is more like a curse. It’s hard to have to call different people as your mom, dad, sisters, and brothers. The people, places, everything is always changed every time I reach the age when I started to understand about the world. They all changed but my memories are still the same. It comes rushing over me like the unwanted waves. It drags me with its strong current to a lonely shore, where there is only me and my memory which is filled with you. I have lived many lives but the only memories that I still remember clearly are the memories where there is you in it.

It sure is hard to live with a bank of memories from your past lives in your brain, but I still try my best to enjoy every life that I lived. I have just graduated from college and am now trying to find a suitable job for my skill. My friend, Sera, has been offering me a promising job in a radio station where she worked. She said if I later decide to join her, I will fill in the position where I will be broadcasting about the sky and songs that fit the weather. It sounds interesting but the hesitation just won’t go away. It’s not that I am insecure about my physical appearance because thankfully, God is being kind with me in this life regarding my look and the only thing I hate about myself in this life is my name which seems like it wouldn’t let me off of my past memories. Well, I have a slender body with shiny shoulder-length black hair, my hazel eyes are not that pretty like the ones that people used to draw for an animation character on TV for it has seen many things from hundred years ago but it still has the sparks, especially when I think about him. It’s also not that I am afraid that I will mess up because with hundreds of years of experience, I can easily aced every interview that I attend to. It’s just…I have made a promise to myself in my last life, which was my fifth life, that I will not give a single damn about stars and everything related to it. Stars…it always reminds me of him.

“Let’s make a promise under this red star, under the Betelgeuse, that as long as it still hasn’t lost its glow, we will always find each other. No matter what life that we are in, Betelgeuse will help us to connect with each other,”

That damn promise that he made, and I agreed to, was a laugh for the universe. We got separated right after that moment ended. The fever that I thought was only a seasonal fever turned out to be so much worse than that. I left him a week after we made that promise and when I woke up, I was a young boy living in a beautiful cottage in the countryside near the mountains, it was when his days had already ended. I couldn’t grow old with him. I didn’t know how his life went on after I left. Was he happy and acted like I never existed? Or was he also suffering from the grief just like how I feel every time I look up at the sky? I will never know. The last four lives that I lived after my first life was all the same. I couldn’t find him just like how I couldn’t find the red dot that usually appears in the winter months just after midnight.

In my last life, I started to think that maybe the star has lost its glow and that we could not meet again because of it. I decided to stop believing that the red star would appear again and will finally reunite this heart that is missing its half. I prayed to God in my last life to stop this curse. I begged Him to end these endless memories of mine unless he would make the star shine again that could connect me with him. But the last thing I remember after I begged Him for that, I woke up as myself now.

It's Friday now, I would be spending my time at the nearest rooftop tonight if this is my fifth life. Since I decided to just go on with my life and think of it as my first, I’ve been staying away from everything related to the stars. Sera called me this afternoon and asked me to meet up at the café near her office. It’s September and the weather has started to shift to winter. I took a cab and went to the café where I was supposed to meet Sera. She was already there sitting beside the glass window with a tall cup of Americano and her favorite red velvet cake. She has fair skin with sparkling green eyes. I would describe it as a beginners’ eyes, eyes that would show its light at every object it lays on. I sat in front of her right after I got there and ordered an iced strawberry latte. Not long after that, the waiter came to serve my drink and I immediately inhaled it until only half of the glass was left. Sera just watched me while smiling with her white teeth and waited until I finished to finally be able to talk to me.

“So, have you made up your mind?” she asked.

“About what?” I asked, pretending not to know what she meant.

“The job. You need it, Sky. Think about the stuff that your parents still have to pay for you while at this age, you should be the one who pays for their stuff!” she said, now her tone became serious that I almost couldn’t sense the sweetness in her.

“Hey! I’ve earned some money okay. Well, not as much as you but it is enough for me,” I lied.

“What is your reason for turning down my offer now? Last time you said you were busy helping your cousin with her pregnancy but now that the baby is born, aren’t you supposed to just accept my offer? Don’t get me wrong, I am not trying to nag you here, I am just trying to be a good friend to you,” she said.

She is right, I should just accept her offer regarding the job. I can’t go on living like this, tied with my past that only gave me more suffering rather than happiness. If I decide to live this life like it is my first, then I have to start to focus on this life only. To put it simply, I have to act like it is my first time seeing the stars every time I look up at the sky.

“…I guess you’re ri-,”

“OF COURSE, I AM!” she said, cutting my words with a loud voice that made the people who’s sitting in that café turn their heads to us.

“Sorry! I am just a little too excited. Okay I will arrange everything, in the meantime, I have prepared this for you,” she said while handing me a piece of a black A8 paper.

“What is this?” I asked her.

“A ticket to an art exhibition, I found it online,” Ohh…what a sweet gesture, I thought. “There’s this new artist called Arthur, he’ll hold the exhibition near my office so I thought an unemployed person like you would have many free times to go,” she replied, well I guess she’s not so sweet after all.

“An exhibition? Are you joking?” I asked, trying not to crawl on the table between us.

“Make sure to go there! I don’t want my money to go to waste, it’s also my last requirement for this job offer,” she adds with her cunning smile.

        Paintings have never been my thing since my first life. My hands are too stiff to even hold a painting brush properly. I also don’t understand a thing about good paintings, I only know colors. But I still have to go there if I am serious about this ‘live this life like a first life’ thing. Going to the exhibition is the beginning of that life and hopefully will be my last step of walking down the past memory-lane.

The day of the exhibition finally came. It’s the 22nd of September, the last day of the exhibition and it starts at 8 pm until 1 am. I don’t know why someone would hold an exhibition in the middle of the night, Sera said that the artist stated his reason for that on the website, but I don’t really care because I only go there just so that I could finally start my new life. It feels weird to say the word ‘new’ over and over again in my head because there is nothing new about life for me. Having to remember your last five lives makes you feel like there is nothing special anymore about the universe, unless I get to find him in this life but that would be impossible since the red star hasn’t shown its glow since that night where we made a promise under it. The truth is my heart started to believe that the red star, the one who will decide our fate whether we can meet again or not won’t appear again, but my heart also still holds a little hope. That hope is the only thing that keeps me looking up at the sky at least once every week, imagining there will be red light twinkling and you suddenly come to me with that smile of yours, your deep voice greets me as we both stand under it, but that never happens. Never in a hundred years of me living as a different person.

I grabbed my favorite long beige blazer and went to the exhibition at 11 PM. I was supposed to go there at 9 PM but my sister suddenly came to my house and asked me to babysit her baby as she had dinner with her husband’s colleague. That night was cold, I regret not wearing the red scarf that was hanging behind my room door. There weren’t many people as I got there, the exhibition was held on the sixth floor of a ten-story building across from Sera's office. I would describe the whole exhibition as unique; the artist didn’t display more than 10 paintings and most of them are painted in a black canvas. I went to see the paintings one by one until I reached what I consider as the winner among these paintings here. It was displayed right beside a window that was pointing straight at the empty sky. The painting feels familiar somehow, it was an abstract form of the view of a dark forest that was mostly surrounded by tall pine trees, the sky filled with light-yellow dots and one unique red object in the middle of it. I was so immersed that I didn’t realize an Asian man with natural sparkling brown eyes that looked like they held the whole galaxy in it was approaching me. He was wearing a black turtleneck with mustard wide leg pants. His black frame glasses were hanging on his sharp nose. He was standing beside me in front of the window, his face was as bright as the sun but his eyes would remind you of the stars. He greets me with a smile that shows his canines.

“What do you think of this painting? I noticed you have been standing here for a quite long time,” he asked.

“It’s beautiful, it somehow feels familiar to me,” I replied while looking at the painting.

“Familiar? How so?” he asked again, now sounding more curious.

“I feel like I’ve been there before, well maybe I did,” I said with a smile, then I realized that his face was all being serious about it. “Oh, I mean it’s not like I’ve really been there before. It’s just my memory that was playing tricks on me,” I said. That was one of the dumbest things to say. Yeah, talking about memories to someone whom we just met is not weird at all. It’s beyond that.

“Are you Arthur, the artist of these beautiful paintings?” I asked, trying to change the topic. He nodded at me as the answer, now his face softened.

I got distracted by the sky when I was about to look at the painting once again before going back home. I am now shifting my attention to the window beside the painting, and it made my body tremble just by seeing it. The red dot that once had lost its glow now appeared again in front of me, brighter than how I'd seen it a hundred years ago. Without me realizing it, tears were running down my cheeks. I got lost in that red star view until a voice that was saying a familiar word pulled me back to my consciousness.

“I named this painting Betelgeuse. It refers to the red object in the middle of it. I think of it as the star that connects us to the people we care the most, no matter what circumstance that we are in,” Arthur said to me while looking at the painting, he then looked at me with his beautiful smile after he finished his sentence.

The word that only existed between me and someone very dearly to me in my first life came out of his mouth. It’s him, the one that I have been waiting for. He is the half that my heart has been missing. He is the reason for every laugh and the tears that I shed throughout my six lives. I have been waiting for him like this red star that was waiting for 642.5 light years to finally reach the earth. Now he is here with a smile that I have been dying to see since my first life ended. Now we’re here just like how it was in my first life, looking at each other under this red star. Now I have finally found him in my sixth life. I have found him, my Betelgeuse.
