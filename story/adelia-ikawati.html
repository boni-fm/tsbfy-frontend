The Last Story She Told

Adelia Ikawati
Romance

"I have to finish my work." Says Oliver while getting up from a dining chair. It's a dinner night at the house of two Jade siblings.  However, Oliver—the elder one, seems not interested in finishing his meal.

"What's wrong with you? You've been skipping your meal lately." Asks Ivie, the younger sister who recognizes Oliver's weird expression which he terribly attempts to hide so it still can be spotted by her.

"Nothing, I just have loads of work to do. You can have my dinner." Replies Oliver who started to walk away, approaching his room.

"No!! I'll put it away and you can warm it if you want!" Says Ivie whose voice is raised so Oliver who walks away can listen to her.

Oliver is in his room now, he tries to continue his work—finishing a proposal that will be very important for his business. Actually, he doesn't need to finish it soon because he still has plenty of time before the business meeting starts next month. However, it's his mind that is busier lately, since he's contemplating something more interesting to him. He is recalling a young girl that used to be his dearest person back in his teenage days, Magnolia.

Magnolia was his only friend in school. They shared some similarities where they didn't have many friends, so they only had each other. Both of them also liked to read fantasy and adventure stories. Yet the difference was that Magnolia tended to love telling everything interesting to her—whether it was her own experience or about the story that had just been read by her. Oliver didn't like to tell stories, but he really loved when Magnolia told him a story. At that time, he would watch any expression of her which would change in each story, while the most unforgettable thing about her is her golden amber eyes which were really bright and alive.

Oliver remembered the time when Magnolia suddenly gave him a rose quartz necklace and said, "Do you love someone? If you love someone you can give her this."

Oliver did not understand at that time, but he pleasantly accepted and kept it in his pencil case, until someday, he realized that he loved Magnolia. Yet, what could he do? He was not that brave to express his feelings, he worried that it would destroy their friendship, or it could even make him lose her.

He then remembered a rose quartz necklace, a gift from Magnolia, and also her words when she gave it. So he thought that it would be the best way to express his feelings. But, alas, he didn't find it. There was no rose quartz necklace in his pencil case. The loss of that thing led him to feel guilty thus he never told Magnolia about it as well as his feelings.

The day of school graduation came. After the ceremony, Magnolia met Oliver in the school garden and they congratulated each other. It was the time when Magnolia told that her family would move to a faraway country and live in a solitary and lovely village there. For the first time, Oliver didn't like to listen to her story since she said that she would follow her family and continue her study in that country—which means they would be separated miles away.

"Why have you told me now, Meg?" Asked Oliver, whose eyebrows were frowned.

"I'm sorry." Replied her. "Actually… I just hope that you will give me back the necklace."

Her words were really hard for Oliver to process, so he asked again, "Give you back the necklace?"

Magnolia nodded. "Yeah, I like you and I hope you like me. I gave you the necklace and I hope you will give it back to me. So… Maybe I have a reason to not go with my family. But, no, it was a dumb idea. I have to be with them." She said it frankly with her golden eyes, which were really bright, focused on Oliver's. Yet, Oliver could also sense a kind of sorrowful sight from it. Her sight and words made Oliver's heart beat so fast, but he only could stare back at Magnolia's eyes.

At first, it was hard for words to get out of Oliver's mouth, so he abruptly hugged her tightly. When he finally got a nerve, he confessed that he liked her too but the rose quartz necklace was gone, so he felt guilty and couldn't find a way to confess his feelings. Between the hug, they were crying for a moment. Then, Magnolia said, "Thanks, Oliver. Please don't feel guilty, it's just a necklace."

Their conversation at that time ended after they promised to write a letter to each other every week. Oliver also promised that he would come to Magnolia at the right time someday and fetch her up to live together. They agreed then parted away.

The time changes, in Oliver's thirty-fifth, when he's contemplating his past with Magnolia. His jaw is clenched. It is all lovely to remember her, but it is also shameful because they stopped writing letters to each other ten years ago, and he also can't fulfill his promise to come to her. He takes a really deep breath and breathes it out roughly. The worst thing, he feels guilty since he remembers the fact that it was him and his ego that caused all of these problems. However, he thinks that he still can fix all of these and come to Magnolia. This idea comes again to him years ago, when he realized that he still misses and loves her truly, when he admitted how stupid he was. Lately, this idea is getting louder and bigger in his mind. Besides, he also thinks that now is the right time to fulfill his promise to her. Therefore, in the middle of the night, with all the necessary things that are packed in his old brown suitcase and with his black suit that looks well on his skinnier body, he slowly walks out of his room. Before he departs, he walks to the dining room when he realizes that he needs to eat something. After eating two slices of bread, he doesn't forget to pack some of it as well as some water. Then, he leaves a little note to inform Ivie that he will go to manage an urgent business for weeks and will be back very soon.

Seven hours later, Oliver is on a ship that is heading straight to a country where he wants to go. He is standing on the deck of the ship while staring at the vast blue sea. His mind flies again to the past when he had a letter every week from Magnolia and when he wrote back to her. It was his youth story which was full of happiness, where he could keep in touch with Magnolia who lived miles away, where he couldn't listen to her stories but still could read those every week through the letter, where he learnt that Magnolia continued her study at the college in a city that was pretty far from her family in a village. Yet those kinds of happiness were over when Olliver was twenty-second, the lowest time of him when he failed on his way to achieve his dream and when he lost his beloved parents. Hence his foolishness took over his mind. At this time, he felt he did not deserve Magnolia's love, so without telling her his condition or any obvious reason, he asked her to stop writing a letter to him and find another better person. However, Magnolia still sent him a letter every week, yet Oliver never wanted to read it since she was afraid to fall in love with her deeper. He let the letters from her pile up in his room while denying that he still loved her. It happened for three years until there was no letter from her again. Oliver himself admitted that it was his worst decision, he should’ve not done that, he obviously regrets it, hoping there is a time machine that can bring him back to the time where he still keeps in touch with the dearest Magnolia.

Instead of contemplating his past, he decided to go to Magnolia and fix the problem he made. This idea brings Oliver here, on the deck of the ship, who is determined to find his old love. In fact, he doesn't know exactly where Magnolia lives, but he just relies on the address of the last letter from her, which was sent ten years ago and has never been read by him. He doesn't know why, he is still afraid and somehow not ready to read each word in that letter.

Nearly a week later, in the early morning, the ship reaches its destination—the harbor of the faraway country. He thinks that he should give Magnolia something when they meet, so he buys a pretty huge bouquet for her. After that, Oliver looks for another transportation that could bring him to a village named Canopus. After eight hours on the train and then on the cart, he arrives. This village suits Magnolia's description years ago, a solitary and beautiful place filled with greenery. No wonder Magnolia and her family really love this place, even though it is pretty far from the city. There are not many houses in this village, so it makes Oliver’s spirit increase. It will make Magnolia’s house easily spotted, won't it?

However, the fact isn’t as easy as Oliver’s expectations. Here, some houses have no master. Other than that, the villagers who are in their houses don’t know who Magnolia Jasper is when Oliver asks them, as if the Jasper family has never lived in this place. The sun is getting sunken to the west, along with Oliver who is getting tired because he realizes that he hasn't eaten anything since he gets off from the ship. He then visits the only inn in that village that also has a bistro in it. There he orders a bottle of water and some gingerbread. He puts the bouquet hat he brings on the table. He feels really tired, if he can't meet Magnolia, he can't give her the flowers.

“It’s rare for a traveler or even a villager to visit my little place. But those who come here always look umm… pathetic.” Says a woman, who owns the inn, abruptly.

It makes Oliver’s sight turn to her, he then says, “I'm looking for a person who lives in this village, but no one knows her.”

The owner of this inn who looks busy preparing freshly baked gingerbread on the plate says, “You have to know that most villagers could not live here for a long time, maybe only for four or five years. This village is really beautiful but it is far away from the market center and hospital. Those who prefer a busy place or any place that is closer to the city will move out." She continues, "But for me, I prefer a place like this, a beautiful place where I can grow my food all by myself and run a farm as well as a small inn for both travelers and villagers.”

Oliver listens to her. He somehow sees a resemblance of Magnolia's personality in that woman since he thinks that Magnolia will say the same thing as her. Hence, he says, “But I think she's the person who really loves this place and has lived here for more than six years.”

The owner immediately turns to Oliver, putting a plate of gingerbread and a bottle of water on his table. “Who are you looking for?”

“Magnolia Jasper.” replies Oliver frankly, “I want to meet her.”. Then, the woman’s eyebrows frown, but her eyes make an expression that can’t be interpreted by him.

“Are you Oliver Jade?” the woman guesses. Oliver is startled because the woman in front of him knows his name, even his full name.

So he asks,  “How do you know?”.

“I was Magnolia’s good friend here. We have lived in this village together for a little long time. She always told me about you," The woman replies. "Ten years ago, when the ship that took her and her family to another place sank, the help came late to save them. They died and fortunately their bodies could be identified.” Continues the woman with sorrowful eyes.

The fact that is previously conveyed to Oliver is immediately stabbing his heart and scratching his mind. “Magnolia and her family died when they moved out? You’re lying, right?” Asks Oliver, but the woman has no words to reply and just shakes her head.

So, Oliver asks again, “How did it happen? Could you tell me?”

        “Have you read her last letter?” Asks the woman. Then Oliver shakes his head, feeling completely guilty and regretful since he never read Magnolia's letter, even the last one. “I want you to read it, and you can visit her grave in this village’s cemetery. It was in the west of this village, near the sunflowers field.”

There are no words except ‘thank you’ from Oliver’s mouth. He puts some money on his table, grabs the bouquet, and runs away from the inn, leaving his gingerbreads and water.

Several minutes later, he arrives at the cemetery. His breath is out of control while his eyes scan here and there to spot the grave that has the name Magnolia Jasper curved on it. Then he stops when he spots a grave of someone who has the name of Magnolia, along with the exact same date of birth. Yet, he senses a strange thing. Her surname is different, it changes into Magnolia Agate. Oliver is beyond perplexed, did he find the wrong grave? But the date of birth that is carved on the grave is exactly right. He also spots the other two graves on the left side which have the names of Magnolia’s parents, the Jasper family. Hence, Oliver is really sure that it is Magnolia’s, but why is it only her that has a different surname?

He is still confused until he realizes that there is another grave, on the right side of Magnolia’s, the grave of Oleander Agate. The four of them have the same date of death. Then Oliver just recognizes their death had happened approximately a week after Magnolia sent him the last letter. Startled, he dropped the bouquet of flowers on his hand to reach for the letter in his suit pocket. Hoping that he would be enlightened about what exactly happened to them, and especially about the Agates surname, he then opens it and starts to read every word on it.

While reading the letter, he learns that Magnolia worked as a teacher at the neighboring village after she finished her study. He learns that Magnolia emotionally suffered so much for three years since Oliver stopped writing for her. He learns that Magnolia needed him and really loved him until she met another person. It was all like what Oliver asked, Magnolia could meet another person, Oleander Agate—who was better for her and always be there for her until they married ten years ago. Then Oliver continues to read:

"I admitted that losing you is the hardest part of my life, but life gave me a chance to awake from the most horrible nightmare. I let you go, but you have to know that I'll never hate you. Now, I'm happy with Oleander. He's really nice and he knows everything about you. He's even with me now, accompanying me to write this for you.

Next week, two weeks after our marriage, I, Oleander, and also my parents will move to another country. We need to go there by ship. I hate to say this, but I think a letter from me every week will disturb you so much. I decide that I'll write to you another day—at the right and lovely time when we’re already moved in. So, I'm truly sorry to break our promise, Oliver.

I hope you read this letter, but importantly I hope you can always discover a million of happiness in your life, even when you’re in your lowest time. I don't know whether you have met your source of happiness or haven't. But, I'll always pray for you that hopefully, you’ll meet or always be with someone that you love and loves you dearly, someone that will make your world brighter. Hope you, your parents, and the sweet Ivie are always happy and in full health.

I'll always love you, my best friend, my dearest soulmate."

        –Love always, Magnolia

The tear flows down Oliver's cheeks. He drops to his knees when he feels his legs sore. His feelings in his heart can't easily be described by him since regret, sadness, relief, and love are all mingled into one. He feels grief and sorry because he let a precious person walk away from his life. He foolishly lost Magnolia and now he must accept the fact that he can't see her anymore and he can't listen to nor even read her stories anymore. He also felt sorry, since he never told Magnolia about his parents’ death. Causing her, in her last letter, to kindly hope for happiness and health for Oliver's parents without knowing that they already died. But on the other hand, there is another feeling of relief when he knows that Magnolia never hated him and could meet her beloved one, her happiness. That evening, Oliver's feelings are too overwhelming, until he is fast asleep, right near Magnolia's tomb.

Then, in the dark of the night, Oliver feels that someone's hand is shaking his shoulder slowly. He opens his eyes and sees a bearded old man. "Are you okay, Sir?" Asks the old man. Oliver, who starts to be aware of his surroundings, immediately gets up from his position, "Oh, I'm alright, Sir."

The old man offers Oliver help, but he refuses it politely, thanks to him for his offer, and says he will go in a moment. When the old man goes away, Oliver turns to Magnolia's grave in front of him. Then his eyes move to Oleander's and Magnolia's parents' graves until his eyes turn again to Magnolia's. He hardly breathes, then he touches Magnolia's grave, stroking it slowly. He then says, "I'm sorry, Meg. I came too late. And… I don't know if I can find another source of happiness or not. But I'm looking forward to it."

He feels the harsh and cold texture of the stone grave, and he says again, "I love you and I'll always miss you for my whole life. Thank you very much for everything."

After that, he stares at Magnolia's grave for a while. Now, the tears can't seem to come out of his eyes. So, he smiles and acknowledges the weird feelings that revolve around his heart, while hoping that Magnolia will see his smile from up there. He then realizes that he dropped the bouquet near his legs, so he picks it up. Even though the flowers are slightly shriveled, it still looks alright and their fragrance still can be smelt by him. He then thinks that he can give it to Magnolia now.

Yet, he also thinks of something. Aside from his feeling of gratitude for Magnolia, he realizes that he should also thank her parents as well as Oleander—the people who were always with Magnolia and made her happy. He picks some flowers from the bouquet, so he can also put them on the other graves of Magnolia's parents and husband. Lastly, he put the flowers which are still in the bouquet on Magnolia's.

After he finishes, he turns around and then walks away. That night, he decides to go back to the inn since now he really needs to eat something. He also plans to stay a night there before he leaves this place tomorrow morning.

A week later, Oliver arrives at his home in the evening. He is welcomed by Ivie who abruptly runs to him, hugging him tightly. "Where have you been?! How could you leave me alone with only a little note!" Cries Ivie.

Oliver is slightly surprised by her abrupt action, but he rubs his sister's back. "I'm sorry, Ivie. It's just an urgent business." Ivie doesn't reply and still hugs him. So, Oliver continues, "But, let's prepare and eat our dinner now. I think I'm hungry."

Ivie releases her hug, and asks, "God, what kind of business can make you want to join me for dinner?"

Oliver replies, "I won't skip the meals again, especially with you. I don't want to let go of anything good for me."

Ivie giggles right after she listens to Oliver's reply. She adds, "You say as if you've just lost something good from your life."

After hearing her words, Oliver just smiles while witnessing her sister walk away to the kitchen to prepare their dinner.
