The Innocent Yuan-Ti’s Lullaby

Esther Angelia Rahayu
Fantasy

Long ago, deep in the woods under the moonlight, a girl laid down on the grass, staring at the sparkling diamond up in the sky.

“Mother.” She called the woman beside her with a very soft voice. “I still feel cold. Should we find more wood to throw into the fire?” She asked her mother. The woman stared at the young girl, got close to her then pulled her into a warm embrace.

“Still feel cold?”

“Far better now, mother.” The young girl shook her head and snuggled to find comfort. A smile was carved on both of their faces. The mother caressed the hair of her daughter.

“Sleep deep my sweet little Josselyn. The cold of the night won’t touch you cause mother got you. The dark of the night will be banished by the moon's grace. Even a nightmare will not dare to corrupt a single of your sweet dreams. Don’t you ever worry because mother got you.”

Josselyn chuckled a bit with her eyes closed. She hugged her mother, feeling her body calm till she fell asleep on such a tranquil night along with her mother's lullaby.

“I won’t leave you alone in this cold world, Josselyn.” She whispered, then kissed her forehead, gently caressed the hair of her precious one, closed her eyes, and finally fell asleep.

–

The sun was already rising, and so were both of the Yuan-tis too. Half-Yuan-ti, to be more specific. Snake-like eyes and scales on some parts of their body. They were not human, even though they looked human. They covered their body with a cloak to disguise so that others, the normal humans, would not get scared by their appearances.

“Ready for the journey, snakelet?” Josselyn brought a pocket knife, nodding to show that she was ready. Her mother smiled, tilting her head in the direction of their heading.

“Go lead the way, would ya?”

A bit of hesitation came to the young one, but she wiped it out. Josselyn promised her mother to learn about the map so that, when she grows up, she will not depend on her mother to read a map. Her mother chuckled seeing her spawn’s eyes fixed on the map, trying hard to comprehend the symbols, tracks, and the mark.

“I-I’m trying my best, okay? Don’t make me nervous!” wrinkles on her forehead, and murmured each of the symbols’ meaning to find the track to the next stop.

“Gotcha! We go to the south, and then, after we arrive at Verde Lake. We… uh… go to the northeast of the lake?”

She looked up to her mother, stared as if she was asking her mother’s avowal. Her mother got close to her and looked at the map that she was holding, made her more nervous. Her mother tried to hold her laugh while seeing her spawn back and fixed her eyes on the map to make sure of the interpretation she told.

“What are you murmuring there? Casting a spell?” Her mother chuckled

“The map won’t fly to lead us, Snakelet! To the south, yes!”

“Really?! Yes! I did it! Uh-uhm..” she cut her excitement and faked her throat-clearing noises as she saw her mother about to make fun of her.

“I mean, yes, ma’am! We go to the south! Right this way!” her mother ruffled the spawn’s hair as she followed her steps back into their journey.

 –

It was approximately 5 hours since they left their temporary camp. They finally stepped foot at the first stop, The Veder Lake.

“Let’s rest our feet a bit? Shall we?” She approached her mother, who was already sitting under the big tree by the lake. She sat right beside her mother, brought the map to her, and asked several questions that she might have already asked before. The triangle symbol, the highland and lowland symbol, the wavy thing, the color of red, yellow, light green, dark green, and many more. Also, the mark that her mother made on the map.

“Will this thing bring us to father?”

Her mother shrugged. A smile and chuckle did not sound happy but not sad, just flat.

“Yes… of course! We’ll see him in that place that I marked.”

Josselyn smiled widely. She continued excitedly reading the map, trying to interpret the meaning for her mother.

–

After an hour rest, they continued their journey to the northeast of Veder Lake. The trip was smooth until three unknown people blocked their way. Quickly Josselyn hid the map inside her bag. One of them confronted Josselyn as she put the map in her bag.

“Oh, relax, kiddo! What do you have there? Why suddenly hide it?”

“Seems very… precious, huh?” The other two said, in turn.

Josselyn stepped back as the person approached her.

“We are not raiding you, don’t worry, kiddo. We won’t hurt-oh?” He didn't even finish his words yet as her mother pulled Josselyn close to her.

“We don’t have anything to trade. You guys are wasting your time.” Josselyn gripped her mother's cloak as her mother stepped in and confronted them.

“Relax, Ma’am. We won’t hurt both of you. But are you sure you don’t have anything to trade?” The one who confronted Josselyn said with a smirk. He seemed to be the leader of the other two. He glanced at Josselyn’s bag, then looked back to the other two, signaling them to surround the Yuan-tis.

“That’s just a map, towards our relative’s village. Nothing much, and nothing more.” she glanced at the three raiders in turns.

“Is that so?” his one eyebrow raised.

“This could be easy if you just hand it, kiddo.”

“No! I won’t lend the map to you nor a trade!” Josselyn yelled to them, startled her mother too, because she never heard her own daughter confront people before.

“This is precious, yes! and it’s ours!” she continued.

There is a bit of silence, and the three raiders burst into laughter.

“Well I suppose there is no other way now.”

“Duck, Josselyn!”

As her mother commanded her, Josselyn managed to dodge the sudden attack. Her mother kicked one of them on the stomach.

“Mother, behind you!” Her mother turned, smacked the other one who tried to stab her with a knife. The leader quickly approached Josselyn to get her bag. She hugged her bag and ran around the chaos, not letting the three of them take her bag.

After leaved bruises and knocked-out the other two, her mother pulled out a smoke bomb made by a handful of dust from the firewoods that was put in a patchwork, tied with a thread. She pulled Josselyn and threw the smoke bomb toward them. They ran away as fast as they could without knowing where they were heading.

Feeling the raiders did not follow them anymore, they rested under a big old tree near a village. The village seemed to be a peaceful place for them to rest, so they chose to camp outside. They opened their cloak and rid it of dust.

“The village must be nice, but I am afraid they’re not accepting us,” Josselyn observed the village from afar. There were children-well normal humans- played around, some adults came back from the farm as the sky turned orange, and some elderly sat in front of their house with their grandchild.

They seemed nice. That was what Josselyn thought.

“Let’s prepare our camp here.” Josselyn came to her senses and nodded. She observed her surroundings to find any sticks from the tree to make a campfire. Her mother stayed in the temporary campsite they built while preparing the dinner for them to eat.

–

“An adventurer? Ah, she shall be welcomed here! She shouldn’t camp there.”

“A monster. She is not an adventurer. Monster! We should tell the whole village! The monster built a camp here.”

–

A campfire was ready, and both of them sat near it. The sun already set, the moon started rising. Both of them shared their thoughts and feelings for what happened today. Josselyn tripped on her own cloak while she was busy reading the map, recalled the lame jokes her mother made, met the raiders, confronted them, and again managed to survive another day as a half yuan-ti.

“I can’t believe that you yelled at those raiders. Who taught you such behaviour?”

“Isn’t it obvious?” They both shared a laugh while munching their dinner.

Her mother smiled, giving her spawn a loving stare. “You must be happy to see Josselyn now. She is not that timid now.” Her mind seemed to speak to her husband, which only gods knows where. Both of them finished their dinner and prepared themselves for tomorrows’ journey. Read the map to track the tracks. They did not know the chaos that would come to them.

–

“Hide! Go!” She pushed her spawn to the nearer bush. The angry mass came to her with torches and some of them with weapons. “P-please, let me talk. I understand I shouldn’t be here. I’m-”

“Die monster!”

“You shall not step into our village!”

“Such an unpleasant creature!” The mass yelled, shouted to her.

“PLEASE HEAR ME OUT! I’M NOT GONNA HURT YOU ALL!” She shouted to the mass. It went silent for a sec. Their faces still look not happy nor convinced by her statement.

“I-I AM NOT A MONSTER!”

“Once a monster, Stay. A. Monster.” One of them confronted her and said it with emphasis on each word.

“We won’t be fooled again!”

A second after that, her mother got ambushed by the mass. Josselyn was shocked. She witnessed how brutal the mass was. She held her breath. Her body was trembling. Unfortunately when she stepped back, she accidentally stepped on a stick that made a crack. Thus, some of the villagers saw her and yelled at her.

“She got a spawn!”

“Chase her!”

Josselyn saw the helpless body of her mother, being kicked and stabbed by them. Their eyes met in the middle of the chaos.

“Run..." More or less, that was what Josselyn got by reading her mother’s lips.

“Don’t think you can get away, monster!” One of them said, and almost half of the mass turned to Josselyn. Panicked by the ambush that turning to her, she ran away as her mother said. While she was running, her eyes observed the woods, tried to find a way to escape from the mass.

"Gotcha!" She found a cave located between two different paths to hide inside. She curled herself hoping the mass had no idea to find her here. She holds her breath and cries. She tried not to make any sounds that could trigger the mass. Hurt all over her body. Her head felt dizzy, and her foot felt sore. Even she can hear how fast her heartbeat is. As she thought that the mass had already passed the cave, she slowly took a deep breath, breathed in, and breathed out slowly to calm herself.

–

In the middle of the night in the woods, the light remains from the moon only. She walked out of the cave, walked down slowly, sobbed, and hugged herself. Cold. She is confused, digesting what she just witnessed and what happened to her. Feels unreal.

She walked back to the campsite she was about to rest and where she witnessed the cruelty. There lay her mother. Helpless, bruised, and some of her skin burned. She grabbed her bag and some of the stuff from his mother’s bag.

She stared at the soulless body with sorrow. She caressed the hair of her mother, and a drop of tears fell from the young Yuan-tis eyes. It happened too quickly, and the fact that she couldn’t do anything to help made her feel guilty.

“The cold of the night won’t touch you cause mother got you. The dark of the night will be banished by the moon's grace. Even a nightmare will not dare to corrupt a single of your sweet dreams. Don’t you ever worry because mother got you.”
