November 14th

Shafira Dwi Azizah
Slice Of Life

Since they left seven months ago, a smile rarely appears on these lips, accompanied by quiet sobs.

I could say that I am a versatile girl since everything that I have done has always been adequate to the point that my surroundings always rely on me when they need to do anything. My days are always busy. Besides just being a third-year college student, I work as an audit staff member for a corporation to help my family financially. Regardless of how exhausted or busy my schedule is, work activities are always enjoyable because I can show my parents that they can rely on me whenever needed.

After getting used to all the schedules, now I can manage my time quite well between work and College. It was challenging since I did not have any experience studying while working. I even have had an academic burnout where I felt tired and anxious about the papers all the time. However, I tried my best and  grew accustomed to it; my grades continued to improve while my work performances kept getting better.  

Once I graduated from high school, I setted a goal in which I want to get higher education without adding any burden to my parents. With my goals, I always try my best and become an ambitious person in every work I get. I try to earn money by myself and rely on scholarship money for college needs. Thus I am so grateful that I was given such sustenance through a chance to enter College without paying the tuition fee myself. Once again, my parents were not workers who had a big salary; my father was a non-pensioner who received just severance pay at the time, and my mother, who was 57 at the time, worked at home as a tailor with a small income because people are looking for a well-educated tailor who has a good record in designing clothes. Thus it would become such a heavy burden if I ask them to pay my tuition fee.

When I returned 14, seven years ago to be exact, my father was retired from his job. It was the time I started to feel the real taste of life. Sometimes I feel sweet and sugary, while most of the time, I feel bitter. I could not afford what my friends could get from their parents. He got a pension fund but that was all the money we had, thus we tended to use it wisely as possible. My family utilized the money to launch businesses like a plastic shop, a sewing service for my mother, and even a motorcycle and vehicle wash service, hoping that the company would last for a long time. Unfortunately, led to Father's lack of financial ability; we were misled into committing $30 million to a friend of Father's, which he promised would be invested.

As time went by, we tried to forget the incident and had a happy life. My father got hired as a driver by a very productive store near our house. We were grateful even though the salary was not enough to support our lives. Yet he could not use all his energy since he was getting old. Every time he got home, he always looked pale and tired. Until seven months ago, suddenly my father felt sick, which was quite serious. We immediately took him to the neighbourhood hospital. He underwent several examinations and was declared ill with dengue fever.

Worse, my father's Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever or well known as DHF, was acute and was treated too late. I, who had been working overtime for two days at the office, regretted everything. I could only see Dad lying in his bed and often wincing in pain or unconscious, pale without energy. Mom kept crying; she felt even sorry because did not aware of how bad his condition was. She blamed herself for not realizing that Dad had DHF.

I could only pray that Dad would get better and Mom could stop blaming herself. She just cried beside Dad; she did not even want to go home; she just wanted to be beside him all the time.

November 14th was the worst day of my life. The world seemed to be collapsing. My mind was blurred.

I visited my parents at the hospital after returning home to get their clothes. I saw Dad was asleep, with Mom just looking at him. Her eyes were tired; her breathing was heavy. She even looked skinny.  

I didn't know if that day was the last time I saw them in this world. Even though it was sad, I was relieved a little since I could see the strong connection and love between them, which was very difficult to describe. Later on, the doctor informed me that my father had died at night. I burst into tears; I hugged my father, my dear father, who would start a new Journey without my mom and me.

My mom was silent. She sat on the floor and covered her face. I hugged her as tight as I could. My tears were still there, and I looked at Mom, who stayed still, then suddenly she fell. The doctor immediately took care of my mom, took her to the nearest bed and treated her. I was confused half to death. My mind started to make its conclusion. I said that it would be the end of my life if she left me alone. I prayed for the best that God would not leave me alone.

However, not all wishes would be granted. Doctors said my mom had a heart attack and was pronounced dead right away. I was speechless. I remember there were a lot of pity stares at me; the nurses, doctors, and everyone there. I could not even cry at that time; tears could not come out of these eyes. I just froze, silent, staring blurred at the pale legs of the person I loved the most.

Everyone came to our house. Uncle, Aunt, and cousins all came to cheer me up. They came with a lot of food or even took me for walks to various places. I went with them only because I didn't feel good about refusing. I joined not even half-heartedly, my heart and I were not there. I was silent, not even knowing where I was going, what I ate, what I drank, and who I met. If anyone asks me, what have I done, I only remember kissing the graves of the people I love. That's the only memory I have in my life.

Since seven months ago, I have been locking myself at home, just staying in my room, then daydreaming behind the house, remembering our daily lives in the past where my mom usually hung clothes. I left my job and didn't go to college. Yups, I am off College for all the reasons I lost my life goal. The reason I survived. I started to hate the world. I hate everyone, including myself. Why did they have to leave me at once?! Why, among many people, should I? Why did God choose me to have this fate? Why me ?.

It's been seven months. I have been living my life without hope. I lost all my dreams at a glance. I don't have the will to live anymore. I was not even aware of what food I ate, whether it was appropriately digested or not, or whether it was healthy. I could only think about the reason why God took my parents at a time. I regretted why my parents had to die when I had not given them happiness in return for taking care of me. I didn't cry at all; my tears had run out somewhere. I did not even think about the food that I would eat. My friend, Hani, was the one who always delivered her mom's cooking for me. My family suggested that I needed to go to a psychologist, but I refused. I just got angry for no reason. They were right, but I feel that I did not need to go that far. What I needed was just to be alone .

One afternoon after taking a shower, I heard someone knocking on my door. I looked ahead and found the figure of a teenage boy carrying a mortar in front and behind his tiny body. I, who had never paid attention to something for a long time, suddenly felt pity; he smiled at me while offering his wares.

"Miss, mortar, miss, It's cheap, Miss."

I was silent and still watching him.

"I have not sold any, Miss. I will give you the original price, without profit, Miss. So It would no longer be heavy,"said the little boy.

"How much?" I asked as I awoke from my daydream.

"$5  for a pair."

"How much is the profit?"

"$1, Miss."

"Oh God, only $1 for a pcs; he was willing to carry that much burden."

I invited him to sit down inside my garage and asked his name,

"You can sit down here!"

"Thank you," said him.

"What is your name?

"Adi"

"How old are you?" "You look so young."

"I am 15, Miss. Currently, I am studying as a 3rd grade Junior high school student."  

He said that he is an orphan who has the initiative to trade because he feels uncomfortable relying on orphanages and asking for money to cover his needs. I wondered where his parents were. Why did he stay in the orphanage?

His answer surprised me. He was a child, yet his mind was like an adult. The way he thinks was not like the children his age. He was an adult wrapped in a little boy. As he grew up, Adi started to feel that he needed to find out about his birth parents. He tried many ways and found out that he was a child out of wedlock. He was left at an orphanage because their economy was tough. His parents did not have enough money to raise him.  

Even Adi told all this without any hatred. He kept smiling at me while drinking the tea I had given him earlier. Adi then said he was trying to find out where his parents lived because, according to the story he heard, his parents got married when their economy started to improve. Why didn't his parents come back to take him and take care of him to avenge all their sins? Why is Adi even now still living in the orphanage? Why?

Adi says that his parents died after getting married. They died together two years ago when they were at home, and until now, it has not been explained what the cause of the death was. Maybe it was a heart attack, or someone said that they might be taken a suicide because of the economy, and so on. Adi told me that it was harder to know that his parents had died than to know that his parents had abandoned him. He said even though they were wrong for leaving him alone, they were the reason for his existence. Even though he had such a heavy burden to carry on, he was still happy to exist as Adi. He even blamed himself for not finding out about his parents earlier. However, the world still goes on. It could not stop only because of him.

"I have to keep fighting," said him.

"There are many kindnesses that I need to repay. Many people have supported me to become  a successful person."

He had to get up. That's why he always tried his best to support his own life. Adi said sadness should not cover the world. The world is not waiting for you; you have to chase it. His parents now only need prayers. Adi's job is only to be a good and pious child so that the reward will be abundant for his parents.

Once he disappeared from my sight, I entered the house and realized that what I had been doing was not right at all!.

"I am beetle headed!"  

"I am just wasting my time."

"Will mom and dad be happy to see me like this ?"  

There are still many people I need to help, and there are still many Adi who need hugs. I've had to get up. This tangled hair needs to be combed, this skinny body needs to be filled, and this sad face needs to be replaced.

I have to get through this. Why can't I do it if a small boy like Adi can? In reality, my parents provided me with numerous amenities, whereas Adi only met his mother once. Unlike Adi, who did not recognize his mother's smell, Mom and Dad made innumerable sacrifices to keep me alive. I felt sorry that I just did something stupid. Starting from now, I will try to be a nice person who does not give something up quickly. I'm glad you guys are up there together forever. I promise to always remember your sweetness, sincere love with a grin. Thank you for teaching me numerous life lessons. Hope we can meet in Jannah.
