Rain in The Night

Bintang Ramadhan
Horror

It was four in the afternoon. I went back home after a long and tiring day at school. Rode a motorcycle through the woods makes my mind a little bit calmer after this tiring day. I enjoyed this small and short moment for a while to the fullest. The breeze touched my skin, and it felt so nice. The sound of the motorcycle engine that is not too loud was pleasing to hear. The scenery consists of the tree as far as the eyes can see were mesmerizing. The best part was that I was alone, riding the motorcycle heading back home with no one to bother. While it was peaceful, I felt a little uneasy. A sudden chill ran down my spine for less than a second. I paid it no mind so it wouldn’t bother my peaceful time. I could see the end of that scenery. I knew that I was getting close to home, and I knew that this peaceful moment had come to an end.

I could see the house complex from the corner of my eyes as the scenery of the woods passed. My mind, which was at peace and chill, went back to having a distress due as I got closer to my house. Memories about this morning came back, bothered me again and again. The closer I got to my house, the more the memories replayed in my head, making me remember every detail about what happened this morning at my house. Passing the house complex gate, I rode slowly to my house while calming myself down, ensuring that everything would be all right and there was nothing to worry.

R through the complex made me realize that things were quieter than usual. Usually, you could see that there were a lot of people on the outside doing something, like kids playing, few people running, or people coming back home after a long day at work. There were not many people here, just a few of them. It was odd especially considering that it was in the afternoon now. Looking up at the sky, I realized that the sky was getting dark, not only because it was almost night but also because the clouds were gathering. The rain was coming. I headed back to my house and immediately set aside my thoughts about what was about to happen once I arrived.

I saw that the house was pretty quiet once I arrived. There were no cars to be seen, which made me conclude that my parents were currently away for now. I unlocked the gate and went inside, parking my motorcycle. I locked the gate again to ensure no one would get in, especially when it was almost night and rain was about to come. I took a look at my house again before going inside, and yeah, the house was pretty much empty. I looked at my phone to see if my mom left any messages for me about where she went. And I found it.

“We will have dinner with our father's boss. We’ll be home late. There is food in the fridge for your dinner. Take care.” It was her message. Pretty straight forward. It Made sense, considering what happened this morning. It must be pretty awkward for her to make a kind and nice message, so she went with a simple one. I put back my phone in my pocket, picked up a key, unlocked the door, and headed inside.

As I stepped inside the house, I saw how dark the house was. Not only the lamps were off, the light from outside was barely reaching the house due to the rain. I could barely see anything at all. It almost scared me. I shuted and locked the door. I went straight to turn on every lamp in the house. When did so, I noticed that there was one lamp near my parents bedroom that did not turn on at all, broken. We haven’t changed it at all since a few months for no reason at all. I also turned the lamp outside the house because it’ll be raining in a moment. All the lamps, except the one near my parents room were on, and the rain had started.

The house was pretty quiet. The only noise that I could hear came from the rain outside. I went straight to my room to put my stuff in it. I took my towel and went to the bathroom. It was a nice shower with warm water that made me relax after a long tiring day. It was nice, peaceful despite the rain outside, not too quiet and not too loud either. After a 15 minutes bath, I dried myself up and headed out of the bathroom. There the mood changed drastically from before. I could still hear the rain outside, and it was very loud, but inside my house, it was very quiet. This situation creeped me out, so I went to turn on the tv so that there would be some noise.

I put on some clothes after taking a bath. It was raining, and the temperature was cold, so I went with some long sleeve shirts and long pants. After getting dressed up, I went to the kitchen to grab something to eat for dinner. I remember that mom left something for me in the fridge. I opened the fridge only to find a pudding. It was kinda a letdown. How come a mother only left her son a pudding for dinner, in the middle of the rain? It pissed me off for some reason. But since there was nothing left to eat, I went along with it. I sat at the dining table, ate the pudding. The pudding was all right and nothing special. The atmosphere was rather quiet, even though it was raining outside, and I turned on the tv. Noisy but somehow still quiet. Sitting in the dining room reminds me of the fight I had with my parents this morning.

Sitting here makes me remember how it happened. How my father told me to get serious about my future, and how my mother worried about the people I was hanging out with. It happened for a while, like about a month, and we had this talk almost every day. Hearing it many times made me tired of this talk, this family, and this house, as a whole. It made me want to run away as far as possible. And today, this morning, I accidentally broke the plate when we had that discourse. It left my parents shocked, and I went to school immediately. Even though I wanted to run away, there was no place where I could run to.

After I finished my dinner, I went to wash my hands. Suddenly, the electricity went down. The house that was full of light and bright went dark, this house that was full of the sound of tv went quiet. The only sound I can hear is the sound of the rain from outside. I was shocked by the sudden power down that made me not move for a few seconds. I pulled myself together and cooled my thoughts. I went to look for a candle to give some light to this room. I lit it up using the lighter as soon as I found one. I light up about three candles, one near the entrance door, one for the living room, and another one is for my bedroom. The light from the candle is not too bright. There are many parts of the room that were still covered by the darkness. This darkness gave me creeps. Better to stay in my bedroom.

“Knock, Knock!”

I heard a knocking sound coming from outside the house. It was really loud and scared me. I braved myself to look over the window to see if there was anything outside making that sound. I looked over and saw nothing. I thought that maybe it was just a tree branch knocking on the window or the wall of my house, even though it was impossible to make such loud sounds.

“Knock, knock!”

 I heard it again when I walked to my bedroom. I tried to ignore it, but the knock got louder. So I chose to go back to the kitchen to see it, and still nothing to be seen from outside. I grabbed a knife to keep myself safe. When I turned around to the window, I saw a shadow figure outside the window. It was big, resembling a person, and I could tell that it was looking directly at me even though I couldn't see its eyes. I ran to my bedroom, locked myself in it, waited for the electricity to go on again and for my parents to come home.

The knocking noise lasted not too long and eventually went away after a while. I tried to look out the window in my bedroom and saw nothing different, it was still raining outside, and the shadow figure was nowhere to be seen. Not long after that, the power went on. I sighed in a sense of relief that I no longer have to be scared of the power down in the rain now.

“Thumb!” A sound coming from outside my bedroom sounds like a door being closed. It surprised me a little bit, but it seems that my parents are home. Weird, I did not hear the sound of the car at all. I went outside to check it out.

As I arrived outside my bedroom, I saw my parents were at the entrance. They were taking off their shoes and putting them on the shoe shelf. I wanted to say something like, 'welcome home' to them, but I decided not to for some reason. Instead, it was my mom who started the conversation,

“oh hey sweetheart, sorry we are home late, and have you eaten your dinner?” Said my mom. I nodded to her so that she knew I had eaten it. No words came from my father. He just took off his shoes, put down umbrella, and went on to the dining room. Mother also did the same thing. She went to the dining room as well. They were acting kind of weird, or maybe they were just hungry. After they left, I noticed something weird near the door. There was no sign of water dripping at all. It was raining outside, and this rain should have made their clothes wet even with an umbrella. Half of the umbrella was wet, and the shoes were completely dry. The ground you stepped on should have made your shoes wet, and theirs were not at all. Knowing things were getting weirder, I tried to look at the window near the entrance to see the car. I did not hear the sound of the gate and the car when they got home. I tried to get to the window slowly, not sure what I wanted to look at there. My heart was beating really fast. I was bathing in sweat due to fear and nervousness, and my hands were shaking as I got closer to the window.

“What are you doing there, Sweetheart?” I flinched after hearing a voice coming from behind me. I turned back to see that it was my mom. I was catching my breath for a while after that shock.

“Nothing,” I replied to her question.

“Oh, ok, can you come to the dining room? We need to talk, hurry! Your father is waiting for you,” she said. Her tone was serious. It was like demand and would not receive no as an answer. Her eyes stared at me, waiting for my answer. I nodded as my answer, and she headed to the dining room. Considering how serious they were about this talk, it was probably about what happened this morning. I should have known I could not escape so easily. I braced myself and went to the kitchen to finish this problem while completely forgetting what I was trying to do.

Quiet, waiting for a conversation to begin, the ramble, letting all out. As I entered the dining room, the situation was very quiet. Only my parents who sat at the dining table waiting for me, and there was nothing on the table, not even food. The only noise you could hear was the rain outside. I sat down and waited for them to start a conversation. For a while, there are no words that come from our mouths, just quiet with no one to start talking. Unable to handle the quiet, I started talking.

“Is this about what happened this morning?” I asked them, and they looked at me while thinking of a response, “as I said back then, I still don’t know what I want to do for my future. Please stop forcing me to do something that I haven’t decided yet. I’m tired of hearing you guys keep telling me to think about my future when I keep thinking about it. The friends I hung out with are also helping me. They are not bad guys. So please, don’t talk about something you guys don’t know about, and I'm sorry for the broken plate,” I continued. I let it all out about what I wanted to say. I hoped that it would make them understand. I looked at them to see their response, and their faces did not show angry expressions. It was a relief. Are they happy with my answer?

“We understand, son, and we are sorry for being angry this whole time,” said my dad. He apologized for the thing he had said to me.

“We are sorry for forcing you to do what we want,” said my mom. The apology they said made me happy to the point tears appeared in my eyes. After the long fight we had, maybe we could go back to how it used to be, a normal happy family.

“Would you forgive us after what we did to you?” my mother continued. I nodded without hesitation because I wanted nothing more than a happy family.

“Thank you, and I’m sorry as well,” I said to them.

They looked happy when I said that. Those words that came from my mouth were something that I always wanted to say to them, that I was happy to be their son, and I was glad they were my parents.

“Knock, Knock!”

I heard a familiar sound again. The room fell into sudden silence. My parents were looking in the direction where the entrance is. I was looking at it as well. That was the same noise I heard not too long ago.

“Sweetheart, can you go check on the door? There might be a guest,” said my mother.

A guest? In the middle of the rain? Were they serious about it? I nodded to them and went to the entrance to see it by myself. Probably because they were there, I was brave enough to check it. On the way to the entrance from the dining room, it was really quiet, but as soon as I got near it, I heard another sound. The sound itself sounds rough. It was a car engine sound. And another thing I heard was the voice of a woman.

“Sweetheart, we are home! Can you open the door please?”

That was the thing I heard. It was my mom's voice. It was her voice coming from outside. I checked the window near the entrance to see it, and I saw her with an umbrella waiting for the door to open. Another thing I saw was my parents' car with my father in it. From the look of it, it was like they just arrived home. But how could that be? They just arrived back then. How come there was another of my parents? How was this even possible? A realization hit me of what happened. The one that came first was never my parents. The water drip, and dry shoes. I then realized it. I was sweating like crazy, and my body was shaking to the truth I just discovered. The one that I talked to was not my parents. They were-

“Sweetheart, what are you doing here?” A hand touched my shoulder. At that moment, I understood something. That thing was just too good to be true.
