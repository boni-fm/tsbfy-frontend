Next Time

Sawitri Aulia Khaerani
Slice of Life

The man looked down from the edge of the pier he was standing at. His reflection stared back at him from the still murky water below. The dark, dirty color of the sea showed nothing of the inside.

𝘐 𝘸𝘰𝘯𝘥𝘦𝘳 𝘐𝘧 𝘐 𝘤𝘰𝘶𝘭𝘥 𝘵𝘰𝘶𝘤𝘩 𝘵𝘩𝘦 𝘣𝘰𝘵𝘵𝘰𝘮 𝘢𝘯𝘥 𝘯𝘦𝘷𝘦𝘳 𝘤𝘰𝘮𝘦 𝘶𝘱...  

He leaned closer to the edge, eyes never leaving the pair of his reflection down on the sea surface.

𝘞𝘰𝘶𝘭𝘥 𝘐𝘵 𝘩𝘶𝘳𝘵? 𝘖𝘳 𝘸𝘰𝘶𝘭𝘥 𝘪𝘵 𝘣𝘦 𝘭𝘪𝘬𝘦 𝘴𝘭𝘦𝘦𝘱𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘣𝘶𝘵 𝘮𝘪𝘯𝘶𝘴 𝘣𝘳𝘦𝘢𝘵𝘩𝘪𝘯𝘨? 𝘚𝘩𝘰𝘶𝘭𝘥 𝘐 𝘤𝘭𝘰𝘴𝘦 𝘮𝘺 𝘦𝘺𝘦𝘴?  

The man was about to close his eyes when choked sobs and sniffs pulled him out of his trance. He looked to the side, spotting a young girl standing a few feet away with both hands on the railings. Her knuckles were white with how strong she held onto the mildew handrails and shoulders shaking violently beneath the thick coats she wore.

"Uhh... Hey!" The girl snapped her attention to him in an instant. Her dark eyes brimmed with tears that steadily trailed down pale cheeks past a pair of lips that twisted into a sorrowful grimace. A million emotions flashed through her glassy orbs. Distraught, remorse, guilt, hurt and sadness.

𝘚𝘰 𝘮𝘶𝘤𝘩 𝘴𝘢𝘥𝘯𝘦𝘴𝘴.  

"Wanna talk about it?" He proposed gently, trying to appear as harmless as possible. The girl just stared at him in confusion and distrust. Well, not every day a stranger was willing to hear your story, let alone the one asked you to tell them. Also, there was that stranger danger rule which parents and teachers like to remind the youngling any chance they get.

"How about we go to the stall over there? Sounds good?" As if reading her mind, the guy pointed at the nearby food stall that was packed with townsfolk. A place where she could easily call for help if he ever attempted to harm her. Not that he would anyway. "I won't force you to, but it's better to let it all out than bottling up. Don't you think so?"

The girl looked at the small but homey stall, seemingly contemplating her choice, then faced the man again. Her round head timidly nodded as she tucked a strand of curly dark hair behind her ear.

"Then, let's go," They made their way over. Weaving among the crowds of scary-looking sailors and grubby kids running around the dock. A few moments later, they found themselves sitting on empty barrels just beside the food stalls, a cup of hot chocolate warming their hands from the cold, harsh sea breeze.

"Whenever you are ready," The young girl opened and closed her chapped lips a few times, still hesitant to tell her story. But after a few moments of silence, she made up her mind and started talking.

She talked about herself and her family and how she was constantly compared to her siblings. How she was never good enough for her parents no matter what she did. Her childlike voice grew shakier as her story went on, and so did her pale slender hands.

"-It was so unfair. You know? I had worked hard to achieve anything I could to make them proud, but I'm not my siblings... I'm not a genius like my brother or good at household matters like my sister. A-and for that, I wasn't enough." Her gaze found him in a desperate way of asking for him to understand. "I was so depressed. I don't know what to do," She averted her eyes downward, staring mournfully at her pair of worn-out boots.

"Maybe it was better for me to go,"

'𝘈𝘩, 𝘢𝘯𝘰𝘵𝘩𝘦𝘳 𝘭𝘰𝘴𝘵 𝘴𝘰𝘶𝘭 𝘪𝘯 𝘢 𝘣𝘳𝘪𝘯𝘬 𝘰𝘧 𝘥𝘦𝘴𝘵𝘳𝘶𝘤𝘵𝘪𝘰𝘯.'  

"You won't. You will never be enough," He said, gaze falling to the horizon far in the ocean. The girl stared at him in stunned silence. My jaw slacked with a hurt look in her eyes.

"If you try to be your siblings. Then forever you won't be enough for your parents. Just be yourself and do what you think is right," He looked down at the girl, a faint smile on his thin lips. "You can't please everybody, and it's fine. As long as you stay true to yourself, the people that accept you with open arms are the only ones that are worth your time,"

She scrunched up her nose in confusion. "I don't get it.”

"What I meant was," He took a sip of his drink. "You should talk to your parents, get them to understand that you aren't your siblings and that you too have your achievement that they should at least acknowledge too,"

"And if they won't budge?" He shrugged.

"Then you just have to hang in there until you are old enough to fend for yourself and move out, I guess," The girl pouted, looking dejected as she lowered her head down to her chest. "Listen," She looked up slowly.

"If that's not enough to motivate you to keep going, how about keeping small goals? Do you have someone you want to meet?"

"Well... I have a friend who lives across the city...."

"Then, keep hanging on until you meet them. Or you can have any other small goals worth looking forward to. There must be something in this world that you haven't tried yet. Make that your motivation to keep going," He smiled when watching the sadness leaving her dark eyes bit by bit. "It would be such a waste to lose another brave soul to the darkness. Don't let them drag you down. You are worth so much more than what they told you,"

The girl averted her gaze shyly to the side, and a small smile bloomed on her pale lips. "... I'm not brave,"

"You've told me your story, and that alone was enough proof,"

"Okay then," She stood up, a new determination shining in her now clear eyes. The man followed in suit. "I guess I better get going now. Thank you so much..."

"Ah, just call me Gege,"

"Then thank you so much, Gege. You've saved my life," She tilted her head to the side. "Will you be here tomorrow?"

"Well... I'm here every evening. Unless something came up,"

"Great!" Her lips stretched into a wide grin, showing a set of cute bunny teeth. "I'll see you tomorrow then,"

With that, she left him on the now less busy dock. Her head of long, curly black hair swished along as she walked with a little skip in her step. Seemingly happier and more relaxed compared to the girl she was half an hour ago.

The man watched until her small back vanished among the crowds. Felt something in his chest lighten after helping another soul to their way of happiness. Well, at least he hopes so. He walked to the edge of the dock. Peering at his reflection on the dark surface of the sea. But instead of the thoughts he was having earlier, there's nothing left. Just a faint buzz of warmth filled up his supposedly empty heart. He sighed, then turned away from the edge.
