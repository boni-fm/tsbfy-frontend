The Horned Goat and The Serpent-bearer

Fariel Rizky Dermawansah
Fantasy

“Ah! I see the North Star!” The cheerful boy who bears a devilish appearance shouted. He immediately ran to the mouth of the cave and pointed his right index finger to the brightest star in the sky. “This means we are going in the right direction, Ophir! I think we are going to find a village tomorrow!” The boy continued as he looked happily at his interlocutor.

        

“Y-yes. I hope so,” answered the boy who bears a resemblance to a bird. “Please sit down, Bel. It’s not warm over there,” he said in a concerned tone.

        

The cheerful boy laughed as he approached his worried friend. He sat across from him, near the campfire he previously cast. In the light of the flame, the devilish features of the boy were becoming apparent. His skin was uncommonly pale. There were no pupils in his eyes. Only a solid color of crimson was visible. His nails and teeth are razor-sharp like a carnivorous beast. He had a tail that further alienated him from a regular human and a striking pair of goat-like horns on his head that established his appearance as a demon.

It was no surprise that he never liked his appearance, as it definitely would strike fear in those around him. As the kin to the dreadful fiend, his kind was cursed with the power of the abyss. They were the symbol of blasphemy and destruction in the eye of the masses; they were the minorities of this world, often being ostracized or even killed by the hands of the holy believer. The boy knew this as he experienced the exact treatment. His family members were purged, leaving him alone in this unjust world. He covered most of his body with clothes, coiled his tail around his right leg, and desperately his horns with a hood to avoid discrimination by the local villagers.

At that moment, however, he was smiling. He finally had something that he always wished upon the stars. He found his one and only friend, someone who could drive his fear away.

His name was Ophir. He’s the boy in front of him, a demihuman with the features of Aves. Unlike the devilish kind, his race, often called Aarakocra, was a prominent kind in this region. They were the ones who conquered the sky with their wings. They were the ones who could easily traverse this mountainous region and made the most powerful leader in this region. The boy resembled a golden eagle except for his timid personality. His body was also abnormally small for a bird of prey, and he was still unable to use his wings to soar in the broad sky.

While he wrapped his body with his wings, Ophir looked at the two fish they cooked inside the campfire.

“I think the fish are ready, Bel.”

        

“All right!” He quickly grabbed the fish on a stick and ate it. Ophir waited patiently for his dinner to cool off.

        

“These are the last two fish that we have. We don’t have any more food, Bel.” Ophir said anxiously.

        

“Don’t worry. I’m sure we will find a village tomorrow. My hunch is always right, remember,” said Bel as he immediately choked on his food. “I-I’m sure we’ll find an abandoned house where we could temporarily stay and find a local market to steal.”

        

After hearing what Bel said, Ophir looked down as he still showed his anxiousness.

“Are we going to do this forever, Bel? What if we get caught by the local villagers, or even worse, the local gang? They are not going to spare us for what we have done.”

        

Bel turned down his smile, pondering on Ophir’s fear. “I know…it’s been a while since we escaped that internment camp in Kigal. Since then, we steal the food from every village we’ve found, moving from one to another if we almost get caught.” Bel stopped for a while.

“And in the last village...I’ve realized that I only dragged you down, Bel,” said Ophir as tears started to well up in his eyes. “If I wasn’t such a burden, we could stay in that village longer. If only… I could fly…”

Looking at dejected Ophir, Bel knew that he needed to think of something. Ever since he fell and almost got caught in the last village, Ophir had felt guilty and thought of himself as a burden to Bel. Now they need to find a new source of food. Since the wildlife of this region is too unforgiving for the two boys, the only option is to find a village where they could steal.

After a while, Bel shook his head and started to regain his smile. “Don’t cry, Ophir. It’s not your fault. I’m sure we’ll be able to survive in the next village. Besides, now I know what our main goal is going to be.”

Ophir tried to stop the tears by wiping them off. “What is it?”

        

“The capital city, Elam! My grandfather once said that it’s a place where everyone has a chance to live and get a job. My grandfather said it, so it must be true! He said that even my kind could find a place there, as long as you could contribute your skill to the city.” said Bel exhilaratingly as he began to stand up. “I’m sure we could join the capital gang with our skill as a thief. With a bit more practice, we could be the best thief duo in all of the highlands! We could have our nickname. You could be… Ophiuchus, just like the giant constellation of a man holding a snake in the sky! And I could be… hmm… Ah! Capricornus, just like my horns! Ain’t that awesome!”

        

Bel’s optimism quickly cheered Ophir. “Ophiuchus! I can’t wait for that, Bel!”

        

“Hahaha, I know you are going to be excited, Ophir! Grandfather said that the capital city was in the northernmost mountain region of the highlands. Then, we follow the Northern Star, and we are good to go!”

Both of them finished their dinner in a happy mood. Bel’s plan sparked new hope for the two of them. It promised a new life, a way better life, compared to their current state of living. In their far away goal, the nearest destination was to find a nearby village for them to find food. For now, both of them were resting, waiting for the daylight.

The two woke up as the sun began to rise. They felt energized after the rest and found a new goal. After Bel cast off the campfire, they embarked on their journey to find a new village. Heading up north, they tried their best to climb the mountainous region, pass through the taiga, and hide from the local beasts as the wandering adventurers. After spending half a day roaming across the nature of the highlands, they finally found a village.

It’s the village of Anshan, located at the foot of the mountain with the same name. It’s a rather lively village, with thousands estimated to live in this settlement. As they entered the village gate, Bel and Ophir immediately searched for their temporary hideout. Luckily, they found a dilapidated house at the edge of the village.

“What luck! We find ourselves a good hideout!” said Bel happily. “With the location near the cliff edge, I’m sure no one will come to this house except us!”

“Yeah. I hope so,” said Ophir. “Then shall we find the market, Bel?”

“Yeah!” Bel paused for a while as he immediately thought of something.

“What’s wrong, Bel?”

“Listen, Ophir. Whatever might happen later, remember to come back to this place. If something bad happens, leave me be and run to this place, okay? This place is pretty secluded, and I’m sure you will lose those who are chasing you.” Hearing this, Ophir was dazed.

“Wh-what are you talking about, Bel!? I will never leave you! I want us to come back to this house together!” The boy said in a shaky voice and with teary eyes. Looking at Ophir, Bel instantly regretted the things he said before. He then smiled and grabbed Ophir shoulder.

“Hahaha, you’re right! We are going back to this place together! Now come on! Let's go to the market!”

“Yeah!” Ophir shouted while he regained his smile.

The two then went off to the village market. It was in the square, packed with many people as the sun was on the brink of sinking. As far as eyes could see, there were stalls everywhere, selling everything from clothes, food, and equipment, to even spell scrolls.

After looking around, Bel and Ophir fixed their eyes on the local bakery stall. Before they looted the place, they knew that they needed a diversion to keep the people’s attention away from them. In order to accomplish that, Bel casted a fire to the nearby tree. As the fire quickly spread to the tree, many tried to extinguish the fire as they feared the fire would spread to the stalls. Both Bel and Ophir hurriedly grabbed every piece of bread they could carry and ran away from the crowd.

They ran like the wind, slowly feeling confident that they succeeded.

“Looks like we are going to eat tonight, Ophir!” said Bel as he ran. “Now let’s go to the house and—” Bel’s utterance was interrupted as he bumped into someone.

“All you alright, Bel!”

“Yeah, I guess—”

“Ah! It’s a demon! Somebody help!”

The scream quickly alarmed Bel. He immediately noticed that his hood was removed as he collided. Now, his horns are showing to a fearful villager who is trying to get some help from the other villagers.

The scream quickly alarmed Bel. He immediately noticed that his hood has removed as he collided. His horns were shown to the fearful villagers who were trying to get some help from the other villagers.

The scream spread like wildfire as more people looked at the boy with a devilish appearance. “A demon! He must be the one who caused this fire! Kill him!” screamed one of the villagers.

“Ophir, run.”

“But, Bel—”

“RUN NOW!” Screamed Bel as he cast a flame into his right hand and threw it into the gazing crowd.

The crowd screamed in terror as the fire burned a couple of villagers. “Ahh! The demon is attacking! Get the guards now!” cried one of the villagers.

He looked at this scene, realized that his friend was in grave danger. He knew that Bel was going to be killed, yet he couldn’t do anything. Bel’s scream signaled his previous advice: “Just leave me be and run to the hideout.”

So Ophir ran. He held back his tears and kept running to their hideout. As he ran away from the market, a couple of paladins showed up in front of Bel.

“Akin to the blasphemous fiend, prepare to meet your end!” said one of the paladins as he drew his sword.

Bel looked behind him and then smiled. He knew Ophir had already run away, just like what he wanted. Now he could close his eyes and recall his short yet joyful moments as the paladins were approaching.

“Take care, Ophir, my one and only friend.”
